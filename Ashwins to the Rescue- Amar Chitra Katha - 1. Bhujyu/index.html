    Bhujyu

    Ships laden with riches were sailing back to Sindhu, the land of Raja Tugra. The merchants on board were quite pleased with themselves.

    "We have done better on this voyage than we did ever before," one boasted. "Or can hope to do in the future."

    "Patience, my friend," another advised. "Since we are taking the route through the strait, we should get home faster than usual."

    "Our Raja will be pleased with the money the royal treasury will get as its share. I am impatient to land," a third added.

    Meanwhile, at a hideout on an island by the strait, a group of islanders lay in wait.

    "How many do you see?" the chief asked his lookout.

    "Seven."

    "Are our ships in position?"

    "Yes. And our men are ready."

    "Let them come nearer... all seven of them!" the chief smirked. "So am I. Come on. Now attack!"

    The islanders ambushed the merchants. "Take everything but spare our lives," the merchants pleaded. The islanders ruthlessly transferred all the cargo to their own ships.

    A few days later at the court of Raja Tugra, the merchants reported their loss. "...And as we entered the strait... we were ambushed by some islanders and robbed of all our cargo."

    "These islanders have become a menace," a minister noted.

    "Unless they are subdued no trade is possible with the outside world, Maharaj," a merchant pleaded.

    "As your King it is my duty to make the seas safe for you. And I shall do it," Raja Tugra declared.

    Later, Tugra spoke to his son, Prince Bhujyu. "Bhujyu, I am going to lead an expedition to the island."

    "You? No father. As your son, that should be my privilege," Bhujyu insisted.

    "But you are young, inexperienced and unacquainted with the hazards of the sea, my son."

    "All the more reason why I should go, father. It would be a good beginning. How else will I gain experience?"

    The Queen, Bhujyu's mother, was worried. "Oh, no, my dear child! The island chief is wicked... wily... treacherous..."

    "I am the son of a king... born to destroy the wicked and protect the virtuous," Bhujyu said proudly.

    "You make me happy and you make me proud, my son," the Raja smiled.

    Bhujyu knelt before his mother. "Mother, send me on my first mission with your blessings."

    "For a moment, I forgot my dharma. Forgive me, Bhujyu," she said, placing her hands on his head. "Live long, my son! May you be crowned with success."

    The call was given, and Bhujyu's men assembled before him. "We are with you, Bhujyu!" they cheered.

    Before setting sail, Bhujyu made offerings to various deities. "Let us pray to Pushan to show us the right path," he said, offering oblations to the fire. "This is for Pushan, the path-finder. And this for Indra. And this for the Ashvin twins! And the Sun-god."

    The fleet set sail to the cheers of "Long live Sapta-Sindhu!"

    After a voyage of three days and three nights, they spotted birds. "Birds! We must be nearing the island!"

    Bhujyu blew a mighty conch, and his soldiers emitted fierce war cries. Just then, a veteran sailor of a hundred voyages came running to Bhujyu. "I fear we are in for a storm. Prince Bhujyu! A storm!"

    The very next moment, a massive tempest broke out. "Help!" a sailor cried as he was thrown overboard. Even as Bhujyu tried to pull the man up, a huge wave hit the ship. Most of the ships sank and many of the men were drowned.

    "Did you hear that cry?" a sailor on a surviving boat asked.

    "One of our men calling out for help."

    "Shall we turn the boat?"

    "No! Let us get away from this accursed place." They ignored the cries for help and sailed away.

    Bhujyu, however, was alive. He came up gasping for air and, with great effort, swam on. Suddenly he saw a vessel in the distance. "A ship! One of our ships! Help! Help!" he shouted.

    But the men on the boat saw him and said, "Keep going."

    The storm at last subsided. Bhujyu was all alone, afloat on the high seas. "No... I can't keep it up... any longer," he thought exhaustedly. "O Ashvins, come to my rescue!"

    Just then, a log of wood bumped into him. "A log of wood! Praise be to your name, O Ashvins." Bhujyu slumped onto the log of wood and closed his eyes.

    For some time Bhujyu drifted on, clinging to the log. When he opened his eyes, he wondered, "Where am I?" Suddenly he saw a dorsal fin cutting through the water. "A killer fish! I have neither the strength... nor the weapons..." Then he realized he still held the log. "Oh! I do! I do have a weapon!"

    As the shark lunged at him, Bhujyu yelled, "Come! Come my friend!" and jammed the log hard into its open jaws. "Sink your teeth into it. Sink them hard." The shark, unable to close its mouth, thrashed away.

    Overcome by thirst, Bhujyu took a mouthful of salt water, only to spit it out. "Oh, for some water! O Ashvins, please help me! Don't let me die a futile death. O Ashvins, if I must die, let it be on a battlefield... with arrows piercing my heart... not of thirst in this watery grave. Save Bhujyu who worships you. O Ashvins, you saved Antaka, Vandana, Rebha, Deerghatamas and many others."

    As he lay semi-conscious, the Ashvins, the mighty twin heroes and helpers of all, appeared in a celestial boat and pulled him out of the water. When Bhujyu at last opened his eyes, he saw them. "You must be the Ashvins, the mighty heroes, helpers of all. Accept my salutations!"

    Then the Ashvins gave Bhujyu some honey, milk, and soma to restore his strength. After three days and three nights, their boats safely reached the shores of the Sindhu land. As soon as they heard of the arrival of the boats, Tugra and his queen rushed there.

    "Bhujyu! Oh, Bhujyu! My son!" his mother wept.

    "O mother, I am safe! Alive!"

    By the time the reunited family looked up to thank their saviors, the boats with the twins in them had receded into the horizon. "O Ashvins, ever-vigilant helpers of mankind," Raja Tugra prayed. "You should have waited for an offering of soma and honey!"

    Later, Bhujyu approached his father. "Father, permit me to resume the campaign against the island chief."

    "Are you not afraid of the sea?" Tugra asked in surprise.

    "No, father. On the contrary I am more confident. With the Ashvins on my side, I have nothing to fear."

    "I will not stop you, son. You have my blessings."

    A few weeks later, on the island hideout, a lookout shouted, "Ships from the land of Tugra have been sighted."

    "It is very kind of him to send us a fresh supply of riches so soon!" the pirate chief laughed. "Mount an attack at the usual spot."

    As the ships approached, the pirates yelled, "Here they come! Attack!"

    But this time the islanders were in for a surprise. "Welcome!" Bhujyu shouted, drawing his sword. Bhujyu's well-prepared men easily overpowered the islanders, recovered all the stolen riches, and returned home in triumph.

    "The seas are now safe for the traders of Tugra," Raja Tugra announced proudly. "In performing your dharma, you have helped me perform mine. I am proud of you, my son."
