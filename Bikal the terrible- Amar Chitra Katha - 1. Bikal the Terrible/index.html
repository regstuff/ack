    Bikal the Terrible

    In a quiet village bordering the dense forests of Madhya Pradesh, a wild tiger frequently prowled near human settlements at night, searching for easy prey.

    One dark night, as the tiger lurked outside a wealthy merchant's home, he overheard the merchant scolding his son near an open window. "Are you not afraid of Bikal?" the father warned sternly. "With Bikal around, even the bravest of men think twice before venturing out into the night!"

    In the local forest dialect, Bikal referred to any terrifying unknown danger. But the tiger, having never heard the word before, jumped to a terrifying conclusion. Who is this Bikal? the beast pondered nervously, imagining a gargantuan, multi-limbed monster far fiercer than any lion or tiger. Panic seized the predator. I had better hide somewhere for the night!

    Spotting a dark cattle shed nearby, the tiger slipped inside and curled up in a corner, trembling at the thought of the monstrous Bikal.

    Later that night, three thieves broke into the merchant's house and carried off two massive sacks filled with gold and valuables. Finding the load too heavy to carry on their shoulders, they searched for a transport animal.

    "It's pitch-dark in that shed," one thief whispered.

    "All the better for us," replied another.

    Stepping inside, the thieves stumbled upon the sleeping tiger in the dark. Mistaking the striped beast for a sturdy bullock, they heaved their heavy sacks onto its back, tying them securely with thick ropes and seizing the tiger by its tail.

    The tiger awoke from a nightmare of Bikal right as a thief yanked its tail. My worst fears have come true! the tiger panicked. I am in the clutches of the terrible Bikal and his henchmen! No ordinary man would dare pull my tail or load me down like a beast of burden!

    Paralyzed by fear of Bikal, the mighty predator meekly allowed the three thieves to lead it by its tail out of the village. As they walked through the night, the thieves chatted merrily about buying goats, acquiring land, and getting married with their loot, while the tiger marched in silent terror under the heavy sacks.

    At dawn, near the edge of a forest, the light broke. "Let's divide the loot now," one thief called out.

    As they turned to unbind the sacks, the thieves finally saw their 'bullock' in the morning light. "F-friends..." one gasped, pointing with a trembling finger. "It's a... IT'S A TIGER!"

    Screaming in horror, the three thieves abandoned the loot and fled through the forest as fast as their legs could carry them.

    Standing alone, the tiger stared after the fleeing men. Oh, what a fool I have made of myself! the beast realized, flushed with embarrassment. They were just ordinary men! If the other forest animals hear of this, everyone will laugh at me!

    Anxious to shed the embarrassing load, the tiger thrashed about, but the heavy sack wedged itself tightly between two large boulders, trapping the tiger in place.

    Shortly after, a young cowherd named Shambhu walked down the forest trail. Seeing the trapped predator, Shambhu turned to run, but the tiger called out imploringly, "O Cowherd! Don't run away! I won't harm you. Somebody tied this heavy sack of money to my back, and it's stuck between these rocks. If you help me untie it, you can take all the gold inside!"

    "You promise not to hurt me?" Shambhu asked cautiously.

    "I promise," the tiger swore.

    Shambhu unknotted the ropes, freed the tiger, and collected the sack of gold coins. Curious, Shambhu asked, "How did a tiger end up tied to a sack of loot?"

    "It's all because of that Bikal!" the tiger grumbled testily. "Never utter that name again, do you hear? I hate the sound of it!"

    "I-I'll never utter it again," Shambhu assured him, heaving the sack onto his shoulder and rushing home.

    Overnight, Shambhu became a wealthy man. He bought cows and buffaloes, built a fine house, and married Bhagwati, the most intelligent and courageous girl in the village.

    Months passed in prosperity. One evening, as Shambhu and Bhagwati sat on their porch, Bhagwati asked, "Shambhu, how did you suddenly become so rich?"

    "It's a long story, Bhago," Shambhu smiled, relaxing. "It all started with this Bikal..."

    Unbeknownst to them, the tiger happened to be strolling past their house at that very moment. Hearing the forbidden word echo from the porch, the tiger's eyes flashed with anger. He broke his promise! He uttered the forbidden word! I won't spare him now!

    Late that night, while the couple slept soundly, the tiger crept into the house, lifted the sleeping Shambhu off his cot, and carried him away into the deep forest.

    Shambhu woke up with a thud as the tiger dropped him onto the forest floor. "Ow! Where am I?" Shambhu gasped, looking up into the glaring eyes of the beast.

    "You broke your promise," the tiger snarled. "So now I am going to eat you!"

    Thinking quickly to save his life, Shambhu replied calmly, "Eat me by all means, but wait until morning."

    "Why not now?" the tiger demanded.

    "My flesh is cold and tough right now," Shambhu explained smoothly. "Under the morning sun, it will become tender and warm. To me it makes no difference whether you eat me now or later, but for you it will be a far tastier meal."

    The tiger pondered. "All right, we'll wait until sunrise."

    Meanwhile, back at the village, Bhagwati awoke and found her husband missing. Spotting tiger tracks near the cot, she realized what had happened. A tiger has carried him away! I must act quickly!

    Grabbing a brass gong, a wooden cudgel, and a metal pot, Bhagwati marched fearlessly into the dark forest.

    As the tiger waited for sunrise, a strange, terrifying racket echoed through the trees: THAK-CHHANNAN-CHHANNAN-DHAM! CHOOM-CHOOM-CHOOM!

    The tiger leapt up in alarm. "What's that awful sound?"

    Shambhu recognized his wife's gong and caught her distant shadow in the bushes. To play upon the tiger's fear, Shambhu shouted at the top of his voice, "YES, IT IS BIKAL! HE HAS COME TO EAT TIGERS!"

    Bhagwati heard her husband's clue and immediately joined the act, calling out from behind a tree in a booming voice, "WHO'S THERE? ARE THERE ANY TIGERS AROUND FOR ME TO EAT?"

    The tiger trembled violently, its knees shaking. "He's looking for tigers to eat! What should I do?"

    "Quick!" Shambhu whispered. "Lie down flat and keep still! I'll tell Bikal you are just a pile of dirty clothes!"

    The terrifying predator collapsed flat onto the ground, closing its eyes. Bhagwati stepped into the clearing, brandishing her cudgel. "Have you seen any tigers here that I can eat?"

    "No," Shambhu replied loudly. "There are no tigers here—just this pile of dirty clothes."

    "I don't believe you!" Bhagwati shouted back. "Hit that pile with a heavy rock to prove it!"

    Shambhu picked up a rock. "Friend, I have to hit you gently so Bikal doesn't suspect," he whispered to the tiger.

    "Do whatever Bikal says to save us!" the tiger whimpered.

    Shambhu struck the tiger with the rock. From the bushes, Bhagwati yelled, "HIT IT HARDER! HARDER!"

    Shambhu pelted the tiger again and again with heavy stones until the bruised beast swooned into unconsciousness.

    Leaving the dazed tiger to recover from its folly, the clever couple walked back home hand-in-hand, laughing at how Bikal the Terrible had saved the day once more.
