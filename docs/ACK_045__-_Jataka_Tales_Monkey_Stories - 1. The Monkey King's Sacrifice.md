# The Monkey King's Sacrifice

Deep in the heart of a lush forest, a large tribe of monkeys lived happily on a massive, sprawling mango tree that grew right on the bank of a great river. The tree bore the sweetest, most delicious mangoes in the world, and the monkeys feasted on them every day.

But their wise leader, the Monkey King, gathered them one day with a worried frown. "Friends! We have been living happily on this mango tree for years. But I anticipate trouble soon."

"Why, master?" one of the monkeys asked.

"Men have come to live near our forest," the King explained. "They have never tasted the mango fruit. Once they do, it will be dangerous for us. Make sure that not a single fruit falls into the river! Pluck each and every bud from the branches that spread out over the water."

The monkeys did their best to carefully pluck the buds. But despite all their care, one juicy, ripe mango slipped through the leaves. With a soft *splat*, it fell into the river and was carried away by the current.

Miles downstream, a fisherman was pulling in his net when he saw the bright golden fruit. "Look! See what I have found among the fish. A strange fruit. It looks delicious!" he showed his wife.

"I have never seen a fruit like that before," she marveled. "I think we ought to take it to the King. He will be overjoyed."

They presented the strange fruit to the King. The King immediately sent for his chief forester. "He will tell us what fruit it is."

The forester examined it closely. "That's the rare mango, sir."

"Is it poisonous?" the King asked cautiously.

"Not at all, sir. In fact, it is very tasty."

The King took a small bite, and his eyes lit up. "Hm-m-m! Delicious! It smells good, too! Tell me, forester, where does this mango fruit grow?"

"There is a mango grove, just a little upstream, sir."

The greedy King immediately turned to his minister. "Make the necessary arrangements. We are leaving for that grove. And I hope you have brought some archers along."

The royal party sailed upstream until they found the magnificent mango tree. The King stepped off his boat and smiled. "Tell everyone to eat as much as they can. We will stay here for a day or two."

As night fell, the monkeys, who had been hiding in terror, began moving about the branches to find food. Hearing the rustling, the King pointed up. "Minister, what was that?"

"Just monkeys, sir, scampering among the branches."

The King licked his lips. "When it's morning, tell the archers to shoot every single monkey. Today, along with the mangoes, we shall eat monkey's flesh!"

At the very top of the tree, the terrified monkeys huddled around their leader. "Oh master, we're trapped. What are we to do now?"

"Don't panic. I'll find a way out," the Monkey King reassured them. "Now, all of you do as I tell you." 

He grabbed a thick, long creeper vine, tying one end securely to a sturdy branch and the other end around his own waist. "Now I will swing over the river to that fig tree on the opposite bank. Once I am secure, one by one, you can climb across my back to the other side."

He leaped across the rushing river. But as he grasped for the fig tree, he realized his mistake. *Oh no! The vine is too short!* 

Unable to reach the trunk, he desperately grabbed the very end of a leafy branch, stretching his body taut over the rushing water to bridge the gap. 

"What are you waiting for? Cross over my back," the King strained, holding on with all his might. 

The monkeys quickly hurried across his stretched body. "You are very kind, master," they wept as they reached safety. 

The King of men woke up and watched the spectacle in utter astonishment. "Look at that monkey king. How he must love his subjects."

But the very last monkey in the tribe was a wicked, bitter monkey who had always hated his leader. Seeing the King exhausted and stretched to his limit, the wicked monkey smiled maliciously. *Here is my chance to take revenge. I shall jump across and push him down.*

"Come on. Please hurry," the King groaned. "I am tired. I can barely hold on any longer."

"Don't worry," the wicked monkey sneered, climbing onto the King's back. "I shall soon give you everlasting peace!" With a vicious shriek, he leaped into the air and stomped down on the King's back with all his might. 

*SNAP!*

The vine broke under the sudden force. The Monkey King plummeted toward the river, crashing violently onto a rock below with a sickening *THUD*, breaking his jaw and back. 

On the opposite bank, the King of men was horrified. "Come on. Let us help the poor fellow!" he ordered his guards.

They gently lifted the dying Monkey King onto the riverbank. The human King knelt beside him in awe. "You saved them at the cost of your own life!"

The Monkey King smiled faintly, his breathing shallow. "I don't mind. I have done my duty. I am happy that my subjects are safe." 

With those final words, the noble King passed away, leaving the human King forever changed by the profound lesson of true, selfless leadership.
