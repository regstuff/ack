# The Cat Who Served a Lion

Once upon a time, there lived a Lion. He was a mighty beast before whom all trembled... all except a tiny mouse. 

Day after day, the mouse nibbled at the Lion's magnificent mane while he slept in his den. 

"Ha! Ha! Your fine mane will make a soft bed for my little ones when they come," the mouse squeaked, chewing off a tuft of hair. 

Each day when the Lion woke up, he noticed more hair missing. "Again! More of my magnificent mane has gone!" he roared in frustration. He tried waiting up to catch the culprit. "Ah! There you are. Wait till I catch you," he growled, swiping his paw. 

But the little mouse quickly scuttled into her tiny hole in the cave wall. 

"She's gone! Well, mice are not my prey," the Lion sighed. "I'm no cat. Oh! Why didn't I think of it before? I'll get myself a cat right away."

He soon came across a stray cat in the forest. "Will you come and live in my den, friend cat?" he asked. 

"Why do you want me?" the cat asked cautiously. 

"Just to frighten away a little mouse," the Lion explained. "A mouse! I'll come, friend Lion."

At the den, the Lion treated his new bodyguard like royalty. "Here, dear cat. The freshest of fish—all for you. Only keep that mouse away from my mane while I sleep."

"All for me! Mm-m-m," the cat purred happily. 

While the cat ate, she schemed to herself. *Oho! Our mighty friend has kept a cat as a bodyguard! I must be careful. Aha! The little pest is restless. Thanks to the cat, I'm not. That reminds me. I must give him his treat of fish for today.* Wait, that was the Lion thinking about the cat! *Ah, the cat is doing a good job,* the Lion thought, bringing more fish. 

The cat happily gorged on the daily fish. "Hm-m-m! Delicious, my friend. You really spoil me," she told the Lion. *If I kill the mouse,* the cat thought, *the Lion will stop bringing me fish. I must just keep her frightened.*

That afternoon, as the Lion napped and the lazy cat dozed nearby, the starving mouse crept timidly out of her hole. 

"It's my lucky day," the mouse sniffed. "The cat has dropped some food on the ground. And I starve! It's more than a week since I've left my hole. I must find something to eat."

But she spoke too soon. Suddenly, the cat pounced on her. The mouse tried to run, but the cat was too fast. With a swift swipe, the cat caught the mouse and killed her.

"The richest of cream or the freshest of fish—these are nothing compared to a delicious mouse," the cat purred, licking her lips. 

As the days passed, the Lion no longer heard the little pest moving about. "I no longer hear the little pest move about," the Lion thought happily. "She must have gone somewhere else."

Because he no longer needed the cat's services, the Lion did not bother to feed him. 

"Friend Lion, I'm hungry," the cat complained.

The Lion yawned lazily. "Then go and catch yourself some mice. I'd like to sleep now. Don't disturb me."

The cat left the den, a wiser animal. "I've been a fool! I should not have killed that mouse," she muttered, her stomach rumbling.
