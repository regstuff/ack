# Shyama and Sabala

All the gods were in Indra's court discussing the state of the world, when suddenly a messenger rushed in. "Lord Indra, there's a matter that needs your attention. A group of asuras called the Kalakanjas were trying to get to heaven." 

The Kalakanjas had devised a foolish but ambitious plan. "We will build an altar all the way up to Indra's kingdom!" their leader declared. "Bring the bricks and let each one of us lay one brick upon the other. Smell our triumph! We have found a path to heaven." 

The altar grew taller and taller as each asura climbed up to lay a brick. "The gods, meanwhile, had been trying to think of a way to stop the asuras," the messenger continued. 

"Their structure is getting taller too quickly. We have to do something, Lord Indra," a god warned. 

"We have to fight them," another suggested. 

"Is there a way to avoid war?" Indra wondered. "I need to take a closer look at what they are building." 

Indra disguised himself as a commoner and went down to see. He walked up the immense stairway of bricks. "Let's go! Heaven will soon be ours!" the asuras cheered. 

As Indra watched the asuras at work, he came up with a plan. He picked up a brick and placed it carefully on the altar. "I hope this works," he muttered. 

Eventually, the asuras' stairway to heaven was ready. Indra watched and waited till they had climbed high enough. "The asuras began to climb," he observed. 

When the structure was towering into the sky, filled with climbing asuras, Indra stealthily took his brick out from the foundation. 

Instantly, the structure lost its balance and came tumbling down in a massive cloud of dust and rubble. As they fell towards the ground, the terrified asuras started turning into spiders, and landed safely on their eight legs. 

However, the two asuras who were almost at the very top had the longest fall. As they plummeted, they turned into fierce hounds. 

They came to be known as Shyama and Sabala, the mighty hounds representing dusk and dawn. Impressed by their fierce forms, Yama, the Lord of Death, claimed them. "You will guard the gates of Yama for all eternity," he commanded. And so, it is said, they do to this day.
