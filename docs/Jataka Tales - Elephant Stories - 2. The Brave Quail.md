# The Brave Quail

In a forest near Varanasi, there once lived some quails. The shady grove in which they nested was also the favourite grazing ground of a herd of elephants. A wise and just elephant, the Bodhisattva, was the leader of that herd. 

One day, one of the quails laid some eggs. Soon the fledglings were hatched. One day, the elephant herd came marching towards the grove. 

"Look! The elephants are making for our grove! What shall I do? I can only fall at their feet and beg for protection," the mother quail cried. 

As the leader came close, the mother quail wasted no time. She was at his feet, her head lowered in salute. "O mighty elephant, my little ones are in danger. If your herd enters this grove, they will be trampled to death." 

"Do not fear. Your fledglings will not be harmed," the leader assured her. The elephant stood over the nest while his herd grazed, protecting the babies. When they had had their fill, he warned the quail. "There is a rogue-elephant, a wild and dangerous animal, who might soon be coming this way. You must appeal to him for mercy and hope for the best." 

Soon after the elephants had gone, the rogue-elephant appeared. He looked fierce. The mother quail begged him just as she had the leader. "O powerful one, I beg of you, spare my young ones!" 

"How dare you come in my way? I will soon show you how strong I am!" the arrogant rogue roared. He lashed at the nest, crushing it, and stamped the babies to death. 

"There! That is the end of your silly brood," he scoffed, and walked away. 

As the quail wept over the remains of her dead children, she made a vow. "I will soon show you how strong I am!" 

Grief had made her bold and set her thinking hard. She went to a crow and told him her sad tale. "Depend on me. Such wickedness should not go unpunished," the crow promised. "You must spot this rogue-elephant and peck out both his eyes." 

Having got the crow on her side, the quail went to an ant. "I heard about your babies. I am deeply grieved," the ant said. 

"My little friend, I need your help. My friend the crow will peck out his eyes. After that you must lay your eggs in the empty sockets. The little ones will begin to bite." 

"A good idea! When they hatch, my little ones will begin to bite," the ant agreed. 

The quail then went to the frog. "Dear friend, leave everything and come out! I am trying to get my friends to help me punish him." 

"You can count on me," the frog replied. 

"When the ants hatch, the rogue-elephant will be badly stung. He will run blindly, looking for water to ease his pain. This is what I want you to do... The croak of a frog! There must be water near by. I'll follow the sound," she explained her plan. 

A little later, the crow darted at the elephant... plucked his eyes out, and flew away. Then, the ant laid her eggs in his blind eyes. 

"A-A-AH! My eyes are on fire. Water! I need water!" the blinded, agonized elephant cried. 

Just then the frog croaked from a steep precipice close by. "CROAK!" 

As the elephant got to the edge of the precipice, the frog leapt onto a narrow ledge below and croaked with all his might. 

The elephant followed the sound and went hurtling down the cliff to his death! 

When the crow, the ant, the frog and the quail met later, the quail looked down at the dead elephant. "I hope the story of the elephant will be a good example to all strong creatures who harm the weak and the helpless."
