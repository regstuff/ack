# The Monkeys and the Gardener

The King of Varanasi had a beautiful, sprawling garden, which was meticulously looked after by a deeply loyal gardener. 

One night, a friend came to visit the gardener at his small hut. "Hello friend. The great festival in town begins tomorrow."

"I know!" the gardener sighed, rubbing his tired eyes. 

"What! Aren't you coming? There's going to be plenty of fun," his friend asked in surprise. 

"How I wish I could come!" the gardener said sadly. "But if I do, who will water the garden? It will die without me."

The next day, as the gardener was sullenly digging around a rosebush, a friendly monkey swung down from the trees. "Why do you look so sad, gardener?"

"There's a festival on in town. I want to go to it. But who will water my garden?" the gardener explained miserably.

"Pooh! That's no problem," the monkey laughed. "My friends and I will water the garden for you."

The gardener looked up, amazed. "You will? Thank you, dear monkey. I'll return in a few days, as soon as possible. Have a fine time, friend!"

Thrilled, the monkey gathered the gardener's watering cans. "Now to round up all my friends!"

Soon, a large troop of monkeys arrived. "Come on, my monkeys," their leader cheered. "We have work to do. Very important work! The gardener wants us to water his garden while he is away. Right away! Go get the watering cans and buckets."

The monkeys eagerly grabbed the tools. But as they stood before the massive garden, one monkey scratched his head. "Some trees are small and some are big! How much water should we give to each tree?"

"That's no problem at all," the confident leader announced. "All you have to do is pull up each plant and have a look at the size of its roots!"

"And then?"

"You pour less water for the smaller roots, and more water for the bigger roots!"

The troop gasped in admiration. "You are really clever! What a wonderful idea. Oh, it was quite simple really," the leader boasted. 

With great enthusiasm, the monkeys spread out across the garden. Grunting and straining, they began ruthlessly uprooting every single beautiful plant, flower, and bush, checking the roots, pouring a splash of water into the empty holes, and tossing the ruined plants aside. 

Meanwhile, in town, the gardener was enjoying the festival music. *I wonder how my monkey friends are getting along with their task?* he thought happily.

Two days later, the gardener returned home. He stopped dead in his tracks, dropping his turban in sheer horror. The magnificent royal garden was completely destroyed. Thousands of uprooted, dead plants littered the muddy ground. 

"What have you done?" he screamed at the smiling monkeys.

"We watered each plant according to the needs of its roots! That's right. We did," the monkeys nodded proudly, holding up the dead bushes. 

The gardener collapsed onto a bucket, burying his face in his hands. "Whatever shall I do now? What shall I tell the King? That's what comes of depending on a fool. He may mean well... but will end up doing more harm than anything else."
