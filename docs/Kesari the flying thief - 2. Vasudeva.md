# Vasudeva

Vasudeva came to meet his friend in the city of Shripura to ask him for a loan. "I desperately need some money," he pleaded. 

"I have none to spare. But... our King gives two gold coins to the first Brahmana he meets in the morning," his friend advised. 

"Two gold coins! Why, that's precisely what I need!" Vasudeva rejoiced. 

"I'll spend the night here and go to the palace a little before dawn," he decided. That night, Vasudeva couldn't sleep. "What if I don't wake up in time? What if another Brahmana gets there before I do and receives the money? The best thing would be not to sleep at all!" 

He went to the palace right away in the middle of the night and waited outside. "Then I'm sure to be the first." 

Suddenly, guards seized him. "Don't try to struggle! Let me go!" he cried. 

"I haven't done anything. I only wanted to see the King!" 

"What are you doing? You did, did you? Don't worry you'll see him. In good time," the guards sneered, tying him up and throwing him in a dungeon. 

The next morning he was brought before the King. "This is the dangerous spy we caught last night, Maharaj." 

"Please let me go, Maharaj. I am not a spy," Vasudeva begged. 

"Then what were you doing outside the palace at midnight?" the King asked. 

"I was making sure no one got there before I did." 

"For what?" 

"To receive the two gold coins you give to the first Brahmana you see in the day!" 

The King was moved. "Poor man! All that for just two gold coins! My good man, why two gold coins? I shall give you anything you want. Anything I want, Maharaj! Then may I have..." 

"Maharaj, I need some time to think about what I want," Vasudeva requested. 

"Take your time, my good man. Come back to me when you've decided." 

Vasudeva went to a quiet spot in the forest to think. "Twenty coins will take care of all my needs. But two hundred would bring me many comforts. Shall I make it two hundred coins... or perhaps two thousand? The King can surely afford more. I'll make it a lakh. But a crore will get me every possible thing I want on earth. And two would... two would..." 

He realized that his greed was growing exponentially. "And the more you have the more you want. There are no limits to one's wants. There is only one solution..." 

When Vasudeva returned to the palace, the King asked, "Well, my good man? Have you decided what you want?" 

"Yes, Maharaj. Nothing." 

"Nothing?" the King was surprised. 

"Yes. I wanted money because I was unhappy without it. But I have realised that with it I would be even unhappier," Vasudeva explained. 

"In fact, happiest is he who does not want anything," he concluded. 

The King smiled. "And I bless you for giving me an opportunity to learn this truth."
