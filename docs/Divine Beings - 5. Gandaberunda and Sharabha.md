# Gandaberunda and Sharabha

The evil king Hiranyakashipu had just been slain by Vishnu's fourth incarnation, the fearsome Narasimha. Hiranyakashipu had once asked Brahma for a boon to make him invincible: "I wish that no man or beast be able to slay me, that I be immortal during day and night and that no weapon be strong enough to kill me." 

Vishnu's form as Narasimha was neither man nor beast. Narasimha had killed Hiranyakashipu with his claws at dusk, thus finding a way around Brahma's boon and ridding the world of the evil king's tyranny. 

However, with the killing of Hiranyakashipu, Narasimha had tasted blood. He lost sight of his original purpose and continued on a fearful rampage. "Roar!" he bellowed, terrifying the three worlds. 

The gods went to Shiva in panic. "We must stop Narasimha. He will destroy everything if he continues like this!" they pleaded. 

"I will stop him," Shiva promised. 

Shiva took the form of Sharabha, a massive, terrifying half-bird, half-lion being. He flew down to confront the rampaging avatar. "I will envelop him in my wings to calm him down. His rage will be difficult to control," Sharabha thought as he approached Narasimha. 

But the act of being restrained only angered Narasimha further. "Arrrrgh!" he roared, struggling against Sharabha's grip. 

And from Narasimha's body emerged a creature even bigger and more fearsome than Sharabha—the Gandaberunda, a gigantic two-headed bird of unimaginable strength. 

Gandaberunda, the two-headed bird, met Sharabha in a colossal battle in the skies. "Oh no! What will happen now!" the gods watched in horror. 

They fought fiercely for eighteen days. The impact of their clashes shook the heavens and the earth. The gods watched in terror as the fight destroyed everything in its wake. 

At the end of the eighteenth day, Gandaberunda suddenly stopped to look around him. "What have we done! Everything around us has been reduced to ashes. This must end now," the dual minds of the bird realized. 

The mighty Gandaberunda voluntarily split into two. As the creature divided, a calm and peaceful Vishnu emerged from the centre of the fearsome beast. 

Seeing this, Shiva too resumed his normal form. "Let us go back, my friend," Shiva smiled. 

"Yes, let's," Vishnu agreed. Peace was finally restored to the universe.
