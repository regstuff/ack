# Ganesha and Ravana

Ravana, the demon king of Lanka, observed severe penance to please Shiva. He stood on one leg amidst the icy peaks of the Himalayas, chanting "Om Namah Shivaya!" 

Pleased with his austerities, Shiva appeared before him. "Ask for a boon, Ravana. I will grant you anything you wish for." 

"Lord, I want the Atmalinga," Ravana asked eagerly. 

Shiva was dismayed. "The Atmalinga has the power to make him invincible. But I have promised to give him whatever he asks for." 

Reluctantly, Shiva handed over the sacred Atmalinga. "You must walk all the way to Lanka holding the Atmalinga in your hands. It's a long way from Mount Kailash to Lanka, but I can do it," Shiva warned. "Don't put the Atmalinga down. If you do, even for a moment, it will stay rooted to that spot for ever." 

Ravana was jubilant. "No one can ever destroy me! I have acquired the very power of Shiva!" And then Shiva disappeared. 

Ravana started his journey back home, carefully carrying the sacred linga. 

Up in the heavens, the gods watched, aghast. "Ravana is our enemy and a menace to the world! With the Atmalinga, his power will be greater than ever! He will defeat us all!" 

They sought Lord Ganesha's help. "O Lord! Stop Ravana from taking the Atmalinga to Lanka. If not, the world will be in mortal danger." 

Ganesha thought for a while. "Hmmm... that is not an easy task." And then, "Do not worry. I will find a way to stop Ravana." The gods were reassured. 

Ganesha followed Ravana from the heavens. Ravana walked very fast. He soon reached the west coast and strode along the deserted sea shore. 

"There he is! He must not see me," Ganesha thought, hiding behind a tree. "Now is my chance!" 

"Ravana is a stickler for rituals. He has to perform the evening prayers at sunset every day," Ganesha knew. He invoked the power of Lord Vishnu to block out the sun with his chakra, making it suddenly dark. 

Ravana was puzzled for a moment. "Strange how the sun set so suddenly! But he was unsuspecting. It is time for me to say my evening prayers." 

"Ah, you creature of habit! This will cause your defeat," Ganesha chuckled. 

Ravana found himself in a dilemma. "I cannot put the linga down and I cannot pray with it in my hands. I should ask someone to hold it for me. But there is no one here!" He looked around, worried. 

Just then, he saw a young Brahmin boy walking on the beach. It was Ganesha in disguise. Ravana immediately held out the Atmalinga. "Here, boy, hold this for me." 

The boy shook his head. "No, I can't! Just hold it till I finish my prayers. It's too heavy for me. Keep it on the ground." 

"If it is too heavy for me, can I put it on the ground?" the boy asked innocently. 

"NO! NO! Then I can't hold it," Ravana panicked. 

"Well... all right," the boy agreed reluctantly. 

Relieved, Ravana handed over the Atmalinga and walked into the sea to begin his prayers. 

The wily Ganesha waited until Ravana was in the middle of his prayers. Then, he shouted loudly, "THIS IS TOO HEAVY FOR ME! I AM GOING TO PUT IT DOWN!" 

Ravana was alarmed. "NO, DON'T! I'LL COME AS SOON AS I FINISH. JUST DON'T PUT IT DOWN," he yelled back from the water. 

Ravana hurried through his prayers, trying to finish as soon as possible, when Ganesha shouted again. "IT'S TOO HEAVY! I'M PUTTING IT DOWN!" 

Ravana finished his prayers immediately, and rushed back, but it was too late. The boy had placed the linga on the sand. 

Suddenly, the sun was back in the sky. Vishnu had removed the illusion. 

"I HAVE BEEN TRICKED!" Ravana roared in fury. He tried to move the linga. "IT WON'T BUDGE!" 

An enraged Ravana chased the boy. "TAKE THAT, YOU RASCAL!" he yelled, hitting him thrice on his head with his fists. 

Then Ganesha assumed his true form. Realising what he had done, Ravana bowed before Ganesha and accepted his defeat. 

The place where Ganesha placed the Atmalinga is believed to be in Gokarna, Karnataka. There is a temple built around the Atmalinga. The temple over the Atmalinga is called the Mahabaleshwar temple. 'Mahabala' means great power and 'Ishwar' means God. Next to it is a shrine of Ganesha, where the idol has a dent on its forehead, symbolising the incident. 

The great force exerted by Ravana on the linga is said to have changed its shape. The linga now resembles the ear of a cow. In fact, the name Gokarna, comes from this. 'Go' - cow and 'Karna' - ear.
