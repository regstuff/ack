# How the Miser Outsmarted Himself

Once upon a time there was a miserly king. One day, a son was born to him. While everyone else rejoiced, this made him very unhappy. 

"Now I'll have to give a feast to the villagers," he grumbled to his Diwan. "And they eat so much! What should I do?" 

"Ah! I have an idea!" the Diwan whispered, hatching a cunning plan. 

At the feast, each guest sat eagerly in front of a large plate. But they were shocked when they were served just two laddoos each. 

"Is this all we are going to get?" one man frowned. 

"It's shameful!" another agreed angrily. 

The king stepped forward with a wide grin. "Good people, today we are going to play a wonderful game. You must be wondering why you have been served just two laddoos. Well, as soon as you finish those two, you will be served another two and when you finish those, another two and so on." 

The guests looked pleased. 

"Besides, for every laddoo you eat you will be paid two rupees. BUT..." the king paused dramatically. "...you must eat the laddoos without bending your arm at the elbow and without putting your mouth to your plate." 

The king stepped back, looking extremely smug. "Now you may start." 

The guests were perplexed. "How can we eat without bending our arms? Or without putting our mouths to our plates?" they grumbled, trying to reach the sweets with stiff arms. 

"They'll have to get up without eating now! Heehee!" the king chuckled to his Diwan. 

Then suddenly one man got an idea. "I know how we can do it!" he whispered to the man next to him. 

"What are they whispering?" the king wondered. 

"Let them whisper. There's no way they can eat without bending their elbows... or without... what are they doing!" the king's eyes widened in horror. 

The clever guests had figured it out. Instead of feeding themselves, they simply picked up the laddoos with straight arms and fed them to the person sitting across from them! 

"They've tricked me! Each man is feeding his neighbour!" the king cried. 

The guests ate and ate... much to the dismay of their host. They fed each other plate after plate of sweets. 

At the end of the feast, each guest had eaten at least fifty laddoos! 

"My stomach is full and I have earned a hundred rupees," one guest burped happily. 

"I have earned a hundred and two!" his friend grinned. 

"Stop! Stop! Don't eat so much! You'll get a stomach-ache!" the king wept, watching his treasury drain away. "I tried to save money and ended up paying out a fortune."
