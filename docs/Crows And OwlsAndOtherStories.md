# Crows and Owls and Other Stories

Adapted from Pandit Vishnu Sharma's ancient Sanskrit masterpiece, the *Panchatantra*, these classic fables explore politics, war, statecraft, and human nature through the engaging medium of animal lore.

### Crows and Owls

Deep within a dense forest stood a magnificent banyan tree that housed a prosperous colony of crows, ruled by King Cloudy (*Meghavarna*). 

Not far away, in a dark mountain cave, lived a colony of owls led by King Foe-Crusher (*Arimardana*). The owls held a deep, ancestral enmity toward the crows. Because owls possess superior night vision while crows are blind in the dark, Foe-Crusher led nightly raids on the banyan tree, slaughtering dozens of sleeping crows every night.

Distressed by the heavy losses, King Cloudy summoned his five chief ministers. "The owls attack us at night when we cannot see," the King addressed his council. "Advise me—what strategy should we adopt?"

The first minister advised, "Make peace with powerful foes."

The second minister urged, "Declare open war on the rascals!"

The third minister suggested, "Let us abandon this banyan tree and move to another forest."

The fourth minister argued, "A crocodile in its home water can defeat an elephant. Let us stay here and fortify our positions!"

Finally, King Cloudy turned to his oldest and wisest counselor, Live-Strong (*Sanjeevaka*). "And what do you advise, revered sir?"

"My Lord, I advise duplicity," Live-Strong replied calmly. "You must publicly stage a fierce quarrel with me. Strike me, pluck my feathers, and cast me out of the tree as a traitor. Then fly with all your subjects to Antelope Mountain. I shall remain behind, gain entry into the owl fortress, gather intelligence, and bring about their total destruction."

The next morning, as an owl spy watched from a nearby branch, the crows staged a dramatic quarrel. Live-Strong berated King Cloudy, and in response, the crow army pecked Live-Strong, stripped his feathers, and cast him down into the bushes before flying away toward Antelope Mountain.

The owl spy rushed to King Foe-Crusher. "Your Majesty, the crows have fallen out among themselves! They have beaten their senior minister and abandoned their tree!"

Foe-Crusher led his army to the banyan tree and found the battered Live-Strong lying in the grass. 

"Who are you?" Foe-Crusher demanded.

"I was King Cloudy's chief minister," Live-Strong groaned feigning agony. "When I advised him to pay tribute to your Majesty, he attacked me and cast me out. I throw myself at your mercy, and I will lead you to their destruction!"

Foe-Crusher consulted his five ministers. Senior minister Red-Eye (*Raktaksha*) warned sharply, "Do not grant asylum to an enemy! Slay him immediately! A crow can never be trusted."

However, the other four ministers argued, "It would be unrighteous to kill one who seeks protection. He will be useful in our war against the crows."

Overriding Red-Eye's warning, Foe-Crusher granted Live-Strong asylum and ordered his guards to carry him to their cave fortress. Sensing disaster, the shrewd Red-Eye gathered his loyal followers and departed from the fortress forever.

At the cave, Live-Strong declined to sleep inside the inner chambers. "O King, I am an unworthy crow. Allow me to stay near the entrance of your cave."

Every day, as the owls slept, Live-Strong brought dry twigs and wooden sticks from the forest, stacking them neatly outside the narrow cave entrance. The owls assumed their guest was merely building a cozy nest.

After weeks of gathering wood, a massive wall of dry twigs blocked the entrance of the cave. 

One morning, while the owls slept soundly inside, Live-Strong flew to Antelope Mountain and summoned King Cloudy. "My Lord, the trap is set! Bring burning torches!"

The crow army streaked through the morning sky, each crow carrying a flaming wooden branch in its beak. At Live-Strong's direction, they dropped the fire onto the dry twigs stacked outside the cave entrance.

Within moments, a roaring fire blazed, sealing the cave entrance. Dense, suffocating smoke flooded the cavern. Trapped inside the mountain furnace, King Foe-Crusher and his entire owl army perished in the flames.

Having eliminated their powerful enemies through master strategy, King Cloudy and his crows returned to their banyan tree in triumph.

### The Noble Enemy

Four travelers walking through a rocky mountain pass discovered a sack filled with priceless gems. Knowing the mountain paths were frequented by dacoits, each traveler swallowed a portion of the gems to conceal them safely in their stomachs.

Unbeknownst to them, a robber had been watching from behind a boulder. *Aha!* the robber thought. *They have swallowed precious gems! I shall pretend to be a friendly traveler, join them, and when they fall asleep tonight, I will slit open their bellies and take the treasure.*

The robber ran up to them with a friendly smile. "Good masters, I am alone on this dangerous road. May I travel in your company?"

"Welcome, friend! The more, the merrier," the travelers agreed.

As they walked toward the next village, they passed a mountain hut belonging to a fierce robber chief who kept a trained caged parrot. The moment the four travelers passed, the parrot began to squawk loudly: *"They have gems with them! They have gems!"*

The robber chief immediately dispatched his men to capture the four travelers. Searching their bags and clothes, the bandits found no gems.

"Search them again!" the chief barked. "My bird never lies!"

When a second search yielded nothing, the chief looked at the caged parrot, which continued to chant: *"Gems in their bellies! Gems in their bellies!"*

"The gems are in their stomachs!" the chief realized. "Take them away and slit open their bellies!"

Hearing this, the robber who had joined them panicked. *If they cut open their bellies and find gems, they will cut mine open next and kill me too! But if I offer to be killed first, they will cut my stomach, find no gems, and conclude the bird was mistaken—thus saving the others!*

Stepping forward boldly, the robber shouted, "Sir! You will find no gems in our stomachs. But if you insist on checking, cut open my belly first!"

The chief's men took the robber away and slit his stomach. Finding no gems inside, the chief cursed his bird. "The bird made a mistake! There are no gems!" He released the remaining three travelers with an apology.

*Moral: A sensible enemy can sometimes prove to be your greatest savior.*

### The Bird and the Monkeys

On a freezing winter night in the forest, a troop of monkeys sat shivering beneath a large banyan tree. Suddenly, one monkey spotted a glowing firefly drifting through the dark bushes.

"Look, brothers!" the monkey chattered excitedly. "A spark of fire!"

The monkeys caught the firefly, covered it with a pile of dry leaves, and began blowing on it with all their might to start a campfire.

A bird perched on a branch above watched their foolish efforts and called down gently, "Good sirs, you are making a mistake! That is a firefly, not a spark of fire. You cannot light a fire with a glow-worm."

"Mind your own business, bird!" the monkeys chattered, continuing to blow on the leaves.

Seeing them waste their energy, the bird flew down to a lower branch. "Listen to me, friends! You are wasting your breath. A firefly carries no heat."

Annoyed by her unsolicited advice, one of the monkeys grabbed the bird and struck her down onto the ground.

*Moral: Do not attempt to instruct those who lack the wisdom to listen.*

### The Camel Who Was Beguiled by His Companions

A merchant's camel collapsed from exhaustion while passing through a thick jungle. Lacking time, the merchant unloaded the burden onto other animals and left the camel behind.

In time, the camel recovered his strength, feeding on the rich forest grass. One afternoon, Madotkata the Lion, King of the forest, passed by accompanied by his court—a leopard, a jackal, and a crow.

Staring at the tall animal, the Lion asked, "What strange creature is that?"

"It is a camel, your Majesty," the crow answered. 

The Lion approached the camel, heard his story, and granted him royal protection: "Fear not, my friend. Henceforth you shall live in my court under my protection."

Months passed harmoniously. But one day, the Lion engaged in a fierce combat with a wild elephant and was severely wounded. Unable to hunt, the Lion remained confined to his cave for weeks.

Deprived of prey, the leopard, jackal, and crow began to starve. Sitting outside the cave, the jackal whispered to the crow, "There is no need for us to starve. The camel's flesh could sustain us for days!"

"But the King has given him his protection," the crow reminded. "He will never kill the camel."

"Leave that to me," the cunning jackal smiled.

The jackal entered the cave and addressed the weak Lion: "Majesty, we cannot bear to see you starve. If you permit, you may kill the camel for food."

"Never!" the Lion roared in anger. "I have given him my word of protection!"

"What if the camel voluntarily offers his own body to you?" the jackal suggested smoothly. "Then you would commit no sin."

The Lion hesitated. "If he offers voluntarily, I might accept."

The jackal returned to his companions and assembled them before the Lion's cave. Stepping forward first, the crow bowed to the Lion: "Master, you are starving. Eat me and prolong your life!"

The jackal interrupted, "You are too small! The master's hunger would hardly be appeased by a bird. Eat me instead, Sire!"

The leopard pushed the jackal aside. "You are too small as well! Master, eat me!"

Watching this display of apparent devotion, the naive camel thought, *All of them have offered their lives, yet the King has not harmed any of them. I too should make a noble gesture to show my loyalty!*

Stepping into the center of the ring, the camel announced proudly, "Master! Eat me! I offer my body to sustain my King!"

Before the camel could realize his mistake, the Lion roared, "I accept your offer, noble camel!" 

Instantly, the leopard, jackal, and crow pounced upon the unsuspecting camel, killing him on the spot.

*Moral: Beware of false friends who use flattery to manipulate you to your doom.*
