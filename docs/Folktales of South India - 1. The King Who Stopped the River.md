# The King Who Stopped the River

Once upon a time, there was a foolish king who had a wise Diwan. One warm summer night, the king could not sleep. "BONG..." the palace gong sounded. 

"It must be almost one o'clock," the king muttered, tossing and turning. 

An hour later, "BONG... BONG..." 

"There goes another gong... It's two o'clock," the king groaned. 

At last, when he heard the sixth gong, the king sat up. "It's six o'clock! I'll have to get up now. But I feel so dull. I think I'll ride out into the countryside and get some fresh air. Tell the Diwan to get ready. We will go riding today." 

So the king and his wise Diwan set out. An hour later, the Diwan asked, "Your majesty, how do you feel now?" 

"Much better. But I'm thirsty. Let's stop by that river." 

They stopped by a beautiful, sparkling river. "Such clear, sparkling water! Where does this river flow?" the king asked. 

"It flows down to that kingdom in the East, your majesty," the Diwan replied. 

The king was outraged. "Our river flowing into their country? We must stop it at once! No buts, Diwan. I want a dam built here." 

The dam was built. But now, since the river could not flow down its usual course, it overflowed its banks and flooded the countryside. 

"It will be worse during the monsoon," the Diwan warned. 

"So what?" the king scoffed. "We've got our river all to ourselves, haven't we? How foolish can he get?" 

The Diwan realized he had to do something. "Can't he see that our neighbours will soon attack us for stopping their water supply? I must get him to break down that dam... Ah! I've got it!" 

That evening, the Diwan went up to the tower from which the gong was sounded and spoke to the man there. "After midnight, I want you to sound the gong every half-hour. Not every hour, as you do now. As you command, sir." 

Because of the Diwan's order, it was only 3 o'clock when the sixth gong was sounded. "BONG... BONG... BONG... BONG... BONG... BONG!" 

"The sixth gong! It's six o'clock. I'd better wake up the others," the man thought. He woke up the guards. "Get up! Our duty is over..." 

"Is it six o'clock already? It is," they said, confused by the darkness. 

"Then why is it so dark?" 

"The sun should have been up by now. Something must have happened to it. What could have happened to the sun? How should I know? We'd better sound the alarm." 

"Wake up! Wake up, everybody! Something has happened to the sun!" 

There was panic in the palace. The commander of the army rushed to the king's chamber. "Wake up, your majesty! A terrible thing has happened!" 

"Eh...?" the king yawned. "It's six o'clock, but the sun hasn't risen as yet. Hasn't risen? What do you mean the sun hasn't risen?!" 

"It hasn't, your majesty. See for yourself." 

The king looked out the window. "You're right. Summon the Diwan!" 

When the Diwan came, the king demanded, "What's all this! Why hasn't the sun come up as yet?" 

"I have been thinking about it, your majesty. There can be only one reason." 

"What?" 

"Somebody has caught the sun and is not letting it come over our land." 

"Caught the sun? Ah, yes! That's what I thought too... But who would do such a wicked deed?" 

"It must be the king of the Eastern kingdom, who else? I heard he was angry because we stopped the river from flowing into his country. And as you know, the sun passes over his kingdom before it comes over ours." 

"Your majesty, we must do something about it soon or we'll be in darkness forever," the Diwan warned. 

"Do you think... Do you think he would let the sun go if we let the river flow into his country again?" the king asked hopefully. 

"What a brilliant idea, your majesty!" the Diwan praised. "Ah, well, my mother always said I was a very clever fellow!" the king beamed. 

"Well, now... we'll have to break down that dam. I agree. Summon the commander of my army!" 

"I am here, your majesty," the commander stepped forward. 

"Get your men! We are going down to the river to pull down that dam as fast as we can." 

The king led his men to the river, and before dawn, they broke the dam down. The river began to flow to the neighbouring country again. 

"The sun should be coming up any moment now," the king said confidently. 

And sure enough, as dawn naturally broke, the sun appeared over the horizon. "The sun! Look! They've let the sun go!" the men cheered. 

"Your plan worked, your majesty. You have saved the country," the Diwan smiled. 

"Oh, it was nothing..." the king replied modestly. The foolish king never realized how he had been outsmarted by his wise Diwan!
