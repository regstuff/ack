# Shiva and Arjuna

Deep in the quiet, peaceful Himalayan retreat of Indrakeela, an ascetic arrived and began observing severe penances. The other young ascetics living in the forest watched him closely from a distance. 

"Who could this stranger be?" they wondered. "Look at his bow! Could he be Arjuna, the Pandava?" 

It was indeed the great Pandava warrior, Arjuna. He had retreated to the mountains to propitiate Lord Shiva, hoping to earn divine grace to win back his kingdom from the Kauravas. 

"Om Namo Shivaya," Arjuna chanted, day and night. For days, he persisted in deep meditation. As the months passed, his penance became so intense and severe that a terrible, burning heat began to radiate from his body, spreading far and wide and choking the whole forest with smoke. 

Unable to bear the heat, the other ascetics of Indrakeela travelled all the way to Mount Kailasa, the icy abode of Lord Shiva. 

"Lord, grant Arjuna his wish, and relieve us of this suffering," they pleaded. 

"So be it," Lord Shiva nodded. 

When the ascetics departed, Goddess Parvati turned to Shiva. "What does Arjuna want, my Lord?" 

"He wants celestial weapons," Shiva explained. 

"Can he wield them, my Lord?"

"I will find out by testing him," Shiva smiled. "I'll appear before him as a Kirata (a tribal hunter) and engage him in a duel."

"May I accompany you?" Parvati asked excitedly. 

"You may, in the guise of a Kirata woman."

Hearing of this great combat, the fierce, skeletal hordes of Shiva also begged to join. Accompanied by his cheering hordes disguised as Kirata women, Lord Shiva and Parvati approached the forests of Indrakeela. 

As they neared Arjuna's hermitage, a massive wild boar suddenly ran past them. Unbeknownst to anyone, the boar was actually the deadly demon Mookasura in disguise, sent to assassinate Arjuna. 

"Hah! A fit target for my arrow!" Shiva announced, raising his bow. 

But the wily, swift boar outdistanced the Kirata and charged straight into the quiet hermitage, destroying everything in its path and driving the ascetics helter-skelter. 

"It is Mookasura again! Now in the form of a boar!" the ascetics panicked. 

Disturbed by the massive din, Arjuna opened his eyes, grabbed his mighty bow, and took aim at the charging beast. 

Just as he was about to shoot, Shiva burst through the trees. "Stop! It's mine," the Kirata yelled. "Leave it to me. I was stalking it."

"Hunters do not beg," Arjuna replied coldly. 

Both warriors released their arrows at the exact same instant. The boar was caught perfectly between the two unerring arrows and fell dead, instantly revealing its true, terrifying demonic form. 

"Victory to our Lord! Your arrow killed it," the Kirata women cheered, dancing around Shiva. 

The wild exultation amused Arjuna. "O Kirata, does not this thick forest terrify your women folk? And you their only escort? Perhaps you are terrified. You do appear soft!" 

"Soft? Me? Didn't you see the force of my arrow piercing the boar?" Shiva laughed. 

"It was our chief's arrow that killed the boar," the Kirata women insisted. 

Arjuna was deeply enraged by the insult. "All right, Kirata. We will settle once and for all who is the better shot."

A ferocious duel began. Arrows whizzed past as the two great archers matched their incredible skills. But to Arjuna's utter shock, no matter how many arrows he fired, he could not land a single hit on the tribal hunter. 

After a while, Arjuna's quiver was completely empty. "My quiver is empty and not a scratch yet on the Kirata," Arjuna thought in disbelief. 

"O mighty archer, shall I lend you a few arrows?" the Kirata mocked. 

Furious, Arjuna lunged forward and, in a deft move, caught the Kirata in his bowstring. But the next moment, the Kirata effortlessly wrested the heavy bow from Arjuna's hands and tossed it away like a twig. 

"The ascetic is beaten!" the Kirata women danced for joy. 

Undaunted, Arjuna drew his heavy sword and rushed at the Kirata. He brought the sword crashing down on the hunter's head, but with a loud *CLANG*, the supposedly invincible sword shattered into pieces against the Kirata's skull!

Shorn of his arms, Arjuna continued the fight, uprooting massive trees and hurling them at the hunter. When that failed, he charged at the Kirata with his bare hands. But the Kirata remained completely unscathed. 

Finally, with a simple flick of his wrist, the Kirata lifted the muscular Pandava warrior over his head and flung him down onto the hard earth. 

Bruised, beaten, and completely humbled, Arjuna sat in the dirt. *No ordinary mortal could ever defeat me so easily,* Arjuna realized. He closed his eyes and thought of Lord Shiva and his divine grace. 

Right on the spot, he fashioned a simple Shiva Linga out of clay and began to worship it. As he offered a handful of forest flowers to the Linga, a new, divine power surged through his exhausted limbs. 

A rejuvenated Arjuna sprang to his feet and challenged his rival again. "Kirata, your time is up!" 

But as he raised his fists, Arjuna stopped, absolutely transfixed. 

The fresh flowers he had just offered to the clay Linga were now resting peacefully on the head of the Kirata! 

"The flowers, I offered to my Lord Shiva, on your head! You are none other than Him!" Arjuna gasped in awe. He immediately fell to his knees, pressing his forehead to the Kirata's feet. "O Lord, pardon me and my vanity."

Lord Shiva smiled warmly and revealed himself in his true, magnificent form, and so did Goddess Parvati. 

"I am pleased with your devotion and unparalleled courage, Arjuna," Lord Shiva blessed him. "I shall impart to you the secret of the invincible Pashupata missile, which will help you in the hour of need."

Shiva's word came true. Years later in the great Mahabharata war, it was only with the divine Pashupata that Arjuna could finally defeat his arch-rival, Karna.
