# The Adventures of Agad Dutta

One day, Dridha Prahari, one of ancient Kaushambi's most renowned teachers, presented his brilliant young disciple, Agad Datta, at the royal court. The gifted youth performed spectacular feats of archery before the King, shooting moving targets blindfolded. 

"He certainly is a skilful archer!" the King applauded. "Give him a hundred gold coins for his trouble and send him away."

But Agad Datta refused the gold. "Maharaj, I came here to win your appreciation, not your gold," the young man said seriously. 

Before the King could respond, the Chief of the Guards burst into the courtroom. "Maharaj, the dacoits have struck again!" he cried. "We've lost everything but our lives. We've complained to the chief of the guards, but the situation hasn't improved."

The King turned to Agad Datta. "Now tell me, of what use are your feats of archery when dacoits harass my citizens with absolute impunity? Maharaj, I have tried my best, but the thugs are elusive," the King sighed.

"Give me a chance, Maharaj!" Agad Datta offered bravely. "I'll try to capture the leader of the gang."

"If you do that, you will win my deep appreciation and my eternal gratitude," the King promised. 

As Agad Datta left the palace, he planned his next move. *The best way to avoid attracting attention would be to change into some old clothes. I'll do so and then go to a quiet place to think.*

He dressed in rags and wandered to a ruined temple in a forest on the outskirts of the city. *Thieves usually choose gambling-dens, liquor shops, old gardens and temple ruins as hide-outs. I must first visit these.*

Just then, a bearded Sadhu holding a heavy iron trident came towards him. 

*This man looks worried, and poor. He'd make a good disciple!* the Sadhu thought. He approached Agad Datta. "Son, who are you? Why do you look so worried?"

"I'm from Ujjayini. I've lost all my wealth here in Kaushambi," Agad Datta lied perfectly. "What shall I tell him? Not the truth in any case."

"Don't worry," the Sadhu said kindly. "I'll show you how to get it back. And if you are clever enough, you'll get back more than you've lost. Come. Let's go to the city. We have a lot to do before dawn."

As they walked towards the city, a sudden roar of a wild animal echoed through the trees. To Agad Datta's complete amazement, the Sadhu grabbed his trident, twisted the stem, and drew a long, gleaming sword hidden perfectly inside the hollow iron staff!

*What! A sadhu armed with a secret weapon! Strange! Very strange!* Agad Datta realized. 

"Ha! That was a false alarm! Let's go on," the Sadhu laughed, sliding the sword back into the trident. 

The Sadhu led Agad Datta into the dark city and stopped in the backyard of a massive mansion. "I'll have to get into the house," the Sadhu whispered. 

"Shall I knock at the door, Gurudev?" Agad Datta asked innocently.

"No! That would unnecessarily disturb everyone. You'll soon see." 

The Sadhu pulled out tools and expertly began breaking through the thick brick wall of the mansion. 

*He's breaking into the house! He... he's a thug!* Agad Datta realized, his heart pounding. *There you are! That's exactly what I propose to do, my friend. I'm certain I won't have to go much further in search of my man!*

After some time, the fake Sadhu came crawling back out of the hole with a heavy bundle of stolen gold. "Here. Take this and follow me."

The Sadhu led him deep into the forest to a secret hide-out. Several other thugs were already there, counting their own loot. 

"Ah, my friends! I see that you have made a better haul than I!" the Sadhu laughed. "But I have found a new disciple!"

As dawn broke, the tired thugs lay down to sleep under the trees. Agad Datta pretended to sleep but kept his eyes slightly open. 

*So he is the leader of the thugs!* Agad Datta thought. *I wonder what he's up to now!*

He watched as the Sadhu quietly stood up, pulled out a small pouch, and began sprinkling a strange powder on the faces of his sleeping men. 

*What's that? Why is he sprinkling powder on their faces?* Agad Datta realized the horrible truth. *He plans to kill his men and keep all the loot for himself! The scoundrel!*

As the Sadhu drew his hidden sword to slaughter the sleeping men, Agad Datta sprang up like lightning. "No you don't, you rascal!" 

Agad Datta quickly snatched the Sadhu's own sword from his hands and struck him down with a single, fatal blow. 

Hearing the commotion, the other thugs woke up. Realizing that their leader had just tried to kill them, they immediately surrendered to Agad Datta. One of the thugs pointed to the dead Sadhu. 

"My son... you shouldn't have done that," the dying thug groaned. "My sister will appear... give her my sword. She will marry you... and you will become... the master of... all my wealth..." With those words, the leader died. 

Following the dead man's instructions, Agad Datta walked to the corner of a nearby crematorium and called out. A beautiful maiden emerged from an underground passage. Agad Datta handed her the sword. 

"Yes, I recognize it. It's my brother's sword," she smiled. "You are welcome, stranger!"

*The villain! He's slain my brother,* she thought fiercely to herself. *But I must not betray my emotions. I must remain calm.*

She led Agad Datta through the underground passage to a luxurious cellar. "You seem to be tired. Why don't you rest on this bed while I warm up some food for you?" she offered sweetly. 

*Perhaps, this is a death-trap. I must not trust her,* Agad Datta realized. 

As soon as she left the room, he quickly rolled off the bed and hid in the corner. Just in time! With a loud *CRASH!*, a massive, heavy stone block plummeted from the ceiling, completely crushing the bed where he was supposed to be sleeping. 

"Oh, God!" the maiden gasped, running back into the room. 

Agad Datta stepped out of the shadows. "No, you haven't, wicked one! I'm still alive."

Realizing she was defeated, she fell to her knees. "No human being could have survived that! Lord, I beg you, please forgive me."

Agad Datta took her and all the recovered wealth directly to the King. 

"Maharaj! I found the leader of the thugs but I couldn't capture him alive," Agad Datta reported, presenting the vast treasure. He pointed to the maiden. "But I request you to pardon her."

"How can I refuse you, Agad Datta?" the King smiled in immense relief. "She may stay in the palace and be my Queen's maid."

The King rewarded Agad Datta handsomely. "Agad Datta, my guards need a Chief of your calibre. Why don't you make Kaushambi your home?"

"Maharaj, my father was Charioteer to the King of Ujjayini," Agad Datta bowed respectfully. "It was my mother's wish that I should learn the art of warfare here, and later acquire the office once held by my father. Now that I have completed my training, I would like to return home."

Before leaving Kaushambi, Agad Datta married a beautiful girl named Shyama. With his new wife by his side, he rode out of Kaushambi in a grand chariot. 

On the road, he met a group of wealthy travellers. "We welcome you, Agad Datta," they greeted him. "We have also heard of your skill in wielding weapons and of your presence of mind. If we could accompany you, we'd have nothing to fear from wild animals and dacoits."

Agad Datta happily agreed. A while later, another Sadhu walking on the road asked to join them. "Could I come with you to Ujjayini? I have fifty gold coins given to me by a sadhu, and I fear for my safety."

"You are welcome," Agad Datta smiled. 

But Agad Datta noticed something deeply suspicious about the new Sadhu. *Who is that young man?* the Sadhu thought maliciously, staring at Agad Datta. *Don't you know? He is the famous Agad Datta of Ujjayini! The villain who killed my friend! The friend who gave me the fifty gold coins! My search has ended.*

Towards evening, they camped in a dense jungle. The Sadhu cheerfully offered to cook for everyone. "My friends have been generous. There's enough for all of us," the Sadhu smiled, bringing out a huge vessel of delicious, sweet *kheer*. 

As soon as the kheer was portioned out, Agad Datta stopped his wife. "Shyama! Wait! It's not wise to eat food cooked by strangers. Don't eat it!" 

"I'm hungry. But I won't eat it, if you say so," Shyama agreed. 

The other hungry travellers, however, eagerly swallowed their portions to the very last gulp. Within minutes, they all fell into a deep, unbreakable slumber. 

"The journey must have made them tired and sleepy," Shyama yawned. 

"Or was it the kheer?" Agad Datta whispered, drawing his sword. "I'm sure the kheer was drugged."

When it became pitch-dark, the Sadhu silently stood up and pulled a sharp sword from the hollow stem of his trident! 

"Shyama, pretend to be asleep, but remain alert!" Agad Datta whispered. "I don't trust that sadhu."

As the Sadhu raised his sword to execute the sleeping travellers and steal their wealth, Agad Datta leaped out of the shadows. "You scoundrel!"

A fierce, brutal sword fight followed in the moonlight. Agad Datta's superior skills quickly overwhelmed the murderous thug, and the villain was struck down. 

When the sun rose and the drugged travellers finally woke from their stupor, they gasped in shock at the dead body. "Why, this is the dreaded dacoit Dhanpujak! He must have been out to avenge the death of his friend!" 

Agad Datta had saved them all. When he finally reached Ujjayini, his mother gave him a tearful, joyous welcome. 

At the royal court, the King of Ujjayini welcomed him with open arms. "Agad Datta, Ujjayini is proud of you. The coveted post of Royal Charioteer is now yours!"

"I am honoured, Maharaj," Agad Datta smiled, taking his rightful place in the kingdom.
