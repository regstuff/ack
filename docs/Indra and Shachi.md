# Indra and Shachi

Indra was the king of the Devas. He lived with his queen, Shachi in his celestial city, Amaravati. 

"Lord! We are so happy that I fear it might not last," Shachi told her husband one day. 

"Nothing untoward can happen to us, my dear," Indra reassured her. 

But there was cause for them to fear. At that very moment a son was born to Tvashta, Indra's enemy. The son, Trishiras, was born with three heads. "You shall recite the Vedas with one head... consume the divine Sura with the other and... watch the world with the third," Tvashta told his son. "My son, you shall become King of the Gods." 

Trishiras grew up fast. He became strong-willed and powerful. "I shall perform severe penances and destroy Indra, my father's enemy," he vowed. 

Indra heard of Trishiras and of his intense penance to destroy him. He sent for the Apsaras, the celestial maidens. "You must distract Trishiras and break his penance," Indra commanded. 

"We will tempt him with these fruits and flowers. I will dance. Your voice is sweet and enchanting. You must sing. We will lead him away from meditation into a life of pleasure," the Apsaras planned. 

Their charms were heavenly. But Trishiras was unmoved. The Apsaras returned to Indra, defeated. "We have failed. Trishiras is steadfast..." 

Shachi was worried. "Be careful! I fear for your safety," she told Indra. 

"At this rate he will usurp my throne. I must kill him," Indra decided. 

Indra threw his thunderbolt at Trishiras while he was praying. Trishiras fell dead, but his eyes remained unshut and staring. 

"Those eyes haunt me. I must get them closed. They drain me of all my strength," Indra shuddered. 

At that moment a woodcutter happened to pass by. "Please cut his heads off with your axe," Indra asked him. 

"His neck is too thick. My axe will become blunt," the woodcutter replied. 

"My thunderbolt shall give it an edge, fine and strong," Indra promised. The woodcutter then cut Trishiras' heads off. 

"Now I will have to atone for my sin. I have murdered a Brahman," Indra realized. He retreated to do penance for a long time. Finally, a divine voice spoke to him: "Arise, Indra. You have atoned for your sin by your penance." 

"Now I am free. I shall return to Amaravati," Indra rejoiced. 

Meanwhile Tvashta heard of Trishiras' death. He was deeply grieved and very angry. "I shall avenge my son's death. From the power of my prayers... shall rise an Asura," he vowed. 

Tvashta performed a sacrifice and created a terrifying Asura. "You are Vritra. Go forth and kill Indra." 

When Indra heard of Vritra, he prepared to meet the challenge. "I knew no good would come of killing Trishiras," Shachi worried. 

There was a terrible battle in which Vritra caught Indra between his teeth! "He has caught Indra. Quick! Hand me the Jrimbhaka Astra," a Deva shouted. 

The Jrimbhaka Astra (a weapon that causes yawning) found its mark. "I cannot control these yawns. That astra does it," Vritra groaned, opening his mouth wide to yawn. 

"This is the moment for me," Indra thought, making himself tiny and escaping from Vritra's mouth. 

He and his subjects went to Vishnu for advice. "Lord, help us," they pleaded. 

"Make peace with Vritra. Then watch for your chance to kill him. I will help you," Vishnu advised. 

Indra made peace with Vritra, but Vritra was wary. "Let us be friends! I admire you!" Indra said. 

"All right. But on my conditions. You must not kill me with any weapon—wet or dry, of wood or of stone... by day or by night!" Vritra laid out his terms. 

"I agree," Indra promised. 

One evening they were on the sea-shore. "I must kill him soon—but without using a weapon," Indra thought. "Foam cannot be considered a weapon. Vishnu has promised to help me. This twilight is neither night nor day. Foam is neither wet nor dry. Vishnu, help me!" 

Vishnu entered the foam, and when Indra threw it at Vritra, the Asura was killed immediately by the power of Vishnu within it. 

But as Indra had betrayed Vritra, and had previously murdered Trishiras, he was deeply ashamed to face the world. "I have killed Vritra. But I cannot show my face to anyone. I shall hide where none can find me," he wept. 

Indra ran away. All was darkness and chaos, on earth and in heaven. "There is no light anywhere." "Oh! Someone is beating me in the dark." "How dare you enter my house?" the gods and mortals stumbled in the dark. 

The Devas met in a council. "Without a king the heavens are in chaos. We cannot allow this anarchy to go on. Indra has deserted us. We must find another king," they decided. 

"Let us go to earth and invite King Nahusha to be our king. King Nahusha is a good and pious man," they agreed. 

The Devas went to King Nahusha's court. "Our king has run away. Please be our king," they requested. 

"I am a simple mortal. How can I be your king?" Nahusha hesitated. 

"We will invest you with our powers and strength. There is no one else. You must agree!" 

"Well. If you insist," Nahusha accepted. The Devas made Nahusha their king. 

In Amaravati, Nahusha enjoyed all the celestial pleasures. "Isn't this Indra's famous elephant? May I ride on it?" he asked. "I am a lucky man to own this." 

"This is your palace and garden. You may give your orders. We are here to obey them," the gods told him. 

"You are all so kind. I do not deserve all this," Nahusha said at first. But power soon corrupted his mind. 

"I am the Lord of the three worlds. The only thing I lack is a queen," Nahusha thought. "Go to Shachi. Tell her that I am the new king. She must now be my queen." 

When Shachi heard the message, she was shocked. "I will never be his queen. Better death," she wept, running to Brihaspati's house for protection. 

Nahusha was furious. "He is protecting my queen! I will have to punish Brihaspati!" The Devas tried to calm him down. "She is in grief. She will come round. You must be patient." 

So, prompted by Brihaspati, Shachi went with the Devas to Nahusha. "First let us search for my lord. If he cannot be found I shall become your queen," she proposed. 

"That is reasonable. I agree," Nahusha conceded. 

The Devas then went to Vishnu for advice on how to find Indra. Agni, the Fire-God, led the search. They found Indra hiding inside the stem of a lotus in a lake! He had made himself tiny to hide. 

"Indra! The Devas need you! You must come back!" they called. 

"I have done terrible things. I cannot come back," Indra refused. 

Shachi prayed to Upashruti, a Goddess of the night. "Oh mighty goddess! Help me!" Upashruti appeared and led Shachi into the dark night, over oceans and mountains, directly to the lake where Indra was hiding. 

Upashruti slit the lotus stem, and inside cowered a tiny Indra. "Lord! You do not know my plight," Shachi wept, explaining Nahusha's demands. 

"You must save me, or Nahusha will make me his queen tomorrow," she pleaded. 

Indra told her what she should do. "Go quickly and do as I have told you." 

The next morning Shachi went to Nahusha. "Indra has run away. I am ready to be your queen," she announced. 

"I expected this," Nahusha smiled greedily. 

"But I have a special request to make. You should come to me in a palanquin borne by the seven sages," Shachi demanded. 

"That is easy. I am their king. I will come this evening," Nahusha agreed arrogantly. "Tell the seven sages to be ready to carry my palanquin. I shall put on my finest clothes and jewels." 

Nahusha set out in the palanquin borne by the seven venerable sages. 

Meanwhile Shachi went to Brihaspati's house. "I have done my part," she said. 

"Then I shall send for Indra," Brihaspati replied. He invoked Agni in a sacrifice. "Agni! You must find Indra and bring him here, at once." Agni called the Devas, and they brought Indra back. 

At that moment Nahusha was on his way to Shachi's palace. "Lazy fellows! How slow they are!" he yelled at the sages carrying his palanquin. "It is all the fault of Agastya. Because he is short he cannot keep pace with the others." 

"I shall goad him on," Nahusha thought, and he kicked Sage Agastya! "Sarpa! Sarpa! (Move on! Move on!)" he shouted. 

When Nahusha kicked him, Agastya became angry. "How dare you! Sarpo Bhava! (Become a snake!) My curse will turn you into a snake," Agastya proclaimed. 

Instantly, Nahusha was transformed into a snake and fell from the heavens. 

Just then Agastya came hurrying up to the Devas. "We are free at last. Nahusha is now a mere snake," he announced. 

"Only because of Shachi's loyalty to me!" Indra said gratefully. 

The Devas and sages welcomed Indra back to his throne, and he was reunited with his devoted wife, Shachi.
