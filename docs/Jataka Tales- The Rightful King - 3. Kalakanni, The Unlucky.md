# Kalakanni, The Unlucky

During the reign of Brahmadatta in Varanasi, there lived a prosperous merchant called Anathapindika. 

One day, a servant announced, "Sir! A man called Kalakanni is here to see you!" 

"But, sir! He looks very poor and wretched!" the servant added. 

"Never mind! It must be my old friend. After all, not many people have a name like his! Show him in!" 

Kalakanni walked in hesitantly. "Ah! It is you. How good to meet you after all these years!" Anathapindika welcomed him warmly. 

"But why do you look so down and out?" 

"My luck, I guess. Whoever chose my name was right. I am really unlucky. Truly, I have been so miserable," Kalakanni sighed. 

"Hush! Don't ever say that! Not any more! Now you will stay with me and share my life. Those days we spent together at the gurukula were the happiest of my life." 

"I have a big business here. I need a trustworthy friend like you to help me." And Kalakanni helped Anathapindika in his work. 

Anathapindika's business associates were perplexed. "How can you trust a stranger so much?" 

"He is no stranger! He is my childhood friend." 

"But you have not seen him for years! And what an inauspicious name—Kalakanni (Unlucky). Why, even uttering such a name repeatedly might bring you bad luck." 

"Bah! I don't believe in such things," Anathapindika dismissed their fears. "They say that a man who walks seven steps with you becomes a friend. After a fortnight of being together, he is like a family member..." 

"I don't know how you can stand an ugly man all day. And he is a real gem, whatever be his name," Anathapindika defended his friend. 

After a few months, Anathapindika told Kalakanni, "I'll be away for a few days. We are going to our native place for a wedding." 

"But who will look after your house?" 

"Oh! Kalakanni will be there. You mean you will trust him with all your valuable belongings? I can trust him with my life." 

"Here are the keys! Kalakanni! Take care." 

"Don't worry master! I will guard your house day and night," Kalakanni promised. 

Some robbers saw Anathapindika leave. "Ah! The rich Seth Anathapindika is leaving town. Let's burgle his house tonight. The servants in the house are feeble and old. They will not be able to resist us." 

By chance Kalakanni overheard the conversation. "Oh! How can I fight these stout rogues? But what if I can fool the thieves?" 

At dusk, he gathered the servants together. "Here! You blow loudly on the conch shell. You must play on the drum. You have a sweet voice, you must sing. The rest of us will clap with the rhythm. Make as much noise as you can." 

At night, when the dacoits arrived, they heard the loud din. "AAA THUMP! THUMP! CLAP CLAP." 

"It sounds as if there's a celebration going on. Why, there must be a real crowd inside. The Seth must have returned. Let's run before anyone sees us," the robbers panicked and fled. 

The next morning, the servants found footprints outside. "Thieves must have got in... but were scared off by our din." 

When Anathapindika returned, he was told about Kalakanni's clever trick. "Of course, he is! Now do you realise why I value him so much?" Anathapindika smiled proudly at his friends.
