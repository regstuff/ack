# The Churning of the Ocean

Long ago, while roaming the earth, the short-tempered sage Durvasa met a celestial nymph carrying a garland of heavenly flowers. Heady with their intoxicating fragrance, the sage requested the garland, and the nymph gladly presented it to him.

As Durvasa walked on, he met Indra, King of the Devas, riding upon his grand four-tusked white elephant, Airavata. Desiring to honor the King of Heaven, Durvasa presented the sweet-smelling garland to Indra.

Indra took the garland casually and draped it over Airavata's forehead. Intoxicated by the perfume, the elephant plucked the garland with its trunk, threw it onto the dirt, and trampled it underfoot.

Seeing the sacred garland of Sri—the Goddess of Fortune—disrespected and trampled, Durvasa flew into a terrible rage. "O arrogant Indra!" Durvasa roared. "Just as you cast this divine garland to the ground, so shall your power, prosperity, and glory crumble! All the Devas shall lose their vigor!"

Instantly, the curse took effect. Plants withered, cool streams dried up, and the Devas felt their strength drain away. Taking advantage of their weakness, the Asuras (demons), led by King Bali, attacked the heavens. Powerless to resist, the Devas were routed and fled to Mount Meru to seek Lord Brahma's aid.

Brahma meditated and led the weakened gods to Vaikuntha, the abode of Lord Vishnu. The Devas bowed before Vishnu's radiant light, pleading, "O All-Knowing Lord, help us! We are powerless against the Asuras."

Lord Vishnu looked upon them with compassion. "Listen carefully. For the moment, you must make peace with your enemies, the Asuras. Join hands with them to churn the Ocean of Milk to extract *Amrita*, the Nectar of Immortality. Once you drink the Nectar, you will become immortal and fear nothing."

"But Lord," Indra asked, "will not the Asuras take the Nectar for themselves?"

"Leave that to me," Vishnu assured them. "Make peace with them now."

Indra traveled to King Bali's court in the netherworld. Surprised by the unarmed gods, Bali listened as Indra proposed a joint venture to churn the Ocean of Milk and share the Nectar of Immortality. Recognizing the immense value of the prize, Bali and the Asura leaders—Shambara, Pauloma, and Kalakeya—agreed to a temporary truce.

Together, the Devas and Asuras gathered on the shores of the Ocean of Milk, casting sacred medicinal herbs into the waters. To serve as a churning rod, they uprooted the colossal Mount Mandara, which extended eleven thousand yojanas above the earth.

However, as the combined armies carried the massive mountain toward the sea, they collapsed under its crushing weight. Seeing many gods and demons crushed beneath the peak, Lord Vishnu arrived on Garuda. With a single healing glance, he revived the injured warriors, effortlessly lifted Mount Mandara with one hand, placed it on Garuda's back, and transported it to the ocean.

To act as the churning rope, they invited Vasuki, King of the Serpents, promising him a share of the Nectar. Vasuki agreed and coiled his long body around Mount Mandara.

As they prepared to churn, Indra and the Devas walked toward Vasuki's head. But the Asuras grew suspicious and bellowed, "We are mighty warriors! We will not hold the tail of a snake! We demand to hold the head!"

Indra hid a smile and yielded politely. "As you wish, friends. We shall hold the tail."

With the Asuras holding Vasuki's head and the Devas holding his tail, the great churning began. But as Mount Mandara whirled, there was no support beneath its immense weight, and the mountain began to sink into the ocean bed.

Seeing their distress, Lord Vishnu assumed his second avatar, **Kurma**—a gigantic turtle as vast as an island. Diving to the ocean floor, Lord Vishnu supported the base of Mount Mandara upon his impenetrable shell, enabling the churning to resume with furious speed.

As the mountain whirled, Vasuki's thousand mouths exhaled clouds of poisonous fire and smoke, engulfing the Asuras near his head. Lord Vishnu sent down cool, refreshing rain clouds over the Devas at the tail to keep them energized.

Suddenly, a thick, terrifying cloud of black poison—*Halahala*—emerged from the ocean depths, threatening to incinerate all life in the universe. Panicked, the Devas and Asuras cried out to Lord Shiva on Mount Kailash.

Hearing their prayers, Lord Shiva gathered the deadly poison into the palm of his hand and swallowed it. To prevent the poison from harming his body, his consort Parvati pressed his throat, holding the poison there. The lethal venom turned Shiva's neck bright blue, earning him the eternal title **Neelakantha** ("The Blue-Throated Lord").

With the poison cleared, the churning resumed, and the ocean began to yield its sacred treasures:
1. *Surabhi* (Kamadhenu), the divine wish-fulfilling cow, given to the sages.
2. *Uchchhaishrava*, the flying white horse, taken by Bali.
3. *Airavata*, the majestic four-tusked white elephant, taken by Indra.
4. The *Apsaras*, celestial maidens who danced for the gods.
5. *Parijata*, the celestial flower tree.
6. *Varuni*, the goddess of wine.
7. *Shree* (Goddess Lakshmi), the Goddess of Fortune, emerging radiant from the golden waves. As holy rivers poured water over her from golden jars, she looked upon all the gods and chose Lord Vishnu as her eternal consort, placing the *Vaijayanti* garland around his neck.

Finally, Dhanvantari, the divine physician, emerged from the churning waters holding a golden vessel containing the precious *Amrita*.

At the sight of the Nectar, the Asuras roared, pushed Dhanvantari aside, and snatched the golden pot by force, fleeing to the shore.

As the Asuras began fighting among themselves over who should drink first, Lord Vishnu assumed the form of **Mohini**—a maiden of bewitching, matchless beauty. 

Mesmerized by her enchanting smile, the Asuras forgot their quarrel and ran to her. "O beautiful lady!" Bali pleaded. "Please take this pot and distribute the Nectar equally among us!"

Mohini smiled playfully. "I am only a woman, and women are fickle. How can you trust me?"

"We trust you completely!" the Asuras declared. "Promise to abide by your decision!"

Mohini seated the Devas and Asuras in two parallel rows. She began serving the Nectar to the row of Devas first, flashing captivating glances at the Asuras to keep them spellbound.

Noticing that the Nectar was being given only to the gods, a clever Asura named Rahu disguised himself as a Deva and slipped into the row between Surya (the Sun God) and Soma (the Moon God). 

As Rahu raised the cup and took a sip, Surya and Soma recognized him and alerted Lord Vishnu. Instantly, Vishnu launched his Sudarshana Chakra, severing Rahu's head just as the Nectar passed his throat! The immortal severed head flew into the sky, where it remains as a demon node, periodically attempting to swallow the Sun and Moon, causing eclipses.

Realizing they had been duped, the Asuras drew their weapons and attacked. But the Devas, invigorated by the Nectar of Immortality, fought with invincible strength. Lord Vishnu joined the battle with his Sudarshana Chakra, routing the demon host and driving them deep into the netherworld.

With peace restored to the universe, the victorious Devas placed Mount Mandara back in its original sanctuary and returned to the heavens in joy and glory.
