# The Rat and the Cat

In the depths of a forest, there was a large banyan tree. In a hole, at the foot of this tree, lived a rat named Palita. Amidst the branches of the same tree there dwelt a cat called Lomasha, and Palita was in constant fear of him. 

"Lomasha must still be up there. I had better stay here till he leaves," Palita would tremble in his hole. "Ah! I can see Lomasha now! Every day he devours so many of those poor birds!" 

One day, a hunter came to the forest. Each day, he would spread out his net at sunset and come back at dawn the next day to check if an animal had been trapped. 

Lomasha was careful to keep away from the net. But, one day, he made a mistake. "Oh, this is terrible! The more I struggle, the more firmly I am trapped!" the cat wailed, caught in the hunter's snare. 

With Lomasha firmly in the net, Palita became very bold. Enjoying his new-found freedom, Palita jumped about near the very snare which held the cat prisoner. "What joy! This is the greatest day of my life! Ha! Ha! Dear Lomasha! You have no idea how happy I am!" 

But his joy was short-lived. Suddenly his eye fell upon a new danger—the arrival of a terrible foe! "Oh! A mongoose!" 

He looked around for a way to escape and got another shock—there was an owl on the tree! "What shall I do? If I stay here, that owl will tear me to pieces with his sharp beak... but if I try to run away the mongoose will swallow me up! On every side there is danger! Death itself is staring me in the face!" 

Turning to the cat, he talked to him with friendly concern. "Dear Lomasha, I wish to help you. If you agree to do the same for me, I will release you from your present plight. Will you listen to me?" 

"Do you really mean to help me? A short while ago you were rejoicing at my misfortune!" the cat sneered. 

"Things are different now. I thought of you as my only enemy but I had forgotten about the mongoose and the owl. Look, there they are, waiting to pounce upon me! I thought I was lucky when my worst enemy, Lomasha, was ensnared. But, in a way, he protected me from the others by his very presence. And now he needs me and I need him." 

"Friend, the answer is very simple: let me lie crouched under your body till they leave!" Palita proposed. 

Of course, Lomasha agreed. Palita quickly snuggled up to the cat. The cat did not move, but lay very still. "Isn't it extraordinary? They don't even think of Palita as a rat! He is my saviour!" Lomasha thought. 

"What a strange turn of events! I actually feel safe under the body of Lomasha, the cat!" Palita marvelled. 

Meanwhile, the mongoose had his eyes fixed on Palita. "A rat in the arms of a cat! I've never seen anything like it in my life! Anyway, I can't wait here forever. I'll find something else to eat!" He turned and left. Pretty soon the owl, too, gave up and flew away. 

After this, Palita began to cut the strings of the net with his sharp teeth. But Palita, in accordance with his own plans, worked very slowly. 

"Dear friend, don't waste time. Do cut these strings quickly! It will soon be dawn and the hunter will be here!" Lomasha pleaded. 

"Patience! All in good time!" Palita replied. 

Palita continued to work slowly while Lomasha waited trembling with fear. When, at last, the sun rose in the east, Palita saw the hunter approaching. "See, the hunter is approaching! Now I will set you free!" 

Palita cut the last strings that held Lomasha, just as the hunter reached the scene. The cat bounded away while the rat quickly leaped to his hole. 

When the puzzled hunter had gone away, the cat addressed his new-found friend. "Oh, Palita, I am very grateful to you for saving my life! Let there be peace between us always! I will ask all my kinsmen and friends not to hurt you." 

But the rat was wise. "You are mistaken, O Lomasha! There are times when self-interest can make a foe a friend. But sooner or later they will become enemies. It is possible you are pretending to be my friend—just to catch me the more easily! No, Lomasha, go your way—and keep your distance from the evil hunter!" 

The cat shivered on hearing the word 'hunter' and made haste to run away.
