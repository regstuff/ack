# The Day the Earth Broke Into Two

One day a rabbit was relaxing under a bilva tree. When a strange thought struck him. "I wonder what will happen if the earth breaks into two?" 

Just then, a bilva fruit fell from the tree with a loud "THUD", hitting a dry palm leaf. 

"Wh... what is that?" the terrified rabbit gasped. "Is the earth really going to break? I must run away to a safe place!" 

He hopped up and ran as fast as his legs could carry him. The second rabbit saw him and followed. "What's the matter? Where are you going?" 

"Don't ask me, brother. Just run for your life! Oh! The earth is about to break!" the first rabbit cried. 

Very soon, a horde of frightened rabbits were running. Then they met a deer. "What has happened? Why the panic? I'll ask them." Learning that the earth was going to break, the deer also joined the rabbits. 

Pigs, tigers, elephants, and other animals joined the stampede. Very soon a panic-stricken troupe was fleeing through the forest. 

Then a lion came by. "What's going on here?" 

"Oh, don't you know? The earth is breaking!" an animal cried. 

"Hmm.... But that is impossible!" the wise lion thought. He climbed to the top of a hill and roared. "GRR-GRR-GRR!" 

The animals froze in their tracks. "Why are you running? What is the matter?" the lion demanded. 

"The earth is breaking, O mighty one! We must go away from here!" 

"Who saw this happen?" the lion asked. The elephant blamed the tiger, the tiger blamed the panther, until finally, the rabbit was brought to the lion. 

"Did you really see the earth breaking, little one?" 

"Yes, my lord. I saw it with my own eyes. I was just lying down there wondering what I would do if the earth were to break, when suddenly... it happened! I heard a deafening noise and fled." 

"A palm grove... a bilva tree... Hmm.... I am sure he has made a mistake," the lion deduced. "I'll go with the rabbit and find out what really happened. Wait here till I come back." 

The lion took the trembling rabbit on his back and ran to the palm grove. "Show me the place where you heard the noise. Don't be afraid. I'm here to protect you." 

"That's the bilva tree where I heard the thundering noise!" the rabbit pointed. 

The lion looked closely at the palm leaves underneath the bilva tree. "Ah! Just as I thought! The sound of the fruit falling on dry leaves frightened the rabbit and he thought that the earth was breaking." 

Returning to the waiting animals, the lion told them what he had discovered. "Friends, you did not check if this was true. Blindly relying on a false rumour, you would have paid a heavy price today."
