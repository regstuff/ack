# The Tiger and the Jackal

Deep in the jungles of the Himalayas, there was a jackal who lived the life of a recluse. This jackal was compassionate and truthful, whereas his companions were cruel and rapacious. 

"What strange behaviour is this? One would think he was an ascetic practising austerities!" the other jackals sneered. "And we jackals relish flesh." 

"Look, friend, the way in which you live won't do! We don't like it!" they confronted him. "All of us will give you meat. You don't have to live like an ascetic!" 

"Thank you for your kind concern, but I am quite happy living on fruit," he replied gently. 

"Such behaviour is perverse! Every one must remain true to his nature! Let's go and give him a bit of advice," they muttered. "Ha! Do you imagine that by imitating a higher caste you can change your position in society?" 

"It's true I am low-born but I am determined that my conduct shall not be low," the righteous jackal stood his ground. 

"So you believe it is up to you to decide on your conduct? Don't you realise it has all been laid down for us generations ago? We jackals have strictly followed the same rules as our fathers!" 

"Friends, since we are responsible for our actions does it not follow that we have a choice over our actions? I do not wish to lead your kind of life. It is evil. It leads to discontent and temptation." 

"Pah! It's no use talking to him! Let's leave him alone!" the jackals gave up. 

The Tiger who was the King of the Forest had been eavesdropping. He drew near as soon as the others had left. "O righteous person! I heard every word you uttered. I respect you for such honourable sentiments! He seems to be a truly learned person of blameless conduct. Most unusual for a jackal! He is worthy of respect!" 

"Please come to my court and be my Chief Minister," the Tiger offered. 

"I thank you for your offer, O mighty king, but... I do not want luxury such as court life affords. I have never served another. I am quite happy where I am," the jackal politely declined. 

"However, if you still wish to appoint me, I will obey—provided you treat my advice with respect and allow me to give it to you in private," the jackal added. 

"I gladly agree to your conditions!" the Tiger accepted. 

So the jackal went to the court and was treated by the king with great respect. But his strict rules angered the corrupt ministers. "He does not always get work because the jackal in charge wants a bribe from him!" the jackal would ensure justice. But those who were exploiting the poor animals were very angry. 

"That minister is one of us. Why should he side with the common people?" the corrupt jackals grumbled. They plotted against him. 

They stole the King's meat and placed it in the Chief Minister's room. "Now look for the ladies," they whispered. 

"He has done an unforgivable thing. He has set the hare free and sent our good friend, the magistrate, to jail!" they complained to the king. 

"Look at that fresh piece of meat laid out for the king! Take it away at once and put it in the Chief Minister's room! Leave the rest to me!" the leader of the corrupt jackals plotted. 

Minutes later, the king came in. "Who has dared to touch my plate before I have eaten? SPEAK UP!" the Tiger roared. 

"It was the Chief Minister. We saw him take the meat away to his rooms," the corrupt jackals lied. 

"It can't be true!" the Tiger roared. 

"If you will come with us, your majesty, we will show you where it is!" 

There, in the Chief Minister's room, was the meat, placed by the rascally jackals! 

"How can I trust anyone ever again? I thought he was honest but the fact is, he is only another jackal! I can't bear to be made a fool of like this! The Chief Minister shall be executed at once!" the Tiger commanded. 

Soon after, the king's mother heard about it and hurried to speak to her son. "This cannot be true! I am certain the Chief Minister is innocent, the victim of some evil plot! I saw it with my own eyes!" 

"The Chief Minister is innocent! To plot his downfall, my friends lied about him to you!" she explained. "You trusted him too much, O king! We dared not tell you the truth about him before!" 

The king realized his mistake. He sent for the Chief Minister. "Good jackal, I was wrong to make so hasty a judgement against you. Please resume your duties." 

"O king, first you bestowed upon me the highest honours of the land. Then you treated me as your enemy. I have wronged you. I should have judged by your actions which have always been just. Trust once lost cannot be regained. Such a scar permanently mars relationships. Please give me your permission to go away," the jackal replied sadly. 

He then went away and spent the rest of his days in solitude in the forest.
