# The Golden Antelope

Long ago, there lived a king in Varanasi who had a royal gardener named Sanjaya. Every day, a wild antelope came from the forest to graze in the lush royal garden. 

"Poor antelope!" Sanjaya would think as the creature bolted. "Every time he sees me, he runs away in fright."

But as time went by, the antelope became accustomed to seeing Sanjaya quietly tending the plants and stopped running away. 

One day, while Sanjaya was taking a basket of fresh fruit and flowers to the palace, the king stopped him. "Have you noticed anything strange in the garden, Sanjaya?" the king asked. 

"Nothing, your majesty, except that a wild antelope comes to graze there from time to time," Sanjaya reported. 

The king was intrigued. "Do you think you could catch him?" 

"Oh yes, your majesty," Sanjaya smiled. "If I had a little honey, I could bring him right into your palace." 

The king immediately ordered some honey to be given to Sanjaya. 

Armed with a pot of honey, Sanjaya returned to the garden. "I shall coat the grass where he grazes with honey, then hide myself and see what happens," he planned. He carefully spread the honey on a patch of grass and hid behind a tree. 

Soon, along came the antelope. He took a bite of the grass and his eyes widened. "Hmm. This grass tastes exceptionally sweet today!" he thought, happily munching away. 

From behind the tree, Sanjaya chuckled silently. "So my plan has worked. Now for the second stage. Hee hee." 

The antelope became so ensnared by the taste of the sweetened grass that he refused to eat anywhere else. Knowing this, Sanjaya decided to escalate his plan. "Hmm. Now to show myself gradually," he thought. 

The next day, Sanjaya stepped out into the open while the antelope was eating. At first, the antelope was startled at his appearance. "Huh?" he snorted, ready to run. But Sanjaya held out a handful of the honey-coated grass. 

Soon, the antelope's lust for the sweet taste overcame his natural fear, and he was eating straight from Sanjaya's hand. 

Day by day, Sanjaya lured the antelope closer and closer to the palace gates using the sweet grass. "He hasn't even noticed we are near the palace," Sanjaya thought. "Now I must lead him towards the palace." 

He dropped a trail of sweetened grass leading right into the grand hall of the palace. The oblivious antelope followed the trail inside. Once the animal was fully inside, Sanjaya quickly shut the heavy wooden door behind him. *Slam!* 

Trapped! At the sight of so many palace guards and courtiers, the antelope realized his mistake. Trembling with sheer fright, he dashed frantically to and fro, looking for an escape. 

Hearing the commotion, the king came down from his chamber. Seeing the panicked, terrified animal, the king's heart filled with pity. "Antelopes are so timid that they never revisit a place for at least a week if they have seen a man there," the king observed. "Sometimes they never go back again." 

He looked at the honey grass scattered on the floor. "But this antelope was carried away by the taste of the sweetened grass and completely forgot himself and his natural instincts," the king said thoughtfully. "Surely there is nothing viler in the world than the lust of taste. By falling into this trap, you have taught us all a valuable lesson about greed."

The king then ordered the doors to be opened. "Go my friend," the king said softly. "You are free to go wherever you please." 

With a leap, the antelope rushed out of the palace and bounded back into the safety of the forest, never to be lured by sweet tastes again.
