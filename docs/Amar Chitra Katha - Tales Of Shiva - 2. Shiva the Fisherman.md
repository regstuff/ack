# Shiva the Fisherman

Once in Mount Kailasa, Lord Shiva started expounding the deep, complex mysteries of the Vedas to his wife, Goddess Parvati, who was listening attentively. 

Years passed by without a single break. Shiva continued his cosmic lecture, but gradually, in spite of her very best efforts, Parvati's attention began to flag, and her eyes wandered. 

Noticing her distraction, Shiva was deeply annoyed. 

"The Vedas are not for you!" Shiva scolded her angrily. "Since you are no better than a common fisherwoman... may you be born as one!"

Instantly, before Shiva could take back his harsh words, Parvati vanished from Kailasa. 

The very next moment, Shiva clutched his head in absolute grief. "What have I done!" he wept. "In thoughtless haste have I cast away one whose love for me was peerless."

Shiva's depressed state of mind did not escape Nandi, his trusted, bull-headed servant. *Now my master will know no peace until Mother Parvati returns,* Nandi realized. 

Meanwhile, down on Earth, Parvati had already taken the form of an adorable human infant. She lay crying softly under a Punnai tree near the coast, where she was soon found by the chief of the Paravars, a local clan of fishermen. 

"What a lovely child! No doubt it is God's gift to me," the chief smiled, picking her up gently. "I'll call her Parvati."

Little Parvati grew up happily by the sea. She used to go with her foster father whenever he went fishing, and as she grew older, she even learnt to perfectly row the heavy wooden boats. 

"You are a real help to me, Parvati," her proud father smiled. 

She grew into a stunningly beautiful young woman, beloved by all the fishermen. "A pretty girl! Strong and clever too! Lucky is the man who weds her!" they would whisper as she walked by the docks. 

Meanwhile, back at Kailasa, Nandi approached his brooding master. "My Lord, why don't you go down to earth and claim her?" 

"How can I, Nandi?" Shiva sighed miserably. "She is a fisherwoman now. She will now marry a fisherman."

Nandi smiled to himself. *Then I will see to it that you are forced to become a fisherman!*

Assuming the form of a massive, terrifying shark, Nandi plunged into the ocean and headed straight for the coast where the Paravars lived. 

"A shark!" the fishermen screamed as the giant beast breached the surface. "Quick! We must get it!"

But before the fishermen could even cast their nets, the shark attacked, violently smashing their small wooden boats to splinters and throwing the men into the churning water. 

As the days went by, the massive shark became a regular menace, destroying boats and tearing expensive fishing nets to shreds. 

"Our nets are torn, and our boats in pieces! What shall we do?" the terrified fishermen wept. 

At last, the desperate chief of the Paravars came up with an award. He stood before his tribe. "I will offer the hand of my beautiful daughter, Parvati, to the one who captures the shark!" he announced. 

Many a brave young man took to the sea with heavy nets, hoping to win the chief's daughter... but the shark effortlessly destroyed their boats, and they all failed. 

Having lost all hope, the desperate Paravars at last sought divine help. The chief fell to his knees before a Shiva Linga at the village temple. "O compassionate one, save us from the shark," he prayed. 

The chief's daughter, Parvati, also knelt and prayed with all her heart. "Lord, come to our rescue. Don't fail us in the hour of need."

Up in Kailasa, Lord Shiva heard her sweet voice. He immediately transformed himself, appearing before the chief of the Paravars as a handsome, muscular young fisherman. 

"I have come to catch the shark," Shiva announced. 

"Our tribe will be indebted to you forever, if you succeed," the chief said, grasping his hands in relief. 

Net in hand, Shiva waded confidently down into the roaring sea. When he cast his net, the terrifying shark, who was none other than his loyal servant Nandi, immediately surrendered himself, swimming peacefully into the ropes. 

As Shiva effortlessly dragged the massive, subdued shark ashore, the entire village erupted in joyous cheers. 

"You are our saviour!" the chief cried. 

Parvati looked at the handsome young fisherman, her heart fluttering. *How fortunate for me that this brave man has caught the shark!* she thought, blushing deeply. 

The chief embraced Shiva. "Welcome to our tribe, young man. By catching the great fish you have become a great fisherman. I am indeed glad to hear that. Accept my daughter as your wife."

And so, Lord Shiva, disguised as a fisherman, married his beloved Parvati, the fisherwoman. As the celebrations began, Nandi happily assumed his true, divine form and joyously carried the reunited couple back to their eternal home in Kailasa.
