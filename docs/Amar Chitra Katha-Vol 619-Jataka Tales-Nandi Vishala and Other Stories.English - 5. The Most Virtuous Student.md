# The Most Virtuous Student

In ancient Varanasi, there once lived a highly renowned and deeply respected scholar who had a beautiful grown-up daughter. 

He was a master teacher and had a massive number of young students under his care. One day, a brilliant idea struck him. 

"My daughter is ready for marriage," he thought to himself. "I will put my students through a secret test to find out which is the absolute most virtuous of them all."

The very next day, he gathered all his hundreds of students under the shade of a large banyan tree. 

"My dear boys," the scholar sighed heavily, pretending to be in deep distress. "I cannot afford the expensive clothes and fine ornaments required for my daughter's marriage. Will you, my loyal students, help me out by stealing these things for me?"

The students gasped in shock. 

"But no one should see you stealing," the teacher warned strictly. "It should be done in the absolute strictest secrecy. Only then will I accept what you bring."

From that day onwards, eager to please their beloved master, the students secretly stole gold, silks, and valuables from their own families and friends. They brought the stolen goods secretly to their teacher under the cover of night. 

"I must keep what each one brings in separate lots," the teacher thought as he received the stolen goods, "so that they can be easily returned to their rightful owners later."

A few days later, the teacher was walking through the grounds when he saw one of his absolute favourite students sitting alone, looking rather dejected. 

"My son, you are the only one out of hundreds who has not brought me anything!" the teacher noted. 

"Yes, master," the student sighed, looking at the ground. 

"Couldn't you steal even a small ring?" the teacher asked, testing him. 

"No, master. It's not that."

"Then what is it?" the teacher pressed. "You know you'll not accept anything that is not taken perfectly secretly. But I find that there is no such thing as absolute secrecy in wrong-doing."

"Why do you say that?" the teacher asked, his eyes gleaming. 

"Because... because, even when there is absolutely no other person in sight, I cannot hide from myself!" the young student declared firmly. "My conscience would always know I am a thief."

The teacher was absolutely overjoyed. 

"I have found the perfect boy for my daughter!" he cheered, hugging the young man. "Of all my hundreds of students, he is the only one who is truly virtuous!"

Then, he immediately sent for all the other boys. He stood before the massive pile of stolen goods and pointed at it sternly. 

"Stealing, even for a supposedly good cause, is deeply wrong," the teacher announced. "You have all completely failed the test of virtue. You must now return all that you have stolen to their rightful owners at once."

Then, proudly adorning his beautiful daughter with her own pure jewels, the wise scholar happily gave her in marriage to his most virtuous, honest student.
