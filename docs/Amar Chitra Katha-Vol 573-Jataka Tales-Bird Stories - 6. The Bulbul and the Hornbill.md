# The Bulbul and the Hornbill

The large, fierce Hornbill was once the mighty King of the Birds. He had a massive beak and a terrifying presence. But he was a cruel tyrant, and he used to ruthlessly kill smaller birds if they made even the slightest mistake. 

So one day, all the smaller birds got together and decided that they simply must have a new king to save themselves from the Hornbill's wrath. After a long discussion, their choice fell on the tiny, beautiful Bulbul. 

"He has a regal appearance..." one bird suggested. 

"...and he could not hurt anyone even if he wanted to," another added happily. 

"But how do we break the news to the Hornbill?" a parrot asked nervously. "He won't be pleased."

The clever little Woodpecker stepped forward. "I have an idea. I'll need your help, Woodpecker. Come with me."

Sometime later, the birds approached the fierce Hornbill. 

"O King, we feel that you should undergo a test and prove your worth as our ruler," they announced. "You will have to sit on a thick branch and break it with your sheer weight."

The Hornbill glared at them. "If I don't succeed, whoever does shall be deemed worthier of ruling us, I suppose?"

"Tell me which branch I should sit on," the Hornbill scoffed arrogantly. 

"That's the branch," they pointed to a thick, sturdy-looking limb high up in a tree. 

"If I cannot break the branch, who can?" the massive Hornbill sneered. He flew up high into the air and landed on the branch with all his incredible might. 

*Thud.* 

But the thick branch did not break. It didn't even bend. 

"Remember, anyone who enters the contest must sit on an equally thick branch," the Hornbill demanded angrily. 

"The Bulbul is the next contestant," the birds announced. 

The Hornbill laughed. "Is the branch over there thick enough?"

"That branch is even thicker than this," they pointed out. 

"If the Bulbul can break that branch, he certainly deserves to be King," the Hornbill laughed, assuming it was impossible for the tiny bird. 

"Go on!" the flock cheered. 

The tiny Bulbul flew up and gracefully landed on the thick branch. 

*CRACK!* 

The massive branch instantly snapped cleanly in two under the Bulbul's tiny feet! 

"He... he has broken the branch!" the Hornbill gasped in utter disbelief. Humiliated and acknowledging his total defeat, the fierce Hornbill flew away forever. 

And so, the gentle little Bulbul became the new King of the Birds. 

What the arrogant Hornbill didn't know was that the thick branch on which the Bulbul was to sit had been secretly, almost completely bored through by the clever Woodpecker!
