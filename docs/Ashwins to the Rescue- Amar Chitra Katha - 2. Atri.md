# Atri

Sage Atri lived with his family on the outskirts of a dense forest. 

One day, his sons came to him with alarming news. "Father, I heard the Dasyus have moved into the forest."

"How does that concern me?" Atri asked calmly, tending to a sapling.

"You must not go about in the forest anymore," his son urged.

"Why not? Surely the forest is large enough for all of us," the sage reasoned.

His sons were not convinced. "What if the Dasyus attack him?" one whispered. "I am worried. Let us follow him at a distance."

Later, Atri walked on and on till he reached the thickest part of the forest. He looked up at a giant tree. "What a tall tree! I wonder what the earth looks like from above!"

Curious, Atri began to climb the tree. He went higher and higher. At last, he reached the top. "What a magnificent view! The earth seems to expand when seen from such a height. I wonder how it would look when seen from the skies." Inspired by the vastness, he sat on a high branch and began to meditate on the idea. 

Soon it was night. Atri was still lost in meditation. When he finally opened his eyes to the dawn, he marveled, "What a glorious sight!" 

Atri slid down the tree, walked to a stream near by, had his bath, and prayed to the sun. Even as he opened his eyes from his prayer, he was suddenly grabbed. "Aa..." he cried out. The Dasyus had got him.

As they dragged him away, Atri cried out the names of his favourite deities: "O Ashvins... O Ashvins..."

His sons, who had been following him at a distance, heard him. "Father is in trouble!" they yelled. The boys ran as fast as their legs could carry them to help their father. But in their haste, they suddenly tripped and fell, right into a trap laid by the Dasyus. 

"I knew you were anxious to join your father," the Dasyu chief mocked them.

Atri and his sons were bound and dragged to a massive tree with a hollow trunk. "You will now go on a long journey," the chief announced. "Show him the way."

Atri was thrown violently into the deep, dark hollow of the tree. "Father!" his sons cried out as they were thrown in after him.

Inside the pitch-black abyss, Atri wondered, "Is this infinity... or am I in the womb of mother earth?" 

The next moment he heard noises from above and looked up. He could see his sons tumbling down. "My sons!"

"Father!" they cried as they landed beside him. "Father, they are going to set this place on fire. I heard them say so."

"What shall we do?" they asked in panic.

"Let us pray to the Ashvins!" Atri said steadfastly. "O Ashvins! Come to our rescue! Now this very moment! O Ashvins, save the boys." 

Smoke began to pour down into the hollow. The boys started coughing. "Aa-a-ah!" 

"You must come. Now!" Atri prayed fervently.

Their call was heard. The Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen, came riding on their celestial chariot drawn by two swift asses. 

They swept through the forest, dispensing justice. Those Dasyus who dared to put up a fight fell, while the wiser ones fled in terror as the Ashvins advanced in their chariot. 

The twins quickly rescued Atri and his sons from the burning tree. "We salute you, O twins," Atri and his sons bowed deeply in gratitude. 

Atri's unwavering faith and courage had paid off. He lost neither his freedom nor his life. Overjoyed and relieved, they refreshed themselves at the stream. "Water! The Ashvins are here!"
