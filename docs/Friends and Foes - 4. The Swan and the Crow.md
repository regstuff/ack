# The Swan and the Crow

There once lived a crow who was the favourite of three boys. Every day they fed him with the remnants of their food. 

"Come, lovely crow, we won't harm you! Eat! Eat!" the boys would call out. 

"Doesn't he look lovely? Look at his glossy wings!" they praised. 

The admiration of the children filled the crow with pride. "...And he believed himself to be the handsomest, cleverest bird in the land." 

Just then he spotted a flock of swans gliding across the sky. ...And the sight irritated him! 

"Strangers here! This beach is mine, I won't tolerate any ugly birds in my territory!" the crow cawed loudly. 

The leader of these swans heard this as he was gliding by. "Ugly, my dear sir! Ugly! How can you say such a thing! Nobody would possibly dispute a swan's beauty! Besides we are the world's best fliers!" 

"What nonsense! All you can do is flutter in the breeze like a torn ribbon..." the crow sneered. "...Whereas I...! Look! Just see what I can do as a flier!" 

He turned several times in the air like an acrobat. He made zig-zag patterns in the air and... even flew upside down for a bit. 

"Good Lord, what a clumsy fool!" the swan laughed. 

"Those are just tricks! Any circus clown could do them! I know only one kind of flying—and I can beat you at that!" the swan challenged gently. 

"Done! I take up your challenge!" the crow squawked eagerly. 

The competition began at once. The swan gradually gained height and speed. Soon he was flying effortlessly across the ocean. 

But the crow was so exhausted he could barely keep himself above the water. "What shall I do? There is not a tree in sight! Where can I stop to rest?" the crow panicked as the waves crashed below him. 

"Help! Or I'll drown!" the crow cried out, his wings giving way. 

Just then the swan returned. "What kind of flying is that, friend?" the swan asked calmly. 

"Oh, don't taunt me! I am so tired! In a little while I will drown and die!" the crow gasped, his beak and wings constantly touching the water. 

Taking pity on him, the swan took him on his back... and flew towards the shore. He placed the crow gently on the sand. 

"Brother swan! You are my superior!" the crow admitted, his pride completely shattered. "Prosperity makes foolish ones more foolish. I am indeed only a crow. Thank you for putting me in my place!"
