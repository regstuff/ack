# The Hermit and the Nawab

Once, a poor, wandering hermit went to the grand palace of a Nawab to beg for alms. He approached the armed guards at the main gates. 

"Good sirs, I have some business with the Nawab," the hermit said humbly. 

The guards recognized him as a holy man and lowered their spears. "Walk straight in, holy one. You'll find him in the prayer-room," they directed him. 

The hermit walked deep into the luxurious palace and up to the prayer-room. Peeking through the curtains, he saw the immensely wealthy Nawab kneeling on a rich carpet, his hands raised in deep prayer. 

"I'll wait for him to finish his prayers," the hermit thought, standing quietly by the door. 

The Nawab ended his prayers with his usual, daily request for prosperity. 

"O Lord, I salute you with devotion," the Nawab prayed aloud, his eyes closed. "Give me money, give me riches, give me fame and fortune..."

Hearing this, the hermit shook his head. "Oh! Oh! I've come to the wrong place!" he muttered to himself, turning around and walking away. 

As the Nawab stood up to leave, he spotted the hermit walking down the hall. 

"Wait, my good man!" the Nawab called out, hurrying after him. "You came to see me. Why are you going away without even speaking to me?"

The hermit stopped and turned around. "I came to ask you for money," he explained simply. "But when I heard you begging for money and riches from God, I changed my mind."

The Nawab looked at him in confusion. 

"Why should I beg from one who is himself a beggar?" the hermit smiled wisely. "If I must beg, let me beg from God."
