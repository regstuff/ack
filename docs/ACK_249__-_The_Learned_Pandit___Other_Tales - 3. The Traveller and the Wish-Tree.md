# The Traveller and the Wish-Tree

On a scorching hot summer's day, a weary traveller was walking through a barren landscape. Spotting a large, shady tree in the distance, he rushed toward it and collapsed beneath its branches. 

"Phew! The heat is unbearable!" he gasped, wiping sweat from his brow. "I'll rest here for a while."

He leaned back against the trunk. "Ah! This is good!" he sighed contentedly. "But how much better it would be, if I had a soft bed to sleep on."

Little did the traveller realise that the tree he was resting under was a magical Wish-Tree. 

Instantly, the air shimmered, and a magnificent, intricately carved bed with plush pillows appeared right beside him. 

"And lo! A bed! Appearing out of nowhere! I can't believe it!" the traveller gasped, quickly climbing onto it. 

"This is perfect!" he smiled, bouncing on the mattress. "The sheets are white and soft as a swan's down. But... now all I need is a maiden to keep me company in this lonely place."

*POOF!* 

A stunningly beautiful maiden adorned in gold and jewels materialized before him. She smiled warmly and began fanning him with a peacock feather. 

"Today is truly my lucky day," the traveller grinned. "I couldn't have had a lovelier companion! But... my stomach is growling. I am hungry. If only I had some good food to eat!"

Instantly, a massive feast fit for a king appeared on silver platters before him. 

"Why, it's a feast fit for a king!" he cheered. 

The traveller ate the delicious food and drank sweet wine until he was completely content. Stuffed and happy, he lay down on the soft bed again. 

"This is the absolute peak of happiness," he sighed, closing his eyes. But as he lay there, his mind began to wander. "What if all this should suddenly disappear? And what if a ferocious tiger should suddenly attack me..."

Before he even finished the thought, a massive, terrifying tiger sprang from the bushes with a deafening roar. "EE-A-AH!" the traveller screamed in sheer panic. 

He leaped off the bed and scrambled up the trunk of the tree as fast as he could, narrowly avoiding the tiger's snapping jaws. 

As he clung to the high branches, trembling in fear while the tiger growled below, the traveller realized his fatal mistake. 

*Had I been content with just the shade of the tree, I would not be at the mercy of this ferocious tiger!* he thought sadly.
