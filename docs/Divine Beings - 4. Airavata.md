# Airavata

Before Earth existed, there was endless water. And out of this vast expanse, a golden egg came into being. 

The egg split open, and Brahma, the creator of the universe, emerged from it. From the broken eggshells, Brahma created heaven and the earth, weather and time... and creatures of all sizes and colours. 

Among these magnificent creations were sixteen majestic winged elephants—eight female and eight male. Brahma declared one of them the leader of all elephants. This was the mighty Airavata, a pure white elephant with four tusks. 

The elephants were sent in pairs to the eight gods. Together, they watched over the eight directions of the universe. Brahma decreed that Airavata and his mate Abhramu would guard the East with Indra. 

One day, the venerable sage Dirghatapas was sitting in the shade of a large Vata (banyan) tree and meditating. "Ommmm...." he chanted. 

An elephant happened to pass overhead. "I'm tired. I'll rest on that tree for a while," the winged elephant decided, landing heavily on a branch. 

*Crack!* The branch gave way under the massive weight, and the elephant plummeted downwards. *Thud!* The elephant crashed to the ground right next to the meditating sage. 

"How dare you disturb my prayers!" Dirghatapas shouted in anger. In a fit of rage, the sage uttered a terrible curse. "You, and all the other elephants, will lose your wings. You will never be able to fly again." 

And so it came to be, that elephants lost their wings and began to live in forests on the ground. However, Airavata was the only one who was spared from the curse. The four-tusked mount of Indra retained his magnificent wings. 

In time, Indra came to depend heavily on Airavata in battle. "Follow that asura, Airavata. He is getting away," Indra would command, and Airavata would soar through the clouds in pursuit. Airavata too became fiercely loyal not only to Indra, but to his family, and all of Devaloka. 

Once, the gods were attacked by an immensely powerful asura named Surapadma. "Charge!" Surapadma yelled. The devas fought with all their might, but the asura army was stronger. Surapadma, having earlier received a boon from Shiva, could not be killed. The gods watched in despair as their forces were decimated. 

As the battle raged on, Indra realized that Sachi, his wife, was the target. "Surapadma is after Sachi. I will take her and go away. Airavata will stay here and help you all," Indra told the gods, retreating to safety. 

Without their king, the gods were confused. "There is no one to lead us. What are our orders?" they asked their guru, Brihaspati. 

"Jayanta, you will lead the army now," Brihaspati told Indra's son. 

"I won't let you down," Jayanta promised. 

On the next day of battle, Jayanta fought bravely, causing mayhem in the enemy ranks. His heroics drew Surapadma's attention. Surapadma hurled a spear that struck Jayanta down. 

Airavata, who was in the thick of the battle, stopped fighting when he saw Jayanta fall. He charged angrily at Surapadma's chariot and broke it into pieces. He then turned on Surapadma himself, but the invincible asura struck back, cutting two of Airavata's tusks. He then threw Airavata to the earth below. 

Airavata fell unconscious. When he finally opened his eyes, he found himself lost and defeated on Earth. Looking at the setting sun, he decided to pray to Shiva. And so began Airavata's ardent worship. 

Impressed by his devotion, and touched by his plight, Shiva appeared before Airavata. "Open your eyes, Airavata. You can ask me for anything," Shiva offered gently. 

"Lord, give me back my place in heaven," Airavata pleaded. 

"So be it!" Shiva declared. Not only did Shiva send Airavata back to Indraloka, but he also restored his broken tusks. 

*(According to another legend, Airavata was one of the divine gifts that emerged during the churning of the Ocean of Milk, and was immediately taken by Indra.)*
