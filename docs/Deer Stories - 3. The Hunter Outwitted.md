# The Hunter Outwitted

There once lived a wise stag in the forest near Rajagriha. One day, his sister came to him, accompanied by her young son. 

"Brother, will you teach your nephew the tricks of our herd?" she asked. 

"Certainly," the stag replied kindly. He turned to the young deer. "Go home now, my boy, and come back tomorrow." 

"I wonder what uncle will teach me," the young deer thought excitedly the next day. For two months, the little deer went to his uncle every day to learn the ways of the forest and the secrets of survival. 

One day, while wandering through the forest with his friends, disaster struck. *Clack!* 

"Help! I'm caught! A...a...ah!" the young deer cried out in panic as he stepped right into a hunter's trap. His friends, terrified, fled back to the herd. 

"Your son has been caught in a hunter's trap," they reported to his mother. 

"Oh dear, what shall I do?" the mother doe cried in anguish. She hurried to her brother, the wise stag. "Brother, please help me. My son has been trapped!" she sobbed. 

"Do not fear, my sister," the stag comforted her. 

Meanwhile, trapped in the snare, the young deer panicked. But then he calmed down. "I must remember what uncle taught me," he thought. "I must lie stiff and pretend to be dead." 

He held his breath and stiffened his limbs. He pretended so well that even the flies were fooled. "Poor dear. He's dead," they buzzed, swarming around him. 

Along came the hunter, spear in hand. "I hope an animal has been trapped today," he muttered. When he saw the young deer, his eyes lit up. "Aha! It's a lucky day for me. He must have died this morning. He's stiff already." 

The hunter confidently opened the trap to free the carcass. "I'll cut him up and take the flesh home. My wife will be pleased," he schemed. "On second thoughts, I'll roast some of the flesh and eat it here." 

Leaving the seemingly dead deer on the ground, he turned his back and went to collect some wood to make a fire. As soon as the hunter was distracted, the young deer sprang to his feet. "Hee..hee..hee..." he chuckled silently, bounding away into the forest at lightning speed. 

The hunter turned around to see his dinner escaping. "Come back! Come back!" he yelled furiously, but it was too late. 

The young deer arrived safely back at the herd. "My son, you are back! How did you escape?" his mother wept with joy. 

The wise stag smiled proudly at his nephew. "He was a good student!"
