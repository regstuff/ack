# Dhruva

Dhruva was the son of Suneeti, the senior queen of King Uttanapada. Unfortunately for Dhruva and Suneeti, Uttanapada loved Suruchi, his second queen, much more and was highly partial to her. 

One day, young Dhruva saw Suruchi's son, Uttama, playing near the King. As Suruchi walked up to her son, Uttama asked, "Mother, father is free. May I go and sit on his lap?" 

"Certainly, my son," Suruchi replied proudly. "It is your rightful place as the future king." 

When Dhruva saw Uttama sitting happily on Uttanapada's lap, he ran towards them. "I too will sit on my father's lap," Dhruva said eagerly. 

Suruchi quickly blocked his path. "Certainly not!" she snapped. "You have no right to be there. Go away." 

Heartbroken, Dhruva looked up at her. "Where shall I go, mother?" 

"Go to Lord Narayana and seek his favour to be reborn as my son," Suruchi declared cruelly. "Only then can you enjoy the same rights as Uttama." 

Stung by her harsh words, Dhruva ran crying to his own mother, Suneeti. "Why are you crying, Dhruva? Has anyone hurt you?" she asked, alarmed. 

"Mother, Suruchi said I had no right to sit on my father's lap," Dhruva sobbed. "She said only Uttama had the right to sit on the king's lap." 

Suneeti held back her tears. "She's right, Dhruva," she admitted softly. 

"I am a kshatriya prince, am I not, mother?" Dhruva asked, his face hardening with resolve. 

"Yes, my child." 

"Mother, Suruchi told me to go and seek Lord Narayana's favour if I want to sit on my father's lap. Will Narayana really help me, mother?" 

"Yes, my child. He never fails those who turn to him." 

"Then I shall seek him out," Dhruva declared. 

Suneeti hugged him. "Go, my son. He is the only one who can help us. It will not be easy to find him. But if you try hard enough you will succeed." 

"I will succeed, mother. I will not return till I meet the Lord. I will become a greater king than my father or his father before him." 

As Dhruva left for the forest, the great Sage Narada came to know of his resolve. Narada went to see Dhruva to test his determination. "My child, why don't you accept your fate and go back to your mother?" Narada asked. 

"O Master, I am Dhruva, the son of mighty Uttanapada, a kshatriya," the boy replied firmly. "And I am determined to win the Lord's favour." 

Narada was extremely pleased by his determination. "Forgive me, Master, but I will not turn back," Dhruva added. "O wise one, tell me how I can succeed in my resolve." 

"If you meditate in the Madhuvan forest on the banks of the Yamuna, you will find the Lord," Narada instructed. He then taught Dhruva the sacred mantra: *"Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya!"* "Say that over and over again, and concentrate on that." 

As Dhruva set out for Madhuvan, Narada went to the palace of King Uttanapada. The King was deeply distressed. "Alas! He has gone away alone into the forest. The beasts will devour him," the King worried. 

"Fear not, O King. The Lord Himself will protect your son. He will come back bringing glory to all," Narada assured him. 

Meanwhile, Dhruva reached Madhuvan. "I shall sit here and begin my meditation," he said. 

In the first month, he lived only on the fruits he could find in the forest. Gradually he gave up even that, and ate only grass and leaves. In the third month, he stopped eating and lived only on water. In the fourth month, he gave up even that and lived on air. Throughout the period, he repeated the mantra over and over again. In the fifth and sixth months, he even stopped breathing. 

Dhruva's immense concentration stopped the very air from flowing on Earth. There was panic in heaven. The gods rushed to Lord Narayana. "O Lord, give us life-giving air! We cannot breathe! Save us!" 

"Do not despair," Lord Narayana smiled. "I shall descend to Earth and grant Dhruva the boon he seeks." 

Back in Madhuvan, Dhruva felt a divine presence. "Lord, I thought I had found you but you've suddenly vanished. Have I failed in my worship?" he thought. Then, he opened his eyes. 

Lord Narayana stood before him in all his glory. "The Lord has come to me!" Dhruva gasped, bowing deeply. 

"I know what has brought you to Madhuvan," Lord Narayana said gently. "Your wish will be fulfilled. You shall be a great king. You shall rule for 36,000 years. Then will I give you a place in heaven where you shall stay forever as a guiding star." 

When Dhruva returned to the kingdom, King Uttanapada was overjoyed. "I cannot wait to see my son. Commander, get the chariots ready. We will all go to receive him!" 

Amidst great rejoicing, Dhruva was carried back to the palace. As soon as Dhruva was old enough to rule, Uttanapada crowned him king and retired to the forest. Dhruva became a legendary ruler, and to this day, he is immortalized in the night sky as the Pole Star—the Dhruva Nakshatra.
