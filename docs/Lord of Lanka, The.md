# The Lord of Lanka

Sumali was the King of the Rakshasas. From the nether-world, where he lived, he once came to visit the world of men, along with his beautiful daughter, Kaikesi. 

He travelled from one place to another looking for the worthiest of men for his daughter to marry. At last, he saw a magnificent chariot descending from the sky in front of the hermitage of Vishrava. It was Kubera, Vishrava's son, the rich and mighty King of Lanka. 

"Daughter, approach Vishrava and win him over. If he marries you, you will have sons as mighty as Kubera," Sumali advised. 

Kaikesi went to the hermitage of Vishrava while he was busy offering his evening prayers. "I understand. But you have come to me at the wrong hour," the sage warned her. "The children born to us will be wicked Rakshasas..." 

Vishrava married Kaikesi, and within a few years, Ravana (the ten-headed one), Kumbhakarna, Shoorpanakha, and Vibheeshana were born. 

Years rolled by. Ravana and Kumbhakarna grew up arrogant and ambitious. Jealous of Kubera's wealth and power, Ravana made a vow: "Mother, here and now I make this vow. I will excel Kubera." 

Ravana immediately set out for Gokarna along with his brothers, Kumbhakarna and Vibheeshana. They began performing severe penances. During the penance, Ravana did not touch food. One by one he sacrificed his heads in the holy fire. 

When he was about to sacrifice his tenth head, Lord Brahma appeared before him. "I am pleased with you. Ask for a boon." 

"O Lord, grant me immortality," Ravana asked. 

"Immortality is not for you. Ask for something else," Brahma replied. 

"Then let me not die at the hands of gods, demons, Rakshasas, serpents and spirits. I am not afraid of mortals," Ravana requested, arrogantly dismissing the threat of human beings. 

"So be it. And I grant you more. Your heads will be restored to you. You will also have the power to change your form at will," Brahma granted. Brahma also granted boons to Vibheeshana (righteousness) and Kumbhakarna (a long sleep). 

When they returned home, Ravana sent a messenger to Kubera's court: "Go and tell Kubera that he must vacate Lanka, immediately. I wish to occupy it." 

Kubera consulted his father, Vishrava, who advised him to yield. Kubera vacated Lanka, and Ravana became the king. As the days passed, Ravana became increasingly arrogant, harassing the gods, sages, and Gandharvas. 

When Kubera sent a messenger advising Ravana to mend his ways, Ravana was furious. "Impudent man! You will pay for this," he yelled, slaying the messenger. "Now I must teach Kubera the lesson of his life." 

Ravana attacked Kubera's kingdom. A great battle took place. Because of the boon, Ravana was invincible. He defeated Kubera and seized 'Pushpaka', the flying chariot of Kubera. 

As Ravana flew over Mount Kailas in the Pushpaka chariot, it came to a dead halt. Nandi, Shiva's attendant, came down. "Ravana! This is Lord Shiva's abode. Do not disturb him," he warned. 

"Whose orders are these? Shiva's? I'll uproot this very mountain on which he rests," Ravana roared. Assuming a huge form, Ravana tried to lift Kailas. 

The mountain shook, terrifying even Parvati. But Lord Shiva simply pressed the mountain with his toe, crushing Ravana's hands beneath it. 

"Ahh! Help!" Ravana cried out in pain. Advised by his minister, Ravana began singing hymns in praise of Shiva for many years. Pleased with his devotion, Lord Shiva permitted Ravana to remove his hands and gave him a sword. 

Ravana resumed conquering the world. In the Himalayas, he saw a beautiful maiden, Vedavati, meditating to win Lord Vishnu as her husband. Ravana advanced towards her and seized her by the hair. 

Enraged by his touch, Vedavati created a fire and jumped into it. "I will be born again and be the cause of your death," she cursed him. 

Vedavati took birth in a lotus, which Ravana found. But remembering the curse, he threw the baby into the river. She was eventually found in a furrow by King Janaka and named Sita. Sita grew up and married Rama, the Prince of Ayodhya, who broke Shiva's bow to win her hand. 

Meanwhile, Ravana's arrogance continued. He seized Rambha, the betrothed of Nalkoober (Kubera's son). Nalkoober laid a terrible curse on him: "If he ever marries a woman against her will, his heads will break into seven pieces." 

Years later, undaunted by his past misdeeds, Ravana disguised himself as a monk and visited the Dandaka forest where Sita was living in exile with Rama. While Rama was away hunting, Ravana kidnapped Sita and brought her to his Ashoka garden in Lanka. 

"I love you. Why do you waste your time on Rama? Marry me," he pleaded, but fear of Nalkoober's curse kept him from forcing her. Sita steadfastly refused him. 

Rama, with a huge army of monkeys and bears, constructed a bridge across the ocean and invaded Lanka. Ravana's brothers and courtiers advised him to return Sita, but he was angry. He banished Vibheeshana, who then joined Rama's side. 

The war began. One by one, the warriors of Ravana fell, including the mighty Kumbhakarna. 

Desperate, Ravana used magic to deceive Sita, showing her an illusion of Rama's severed head and bow. But her grief turned to relief when she learned it was a trick and Rama was very much alive. 

Finally, Ravana himself entered the battlefield. The two foes met in a fierce duel. Rama used a crescent-shaped weapon to sever Ravana's heads, but they kept regenerating. At last, Rama used a powerful divine weapon, and the arrow found its mark in Ravana's chest. The Lord of Lanka fell dead. 

As Ravana lay defeated, Vibheeshana wept. "Don't grieve, Vibheeshana! He has died like a brave warrior," Rama consoled him. 

Lanka now had a new, righteous lord. Rama crowned Vibheeshana King of Lanka, and after rescuing Sita, returned triumphantly to Ayodhya.
