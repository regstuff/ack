# The Greedy Hawker

Long ago, two hawkers met near a ferry crossing, preparing to sell their wares in a nearby town. One hawker was an honest, kind-hearted man, but the other was incredibly greedy and cunning. 

"How's business?" the honest hawker asked.

"Bad," the greedy hawker grumbled, eyeing the town across the river. 

"Let's hope we have better luck on the other side," the honest man smiled. "Today, you take the road to the left and I'll take the one to the right."

"I don't think he has been to this town before," the greedy hawker thought to himself, grinning maliciously. "There are plenty of houses on the right, but practically none on the left. As you say," he agreed out loud, quickly grabbing the better route for himself. 

In the town, the greedy hawker walked the streets shouting, "I buy anything... I sell everything. Your favourite pedlar is here!"

Inside a run-down house, a young girl pulled at her grandmother's sari. "Grandmother, why don't you sell that old bowl we have in the storeroom?" 

The grandmother, hoping to buy her granddaughter a small treat, brought the heavy, soot-covered bowl to the doorway. "Pedlar, come here! What have you to offer me, old woman? What will you give my poor child in exchange for this bowl?" she asked, handing it over.

The greedy hawker took the bowl. "It's heavy," he thought, turning it over. Using a sharp needle, he stealthily scratched the bottom. A brilliant gold color shone through the grime. *Let me scratch it and see... Yes, it is gold! And this foolish woman doesn't know it,* he realized, his heart racing with greed. 

He decided to cheat the poor family entirely. He threw the priceless golden bowl onto the ground with a disgusted sneer. "This bowl is worthless! I cannot give you anything for it. Can't you give me anything at all? Nothing! Throw that old piece of junk away." He stormed off, pretending to be angry, but secretly plotting. *I'll come back tomorrow and offer her two silver coins. She'll be so desperate she'll take it!*

The next day, the honest hawker happened to walk down that same street. "Pots and pans... to sell and exchange!" he called.

"Grandmother, here comes another pedlar," the little girl said hopefully. 

The grandmother sighed, picking up the dirty bowl again. "Pedlar! Pedlar! Yes, mother? What can I do for you?" the honest hawker asked.

"I want to sell this bowl." 

The honest hawker took the bowl and noticed the scratch at the bottom. His eyes went wide. "Mother, this is a golden bowl! Good God, I remember now! That bowl used to belong to your grandfather."

"Golden!" the grandmother gasped in shock.

"We were rich then! Oh, those were the days!" she wept happily. 

"It's worth two thousand coins at least," the honest hawker said gently. "I don't have that much money. I have five hundred coins in cash and my goods, which are worth another five hundred. That's a thousand in total."

"You are an honest man," the grandmother smiled warmly. "Give us whatever you can. That's more than enough." 

The deal was made. The honest hawker gave the woman five hundred coins and all his goods, leaving him with almost nothing, but happy. "Goodbye, mother. Goodbye, my little sister," he smiled, waving as he left the town. "Grandma, we are rich now," the little girl cheered.

Hours later, the greedy hawker returned, confidently striding up to the old woman's door. "Now tell me what you... pots and pans! Pots and pans!" he shouted, expecting her to beg him for a sale. "Old woman, fetch that bowl! Though it is worthless, I am in a good mood today and I'll give you two coins for it!"

"Yes, two whole coins!" he grinned, holding out the silver pieces.

The grandmother glared at him with fiery eyes. "Go away, you greedy man! I've already sold that bowl for a thousand coins. And to a very honest man."

"Y-you've sold that bowl?" the greedy hawker stammered, his face turning pale. "Oh, this is terrible."

As he staggered away from the house, the weight of his own greed crushed him. "That bowl is worth two thousand coins and that man got it for just a thousand! I could have bought it for ten yesterday. What a heavy price I've paid for my greed," he wept, tearing his hair out.

Cursing himself and his own foolishness, the greedy hawker left the town entirely empty-handed.
