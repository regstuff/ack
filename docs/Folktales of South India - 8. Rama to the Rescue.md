# Rama to the Rescue

In a village, one night, a tired man came home. "Oh-humm! What a hard day it has been! I'm feeling so sleepy..." he yawned, lying down to rest next to his wife. 

Suddenly, his wife nudged him. "What's the matter? What are you looking at?" he mumbled sleepily. 

"Listen! Ssssh!" she whispered, pointing towards the door. 

"Somebody is trying to get in," he realized, hearing a scratching sound at the wooden door. 

"I... I think he has already got in... The noise has stopped," his wife whispered, trembling with fear. 

"Lie down... don't look," he whispered back. "It's a thief... he must have somehow got past Rama." 

Rama was the village Kotwal (head of the police), a large, imposing man with a thick moustache, known for keeping the village safe. 

"What should we do?" his wife whimpered. 

"I'll tell you. Listen..." he replied in a low voice. 

Meanwhile, the thief, a burly man with a mask over his eyes, had slipped into the house. He heard them whispering in the bedroom. "Voices! They're awake. I'll have to wait till they fall asleep," he thought, hiding behind the open door. He eyed a large wooden chest in the corner. "I wonder where they keep their money." 

He strained his ears to hear the couple's conversation. "They're saying something. Perhaps they're talking about their money. I'd better listen closely." 

The husband had devised a clever plan. He spoke up in a loud, clear voice. "If he is a boy we'll call him Rama." 

"Rama? Yes... that's a good name," his wife played along loudly. 

"When he's in the house, I'll call out softly to him. Rama! Rama!" the husband continued. The thief, hiding just outside the door, thought it was a bit strange. 

"But what if he's in the yard?" the husband asked loudly. "Then I'll call out a little louder. RAMA, RAMA!" 

The thief grinned in the darkness. "I wish they would stop this silly game and talk about their money instead," he thought impatiently. 

"Or fall asleep at least!" the thief grumbled to himself. 

"But my dear, what if the boy is not in the house or in the yard, but in the street?" the husband yelled. 

"Oh, then I'll call out very loudly..." his wife replied at the top of her voice. 

"RAMA! RAMA!" the husband screamed into the quiet night. 

Down the street, the village Kotwal heard his name echoing through the air. "RAMA! RAMA!" 

"Someone's calling me," Rama thought, grabbing his staff and sprinting towards the sound. He ran straight to the house from which he heard his name being called. "Oh, oh! Some thief has dug his way into this house," he noticed the hole in the wall. 

Inside, the thief finally let out a sigh of relief as the couple fell silent. "Ah, at last they've stopped their chatter! Now soon they'll go to sleep and... eh!" 

Suddenly, a heavy hand grabbed his shoulder. "You're under arrest," Rama boomed, lifting his staff. 

"It's Rama! He heard us!" the husband whispered joyfully to his wife. 

"The plan worked! We're saved," she smiled. The man and his wife, by their cleverness, had saved themselves from being robbed.
