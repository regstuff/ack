# The Loyal General

Long ago in Varanasi, there lived a majestic King Crow who had a mate he loved dearly. 

One day, as they flew past the royal palace of the human King of Varanasi, the Queen Crow saw a sight through the palace window which made her mouth water. The royal cook was serving the King a massive, steaming platter of delicious fish. 

"Ah! How I wish I could taste just a tiny bit of that fish!" the Queen Crow sighed longingly. 

The next day, her craving had grown so intense that she refused to leave her nest. "Come, my love. Let's go out and look for some food," the King Crow coaxed. 

"I don't want the kind of food we find!" she stubbornly refused. "I want the food I saw in the palace on the King's table. If I can't have it, I'll die."

As the King Crow sat on a branch, wondering what on earth he should do to save his beloved mate, his most trusted General came up to him. 

"Your Majesty, what's the matter?" the General asked. 

When the King Crow told him the impossible problem, the General puffed out his chest confidently. "Oh! Is that all? Don't worry. I'll see that our queen has the food she craves for."

Taking eight of the best, most agile crows with him, the General flew directly towards the heavily guarded human palace. "Let's perch on the kitchen roof," he ordered his strike team. 

"Now listen carefully," the General laid out the daring plan. "While the food is being taken to the King, I'll dive bomb the cook and make him drop the dishes. Four of you must then immediately fill your beaks with rice, and four with fish, and fly straight to our Queen."

The crows watched the courtyard below like hawks. "Ah! Here comes the cook!" the General whispered. "When he reaches the open courtyard, I'll strike!"

The General swooped down from the roof like a black arrow. *AIEEEEE!*

The massive crow struck the terrified cook squarely on the head. Screaming in shock, the cook dropped the heavy silver platters, spilling the King's entire lunch across the marble floor! 

In a perfectly synchronized strike, the chosen eight crows swooped down, filled their beaks with the finest royal fish and rice, and flew away before the guards could even react. 

"Drop those dishes and catch that wicked crow!" the furious human King shouted from his balcony. 

The royal guards quickly swarmed the courtyard and managed to catch the brave General, bringing him before the King. "Good! You've got him! Bring him here."

"O, crow!" the King thundered. "You have dared to displease me. And in this foolish venture, you've risked your very life! Why did you do it?"

The General held his head high. "For my King. He told me about his Queen's longing for the food she saw on your table. I promised to get it for her—and I have kept my promise."

The King was absolutely stunned by the bird's bravery. He turned to his ministers. "The bird was willing to sacrifice his own life for his King! Such perfect loyalty is rare! He must be rewarded." 

The King turned back to the General. "Set him free!"

Then, the King issued a royal decree. "Henceforth, a portion of the food cooked for me shall be sent every single day to you and your King and Queen. And a large measure of rice shall be cooked daily for your King's entire following."
