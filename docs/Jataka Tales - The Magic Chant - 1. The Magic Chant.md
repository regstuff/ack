# The Magic Chant

A pandit and his pupil were once passing through a forest. Suddenly, robbers jumped out! "Halt! Who goes there?" 

They bound the pandit to a tree. "We have no money. Please let us go," the pandit pleaded. 

"Ha! Ha! You cannot deceive us with humble talk and simple clothes! If you are carrying no money, your pupil can bring it from your house," the robber chief laughed. "Run fast and bring a thousand gold coins. Only then will your guru be freed!" 

"Master, I'll come back as soon as possible," the pupil whispered to the pandit. "So please do not tell these thugs that you know the Vaidarbha Mantra! Now hurry up, boy, and do what you've been told!" 

The Vaidarbha Mantra is a chant known to very few. If uttered when the stars are in a favourable position, it produces a shower of gems. 

That night, as the pandit shivered in the cold, he looked up at the sky. "Oh! The planets are moving into the correct position. Should I tell the robbers about the Vaidarbha Mantra and so be free again?" 

Remembering his pupil's advice, he hesitated. But finally, he reasoned, "After all, what is the use of knowledge if one cannot utilise it in times of crisis? Hey, chief! Listen! I have something important to say!" 

"If I start a shower of gems, will you let me go?" 

"A shower of gems? Are you joking?" the chief laughed. 

"No, I'm serious. I can do it by means of a magic chant." The chief agreed. 

"I can't do it without having a ritual bath and changing into clean silk clothes," the pandit said. 

So the pandit was set free, bathed, and sat reciting the chant. The very next moment... "Gems! Those are precious gems falling down!" the robbers cried in amazement. They collected the gems and allowed the pandit to go. 

But, the next moment, another gang of robbers surrounded them. "Stay where you are! You are surrounded! Tie them all up!" the chief of the second gang ordered. 

"Wait! First tell me why you are doing this!" the first chief pleaded. 

"What a stupid question! For your gems, of course," the second chief replied. 

"He's invaluable! He knows a magic chant that will bring down a shower of jewels. That's how we came by ours!" the first chief revealed, pointing to the pandit. 

The second gang let the first gang go and turned to the pandit. "My friend, let's have a big shower of gems!" 

"You shall have it—but only after one year. I... I'm... sorry. The chant is effective only at a specific time. The auspicious moment will not occur again for another year," the pandit stammered in fear. 

"Liar! Scoundrel! We won't let you get away with it," the furious second chief roared, killing the pandit. "Come on, let's go after those rascals we set free! They've tricked us, but they won't go far with those gems!" 

A fierce fight ensued between the two gangs, and the first gang was vanquished. But the second gang then fought amongst themselves over the treasure. "Realising it was useless to fight, they pretended to be friends. They hid the treasure in the hollow of a tree." 

Only two robbers were left alive. "I am feeling hungry. Let's go and eat," one said. "One of us should stay here to guard the treasure. You take the head..." 

One robber went to buy food. The other hid behind a rock. "I'll hide behind this rock and surprise him when he comes back. I will share neither food nor jewels with him. I'll get rid of him!" 

Meanwhile, the robber who bought food thought, "I'm starving. I'll eat first and then take the rest for my friend. Then he set off again, sprinkling poison on the remaining food." 

When he arrived, the waiting robber sprang out and killed him. "And now, for some food. Ah! I am as rich as a king today!" 

But it was all in vain. For, having eaten the poisoned food, he, too, fell dead. 

When, two days later, the pupil returned with the money, he found the place littered with corpses. He realized what had happened. "My guru used the chant for the benefit of undeserving people! If only he had heeded my advice!"
