# The Drummer

A drummer and his son once went to Varanasi to play at a festival. At the end of the festival, they were rewarded handsomely. 

"Here is some money for both of you. Your son is a very good drummer," the organizers said, giving them money, jewels, and clothes. 

As they walked back through the forest, the son was overjoyed. "Father, I enjoyed the festival very much." 

"You gave a good performance, son," the father smiled. 

The next moment, the son started beating the drum loudly. "What are you doing?" 

"Beating the drum—what else? I feel on top of the world today, father!" 

"Don't make such a din! You will attract robbers! This forest is full of them!" the father warned. "I'll play a martial beat to frighten them off, father!" the son replied, playing a rhythmic march. 

Some thieves hiding in the jungle heard the loud drumming. "It appears a royal party is hunting in the forest," they thought, running away. 

After a while, the father said, "Enough, son! Don't do it again! That should only be played once!" 

But the boy went on. "Stop! Why don't you listen to me?" his father pleaded. 

"Oh, father, you worry too much! I'll scare the thieves good and proper!" the son laughed, continuing to drum. 

The thieves, hearing the continuous drumming, grew suspicious. "What! The same drum-beats thrice! That is no royal party. It is just somebody who has made a lot of money and is feeling happy! Let's attack him!" 

The thieves quickly spotted the drummer and his son. "Stop! Don't move! Give us all you have!" they threatened. They stripped them of their money, clothes, and jewels, and ran off with the loot. 

"I told you to stop, didn't I? But you wouldn't listen. In your pride, you foolishly invited your own doom by playing at the wrong time!" the father scolded. 

"I have learnt my lesson now, father!" the poor boy sighed.
