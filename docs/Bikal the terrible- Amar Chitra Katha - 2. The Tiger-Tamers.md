# The Tiger-Tamers

In a small village tucked near the forested hills of Madhya Pradesh lived two mischievous brothers. While the other villagers worked hard in the fields, the two boys spent their days playing pranks and causing trouble for everyone. 

Hiding behind bushes with their bows, they would shoot arrows to shatter the clay water pots carried by village women on their heads. They chased chickens through courtyard gardens and drove herds of goats into the forest for fun. 

One afternoon, after the boys drove away a farmer's goats, the aggrieved villagers lost their patience. Forming an angry posse armed with sticks and pickaxes, the men marched toward the boys. "Those rascals are becoming bolder every day!" a farmer shouted. "Let's catch them and give them a proper thrashing!"

Seeing the mob approaching, the brothers sprinted toward the woods. Outrunning the villagers, they plunged deep into the dense jungle. 

Collapsing onto the grass, the younger brother panted, "Phew! Where do we go from here?"

"We can only go forward," the older brother replied. "It will take days for those villagers to cool down."

Venturing deeper into the uncharted forest, they rounded a rocky bend and froze dead in their tracks. Sitting right in the middle of the narrow path was an enormous, wild tiger.

Before the beast could spring, the younger brother unexpectedly darted forward, vaulted onto the tiger's back, and grabbed its ears, forcing its snout to the ground. Simultaneously, the older brother leaped behind and seized the tiger's tail in a tight grip!

Startled out of its wits, the tiger squirmed under their grasp. "ARRGH! What's happening?" the tiger growled in confusion. "Who are you? What do you want with me?"

"We are tiger-hunters!" the younger boy announced boldly from the beast's back. "We need a strong tiger to pull our plough."

"A tiger to pull your plough?" the predator asked, stunned. "When you plough with bullocks, you get rice and grain. What do you get when you plough with a tiger?"

"When you plough with tigers, you get gold and silver!" the boy lied smoothly. "We have already captured one tiger today, and you are the second one."

"Is that so?" the tiger asked nervously.

Pulling a small handheld mirror from his sash, the older brother held it right in front of the tiger's face. Gazing into the glass, the tiger caught sight of its own snarling reflection and mistook it for the first captured tiger! 

*If they've already caught such a fierce, nasty-looking fellow,* the tiger thought in sheer terror, *what chance do I have? I'll end up yoked to a wooden plough for the rest of my life!*

Shaking with fear, the tiger offered, "Look, friends! If it's gold and silver you are after, I can give you heaps of it! In my lifetime, I have killed and eaten several kings and princes. All their gold crowns and jewels are lying inside my cave. Come with me, and you can take it all!"

"How do we know you aren't making this up just to escape?" the older brother questioned.

"Come to my cave and see for yourselves!" the tiger pleaded.

The tiger led the brothers to the mouth of its cavern. As the boys stepped inside, the tiger seized the moment and bolted into the jungle as fast as its legs could carry it. *That was a narrow escape!* the beast gasped in relief as it ran. *If not for my quick thinking, I would have ended up pulling a plough!*

Inside the cave, the boys gasped in amazement. Scattered among the rocks were piles of solid gold necklaces, diamond-studded armlets, and royal crowns. The brothers gathered as much treasure as they could carry, wrapping it in a large cloth bundle.

Meanwhile, the escaped tiger hunted a fat sambhar deer and invited twelve fellow tigers from the surrounding forest to a grand feast outside his cave to celebrate his narrow escape.

Carrying their heavy bundle of gold, the two brothers wandered through the forest, only to realize they were lost. Suddenly, the bushes parted, and the boys spotted a pack of twelve tigers walking toward them!

"Tigers!" the younger boy gasped. "We're done for if they see us!"

"Climb that tree!" the older brother urged.

The older boy scrambled up into the high branches, but the younger brother, weighed down by the heavy bundle, was stuck halfway up the trunk when the pack of tigers arrived below, dragging the dead deer.

The thirteen tigers sat in a circle directly beneath the tree. The host tiger addressed his guests proudly: "Before we begin our feast, let me tell you of my narrow escape today. I was captured by two monstrous creatures who catch tigers to pull their ploughs. But I used my brains and escaped!"

Just then, the younger brother's arms gave out. With a loud **THUMP**, he lost his grip and fell from the trunk, landing right in the middle of the circle of feasting tigers!

Thinking at lightning speed, the boy scrambled to his feet, pointed at the host tiger, and roared with all his might, "YEOW! GRAB THE OLD TIGER! DON'T LET HIM GET AWAY!"

From the high branch above, the older brother immediately joined in, shouting, "GRAB HIM! IT'S THEM!"

The host tiger shrieked in terror. "MY FRIENDS, IT'S THOSE TWO MONSTERS I WAS TELLING YOU ABOUT!"

Panicked beyond measure, all thirteen tigers leaped over one another in wild chaos, scattering in every direction and fleeing for their lives through the woods.

When the dust settled, the two brothers climbed down, laughing with relief. Looking out from the high tree branches earlier, the older brother had spotted the familiar landmarks of their village trail. 

Carrying their fortune in gold and silver, the two brave brothers returned home, where the impressed villagers welcomed them back as village heroes.
