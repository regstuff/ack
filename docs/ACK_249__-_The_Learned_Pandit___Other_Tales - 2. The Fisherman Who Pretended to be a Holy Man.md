# The Fisherman Who Pretended to be a Holy Man

One dark, moonless night, a sneaky fisherman stole into the private, walled garden of a wealthy estate. 

"The house is dark and quiet. Everyone seems to be asleep! Good!" the fisherman grinned. He crept up to the large, private pond in the center of the garden. "I can cast my net right away... and catch enough fish for the week."

He threw his heavy net into the pond with a loud *SPLASH!*

Inside the mansion, the wealthy owner shot up in bed. "What was that sound? Did you hear it?" he asked his wife. "I did. It sounded like a net falling into the water."

"A net? In the water? The pond! My fish! Somebody's stealing my fish! Thief! Thief!" the owner yelled, jumping out of bed. 

He quickly woke up his servants. "Quick! It's a thief! Catch him!" they shouted, grabbing lanterns and running out into the garden. 

Outside, the fisherman panicked. "They're awake! They're coming this way. I'm trapped. What shall I do?" 

He looked desperately around the garden. A smouldering bonfire sat nearby, leftover from the gardener's work. *Fate couldn't have been kinder!* he thought. 

Thinking fast, he pulled off his turban, grabbed a handful of white ash from the fire, and hurriedly smeared it all over his arms, chest, and forehead. He sat down beneath a tree, closed his eyes, and pretended to be deep in meditation. 

Moments later, the servants burst through the bushes with their lanterns. "Look! Can you see a man there?" 

They shined their lights on the ash-covered figure. "Oh! Oh! It's a sanyasi!" they gasped, dropping to their knees in reverence. "Forgive us, sir. We did not mean to disturb your meditation."

The servants quietly backed away and ran back to their master. "Well, what happened? Have you found him?" the owner demanded. 

"No luck, sir. He escaped. But we saw a holy man in the garden," they reported. 

"A holy man? In my garden? Take me to him."

The owner tiptoed into the garden and saw the ash-covered figure sitting peacefully under the tree. "Hush!" the owner whispered to his servants. "He must be a great saint. We mustn't disturb him. He is meditating. Let's go away."

Left alone in the dark, the fisherman chuckled to himself. *I've fooled even the master of the house. Now aren't I clever!*

At dawn the next day, the fisherman prepared to sneak away. "It's time I left," he thought, dusting himself off. But as he stood up, he froze. "Hey! Who are these people?"

A massive crowd of villagers, led by the wealthy owner and his family, had gathered in the garden. "O holy saint, we heard of you and have come to seek your blessings," the owner bowed respectfully. 

"Please bless our child," a mother pleaded, laying her baby before him. 

"Good God! More and more of them," the fisherman thought in shock as people brought forward silver bowls filled with expensive fruits, sweets, and fresh flowers. "Are they mad?"

The fisherman sat back down, overwhelmed by the outpouring of love and reverence. "And to crown it all, the look of utter devotion in their eyes!" he thought, deeply moved. 

As an old man touched his feet with trembling hands, the fisherman had a profound realization. *How strange! I am not a real sadhu. And yet, these people show so much respect for me. How much greater would be the respect they'd show if I were a real sadhu!*

Tears welled up in the fisherman's eyes. *Yes, I shall become a true man of God! I shall give up thieving for good.*

And so, the thief who began by merely pretending to be holy, truly became a great saint.
