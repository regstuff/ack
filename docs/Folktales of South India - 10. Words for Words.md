# Words for Words

A city dweller was passing through a village. He looked around at the innocent-looking villagers and smirked. "The people here are so simple. I could easily make some money here if I tried." 

He approached a well-to-do villager relaxing on a swing on his porch. "Let me begin with this man. He appears to be rich... and foolish," the conman thought. 

He walked up and bowed low. "Oh, what a noble face you have, sir! I can tell that you are a man of great generosity." 

The villager smiled warmly. "Wife, bring ten measures of rice for my friend here!" he called out to his wife inside the house. 

The conman was thrilled. He bowed even lower. "Men like you are rare, sir. You shine like a jewel on a heap of coal." 

"Wife! Bring a hundred rupee note!" the villager shouted enthusiastically. 

The conman's eyes sparkled with greed. He decided to turn up the flattery. "Let me recite a verse in your honour, sir. *Flowers bloom only for a few hours; clouds pour only for a few days; rivers flow only for a few months; ...but your glory, sir, shines forever.*" 

"WIFE! Bring two silk dhoties!" the villager bellowed. 

The conman rubbed his hands together. "How easily I have fooled this stupid fellow!" he thought, waiting eagerly for the woman to come out with his loot. 

He waited... and waited. The villager casually picked up a book and started reading on his swing. 

"Er... why haven't the gifts come as yet, sir?" the conman finally asked, his patience running out. 

"Gifts?" the villager looked up, surprised. 

"Now, what did you do with your words?" the villager asked with a knowing twinkle in his eye. 

"I... er... made you happy," the conman stammered. 

"Well, I have made you happy with mine," the villager chuckled. 

The conman realized that the villagers were not as simple as they looked. Embarrassed and empty-handed, he hastily left the village.
