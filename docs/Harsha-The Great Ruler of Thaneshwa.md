# Harsha-The Great Ruler of Thaneshwar

About 1,400 years ago, Thaneshwar, a powerful kingdom in Northern India, was ruled by Prabhakar Vardhan. During this period the Huns had started harassing the people of Thaneshwar. So the king sent his able sons, Rajyavardhan and Harsha, to punish them. 

"Har Har Mahadev!" the princes cried as they led their army into battle. After a bitter fight, the Huns retreated. 

"Victory to Prince Rajyavardhan! Victory to Prince Harsha!" the soldiers cheered. 

"Halt! It is getting dark. Let us stop following them," Rajyavardhan ordered. The Huns did not attack again. 

"There has been no fighting for several days. I think I'll go on a hunt," Harsha decided. 

While hunting far away from the camp, Harsha saw a messenger riding furiously towards him. "Prince Harsha, your father is very ill. He wants to see you and Prince Rajya," the messenger gasped. 

"I'll go to him. You inform my brother," Harsha instructed. 

Both princes rushed to Thaneshwar, but they were too late. Their father had passed away. 

A few days later, Harsha comforted his brother. "Prince Rajya, you will now have to sit on the throne." 

"Oh, no! Let Harsha be the king," Rajyavardhan protested in his grief. 

"Certainly not. Being the elder brother, it is your right and duty to rule. Prince Harsha is right, sir," the ministers agreed. 

"My brothers are young and inexperienced. Please guide them," their sister Rajyashri urged the ministers. And so, Rajyavardhan was crowned. "Long live King Rajyavardhan!" 

Rajyashri, their sister, was married to Grahavarman, the king of Kannauj. Meanwhile, Devgupta, the greedy king of Malwa, was busy plotting with King Shashank of Gaud. 

"The time is ripe. We can now grab the kingdoms of Thaneshwar and Kannauj," Devgupta schemed. 

"But they are very powerful, sir," a minister warned. 

"I know. But if we kill Grahavarman, the inexperienced Rajyavardhan will not be able to defend both kingdoms," Devgupta smiled wickedly. "I have a plan. During the spring festival, we will disguise ourselves as simple peasants and mingle with the commoners of Kannauj. When Grahavarman comes to celebrate the festival with his people, we will kill him." 

According to their plan, Devgupta and his soldiers, dressed in ordinary clothes, mingled with the people of Kannauj. As King Grahavarman was entering a temple, Devgupta struck. "Die! Grahavarman, die!" 

After killing Grahavarman, Devgupta forcibly carried Rajyashri with him and threw her into a prison in Malwa. "Help! Help!" she cried in vain. 

The news of Devgupta's treachery reached Rajyavardhan. "Devgupta has invited his death," Rajyavardhan swore. 

"Maharaj, allow me to attack Malwa," Harsha pleaded. 

"No, Harsha! Being the elder brother and the king, it is my duty to do so," Rajyavardhan insisted. He marched towards Malwa. 

Meanwhile in Malwa, Devgupta mocked his captive. "Say whatever you like. But I shall soon be the king of Kannauj as well as Thaneshwar." 

Devgupta had allied with King Shashank. "When the battle begins, attack Rajyavardhan from the rear. Once he is dead, we've as good as won," Devgupta told Shashank. 

In the ensuing battle, Rajyavardhan fought fiercely and finally killed Devgupta. "Victory to Maharaj Rajyavardhan!" 

Shashank was very upset over the death of Devgupta, but he pretended to be an ally. "I am grateful to you for your help, King Shashank. You shall always be my most trusted friend," Rajyavardhan said, unaware of the betrayal to come. Shashank then treacherously murdered Rajyavardhan. 

Because of the battle, everything was disorganized in Devgupta's prison. The single soldier left to guard the prison possessed a noble heart. He opened a secret passage for Rajyashri. "This secret passage will carry you to the jungle." 

Thus Rajyashri escaped from Devgupta's prison. Devgupta's soldiers were patrolling the jungle, but luckily she was not spotted by them. 

Harsha, meanwhile, was devastated by the news of his brother's death. "I pledge that I will uproot the cause of these sufferings," he vowed, taking the throne. 

He searched the Vindhya forests for his sister. Disturbed over her fate and believing there was nobody to help her, Rajyashri decided to end her life. Just as she was about to step into a fire, Harsha arrived. "Stop! Sister stop!" 

"Brother Harsha!" she wept, collapsing into his arms. "Your bad days are now over, dear sister." 

Very soon Harsha conquered the various small kingdoms to bring lasting peace in Northern India. "Now that all the kingdoms have come under one flag, there would be no more battles between them. Poor peasants will live in peace at last," he declared. 

Unaware of Harsha's peace plans, the great South Indian king, Pulakeshin, was worried about the rising strength of Harsha. "Harsha has won kingdoms as far as Kathiawar. He may attack us one day. We must attack before he does," Pulakeshin decided. 

As Harsha was returning home after conquering Kathiawar, Pulakeshin attacked him. The fighting continued for several days. Losses were heavy on both sides. 

Harsha's tired soldiers had to retreat. He was very sad. "All those men were killed for nothing. I have had enough of this blood-shed. There shall be no more wars," he resolved. "In future, I shall use my strength to protect the weak and the poor." 

To fulfil his dream, Harsha gave financial help to the poor. He appointed physicians to treat the sick free of cost. He helped students to pursue their studies. People prospered, and followers of different faiths lived in peace and harmony. 

During this period, Hiuen Tsang, the famous Chinese pilgrim and scholar, came to India. After spending some time in Eastern India, he met Harsha. 

"Welcome, scholar Hiuen Tsang. I was looking forward to meeting you," Harsha greeted him warmly. 

"Sir, I have come here to learn more about the Buddhist religion," Hiuen Tsang said. He stayed in India for a few years. 

During Hiuen Tsang's stay, Harsha decided to hold a seminar on religions. Eminent scholars of Hinduism, Jainism and Buddhism came to participate. The seminar continued for several days. 

"Acharya Hiuen Tsang, you are the most noble scholar I have ever known," Harsha praised him. 

But some of the greedy participants did not like that a foreigner should be praised by their king. "If Hiuen Tsang makes a good impression on the king, we may not get those rich rewards, we expect," they muttered. "Yes, he must be finished before he does more harm." 

But this conspiracy was reported to Harsha immediately. "I warn the conspirators that anybody who tries to harm Acharya Hiuen Tsang shall be punished. Proclaim this to all," he declared from his throne. As a result of this stern warning, nobody tried to harm Hiuen Tsang. 

From the next day, Harsha started presenting gifts to the scholars and priests of different religions. Then came the turn of poor peasants. Soon the gifts brought for donation were exhausted, but still many people remained without gifts. 

"Bring all the remaining wealth from the royal treasury," Harsha ordered. The royal wealth was also given away, but even then some people were left out. 

In the end only one man was left out. "I am sorry that nothing is left with me except the clothes I am wearing. Please accept them," Harsha said, offering his ornaments and royal garments. 

"Sister Rajyashri, can you lend me a cloth so that I can donate my dress?" Harsha asked his sister. 

"Yes, brother," she smiled, handing him a simple cloth. 

In this manner, every five years, Harsha gave away all his wealth to the scholars, the priests and the poor. "Long live Maha-raj Harsha!" the people cheered. 

After completing his studies, Hiuen Tsang went back to China. He carried with him memoirs of his visit which became world famous as testimonies of our great ancient heritage. 

"I have known many kings but there is none like Harsha," Hiuen Tsang wrote. "I have seen many countries but there is none like Bharat. Truly Bharat is a great country and her greatness lies in her people and in her rulers like Harsha."
