# The Terrible Bell

One afternoon, when all the men of Brahmapura were away at work in the fields, a thief sneaked into the village temple. 

"Oh! Bad luck! The inner doors are closed," the thief cursed. "The only thing I can take away is the bell."

The thief unhooked the large, heavy brass bell from the ceiling and ran towards the hills. "I'll cross the hilltop and sell this in another village. It should fetch a good price," he plotted. 

But that was not to be. For as soon as he entered the dense forest in the hills, a massive tiger leaped from the bushes. "A tiger! Help! Save me! E-e-ah!" the thief screamed.

The tiger killed him instantly, dropping the bell in the grass as he dragged the thief's body away. 

The next morning, a troop of curious monkeys came swinging by. "What's this?" one monkey asked, poking the shiny brass object. 

"I've seen one like it before," another monkey said, picking it up. "It makes a funny sound if you shake it."

The monkey shook it hard. *TING! TING! CLANG! CLANG!*

"I bet I can make a louder sound!" the first monkey challenged, snatching it back. He shook it violently. *CLANG! CLANG! CLANG!*

The entire hilly region echoed with the loud, ringing of the bell. 

Sometime later, two travellers were on their way to Brahmapura. "Brother, do you see that?" one pointed to the bushes. 

"Yes, the bones of a man," the other gulped. 

Suddenly, a terrifying *CLANG, CLANG* erupted from the trees above them. 

"Run, brother, run! That must be a demon!" they screamed, dropping their bags and breaking into a dead sprint. 

When they reached Brahmapura, they were completely out of breath. "Why have you been running?" the villagers asked. 

"There is a demon on the hill. He makes a terrifying noise," the travellers gasped. "And we saw the bones of a man killed by him."

Just then, a faint *CLANG, CLANG* echoed down from the distant hills. 

"There! Can you hear it? Yes, it must be the demon!" the villagers panicked. 

Wild rumors quickly spread about the cruel demon. "His ears ring like bells as he swings them about!" one man claimed. "He is the demon Ghantakarna! He is a killer!"

But one of the villagers, a wise old woman, listened carefully. *I refuse to believe there's a demon there, unless I see him with my own eyes,* she thought. *The noise sounds exactly like the clanging of a bell. I'm sure some monkeys have somehow got hold of a bell, and...*

Meanwhile, the terrified villagers went to their Headman. "Sir, there is a demon on the hill. We are afraid he'll come here and kill us. We want to leave the village."

As the Headman wondered what to do, the wise woman spoke up. "Sir, pay me some money and I will chase the demon away."

The Headman could not believe his ears. "You will chase him away?" 

"Yes."

He turned to his assistant. "Give her all the money she wants."

The woman took the money, went out, and bought a large sack of delicious, ripe mangoes. She set out for the hill alone. In the forest, she scattered the mangoes all over the ground and quickly hid behind a thick tree. 

"If my guess is right, the 'demon' will come out!" she smiled. 

The monkeys up in the trees smelt the sweet mangoes and came scampering down. In their mad dash to grab the fruit, the monkey holding the bell completely forgot about it and dropped it on the grass. 

While the monkeys were busy stuffing their faces with mangoes, the woman crept out from her hiding place, grabbed the bell, and ran all the way back to the village. 

She walked straight to the Headman's house and placed the bell on the floor. "Here is your demon! The terrible sound came from this harmless temple bell."

The Headman laughed in pure relief. "It was clever of you to have guessed the truth. You will be rewarded."

The Headman rewarded her handsomely, and from that day on, she became the most respected person in all of Brahmapura.
