# Greed Does Not Pay

There once was a poor Brahmana who had a loving wife and three young daughters. He loved them dearly and took excellent care of them. But suddenly, one day, he tragically died. 

"How could he leave us and go? Who will care for us now?" his widow wept bitterly. 

Kind neighbours rushed in to comfort the family. "We will. We won't let you starve. Now please wipe your tears."

But their life became one of deep poverty, living entirely on the charity of others. 

Meanwhile, because of his good deeds, the Brahmana was reborn as a magnificent, magical golden swan. One day, swimming in a beautiful lake, he remembered his past life. 

"I must go and see how my wife and daughters are getting along," he thought. 

He flew to his old village and peeked into the small hut. "They live on charity! How degrading! I must do something to help them."

He landed in the courtyard. "Mother! Look! A golden bird! Let's catch it!" his youngest daughter cheered. 

"Wait!" the swan spoke. "I am your husband, reborn as a bird. I can't bear to see your plight. I'll give you one of my golden feathers. Sell it and use the money to buy food and clothing."

He plucked out one of his pure golden feathers, gave it to her, and flew away. 

Week after week, the magical swan returned to give his family yet another golden feather. The widow sold them all, and very soon, the family became incredibly rich. 

But, alas, like most people who suddenly acquire wealth, the widow became fiercely greedy. 

"Suppose he stops coming?" she plotted one day. "A mere bird is not to be trusted. The next time he comes, I'll pluck out *all* his feathers."

"No, mother! You can't do that!" her daughters gasped in horror. "You can't mean it, mother!"

But the widow had made up her mind. The next time the kind bird came to help his family, she lunged forward, caught him tightly by the neck, and despite all his desperate struggling, violently plucked him clean! 

"There! Now we'll never be poor again!" she laughed, holding a massive bundle of golden feathers. 

But as she looked down at the pile in her hands, her jaw dropped in absolute shock. The feathers were no longer gold. They were plain, ordinary white. 

"Alas! What have you done!" the naked swan wept. 

"I've been cheated!" the widow screamed. "They're just ordinary white feathers! H-how did it happen?"

"I'll tell you how," the shivering bird explained. "My golden feathers turned white because you... you plucked them against my will! I forgot to warn you never to do that."

"You forgot? How could you be so careless! We are ruined!" the cruel widow shrieked. "That's where a useless bird belongs... the dustbin! One of you give him something to eat."

She violently threw the poor swan into a dirty basket in the corner. 

Within months, the swan's feathers grew back. But they were all perfectly white. 

"What's the use of staying on here? I'd better go away," the swan sighed, flying out the window forever. 

As for the greedy wife, without the golden feathers, she soon spent all her hoarded money and fell right back into terrible poverty, a fate she well and truly deserved.
