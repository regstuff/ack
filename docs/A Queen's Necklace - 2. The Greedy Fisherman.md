# The Greedy Fisherman

One bright morning, a fisherman and his young son went down to the riverbank to fish. The fisherman threw his line into the water and waited patiently. After some time, he felt a massive tug on the line. 

"Wow! I've got one!" he cheered, wrestling with his fishing rod. But the fish was incredibly strong. "I can't pull him in. He must be as big as a mountain! I'll wade into the water and pull this fish out."

Turning to his son, the fisherman grinned greedily. "Son, go tell your mother I have caught a big one! And... tell her to pick a quarrel with the neighbours."

The boy looked confused. "Quarrel with the neighbours? Why?"

"Because if I don't quarrel with them, they'll come running in when your father brings the fish, and then we'll have to share it with them," he explained. 

The boy ran all the way home and delivered the strange message to his mother. "Mother, father wants you to quarrel with the neighbours. He has caught a big fish."

"I would never dream of quarrelling with my dear neighbours..." the woman began, before pausing. "But you know how greedy our neighbours are. They'll want the best parts. Now run along."

Determined to hoard the entire fish for her family, the woman hatched a bizarre plan to make her neighbors hate her. She rubbed dark soot all over her face, stuck a large green leaf behind her ear, and stepped out of the house. 

"Good neighbours are more precious than all the jewels in the world," an elderly neighbor woman was just saying to her friend. "And all the..." 

She stopped, staring in shock at the fisherman's wife. "Good Lord, what have you done to yourself? Has she gone mad?"

"Who are you calling 'mad', you nitwit?" the fisherman's wife snapped loudly, picking the fight exactly as planned.

"Who's calling whom a nitwit?" the elderly woman shot back, enraged. 

"Don't you dare stare at me with those big eyes of yours!" the fisherman's wife yelled.

In no time at all, a massive, screaming quarrel flared up between the women. They made such a terrible noise that the town Kotwal (police chief) arrived with his guards to break it up. He immediately dragged them both away to the magistrate's court.

The magistrate patiently heard both sides of the ridiculous argument. Finally, he slammed his gavel and pointed at the fisherman's wife. "You are the one who started the quarrel. You will pay a fine of eight coins!"

Meanwhile, down by the river, things had not gone well for the greedy fisherman. In his struggle to catch the massive fish, he had been pulled into the mud and lost his catch entirely. Soaked, bruised, and empty-handed, he limped back into town, only to hear the town children laughing and jeering. 

He looked up to see his wife being escorted home by the Kotwal, looking utterly dejected. 

"What happened?" the fisherman gasped. "Where are your clothes? What's wrong with your eyes?"

"You and your brilliant ideas," his wife sobbed, wiping the soot from her face. "I was fined eight coins for starting that quarrel!"

The fisherman clutched his head in despair. He had lost the fish, and now he had to pay a massive fine. "I could have bought four large fish with eight coins! I hope the fish my husband has caught is worth all this trouble and expense," she cried.

The fisherman groaned. "All this has happened because of our greed. We wanted to keep the whole fish to ourselves. Now we have nothing!"
