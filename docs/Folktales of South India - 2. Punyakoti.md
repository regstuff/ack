# Punyakoti

Hulia the tiger was weak with hunger. "He had not eaten anything for days. If I don't find some food today, I'll die of hunger," he thought miserably. 

Just then, he heard the faint sound of bells. *TIN-TIN* 

"What's that?" Hulia looked down from the hillock. "The cattle returning home after grazing!" he realized with joy. 

He sprang down towards the herd. But the herd was too fast, and he missed. "What's wrong with me? I couldn't catch a silly cow!" he grumbled. Then he noticed a single cow lagging behind. "Who do I see coming this way?" 

It was a gentle cow called Punyakoti. "I'd better walk faster. My child must be waiting for me," she thought. 

"EH!" Hulia jumped into her path. "Yes, I've been waiting for you." 

"Hulia, please listen..." Punyakoti pleaded. 

"No! I won't! I am going to kill you and eat you up." 

"Kill me! Eat me! But not immediately. My child is waiting for me. I'll go home, feed him and come back to you." 

"WHAT! HOHOHO!" Hulia roared with laughter. "Do you take me for a fool? As if you'll come back, if I let you go!" 

"Believe me, Hulia, I will. I give you my word with Mother Earth as a witness." 

Hulia looked at her earnest eyes. "All right. You may go. But come back soon." 

"I will, Hulia, I will," Punyakoti promised. 

Punyakoti ran to her home at the foot of the hill. "Come, my child! Drink, my child. Drink as much milk as you can. For this is the last time I will be feeding you." 

"Mother!" the calf cried, sensing her sorrow. 

"Yes, my son. I have to go back to Hulia. He will be waiting for me." Punyakoti told him everything about her promise to Hulia. 

"But mother, you don't have to go back to Hulia. He's right. We will avoid that route, Hulia can't do a thing," the other cows advised her. 

"I must go. I promised him that I would return. Truth is my mother. Truth is my father. Truth is my God. Truth is everything to me." 

"But mother, who will feed me when I'm hungry? Who will take care of me?" the calf wept. 

Punyakoti turned to the herd. "My sisters, treat this orphan as your child. Please don't gore him with your horns if he comes in your way and please don't kick him if he is at your back. O Punyakoti!" 

"Punyakoti, we will all come with you to Hulia," they offered. 

"Please don't. I want you to take care of my child," she insisted. 

As Punyakoti left, her calf cried, "Mother, don't go! Please don't go, mother..." But she walked steadily back up the hill. 

Meanwhile, Hulia was getting impatient. "I shouldn't have let her go. She'll never come... no, there she is!" he exclaimed in surprise as Punyakoti appeared. 

"She has kept her promise... even though death awaits her here. What a noble creature!" Hulia thought, deeply moved. 

"Hulia, my brother, come! Here I am. Eat me," Punyakoti offered herself. 

Hulia looked at her. "Eat you? Never, my noble sister. Never. Go back to your child." 

"Hulia!" Punyakoti was stunned as Hulia turned back and left. And so, the truthful Punyakoti happily rejoined her child.
