# The Learned Pandit

One day, several men were being ferried across a wide, turbulent river. Among them was an extremely learned Pandit, who looked around at his fellow passengers with mild disdain. 

*I'll talk to one of these fellows,* the Pandit thought to himself. *Not that it will benefit me in any way, but it will at least help me pass the time.*

He cleared his throat and looked at a simple man sitting near him. "Who are you? Where do you come from?"

"I am a farmer, good sir, from a village on the other side of the river," the man replied politely. 

The Pandit puffed out his chest. "I am a scholar. I have made a deep study of the Upanishads. Have you read the Upanishads?"

The farmer shook his head. "No, sir, I haven't."

The Pandit gasped in exaggerated horror. "You haven't? You poor fellow! A quarter of your life has been completely wasted! Well, you must have read the Shastras then?"

"No, sir. I can't say that I have."

"Then half your life is gone!" the Pandit declared dramatically. "What about the six systems of philosophy?"

"I am afraid I haven't even heard of them, sir," the farmer said humbly. 

"Not heard of them!" the Pandit cried. "Then, my friend, three-fourths of your life is..." 

Suddenly, a massive gust of wind rocked the small boat. 

"Hey! The boat! It's..." the boatman yelled in panic. Dark clouds had rolled in, and the river was churning violently. "It's a storm! I can't control the boat. It's going to turn over! Jump overboard and swim to the shore!"

As the boat began to capsize, the farmer turned to the terrified scholar. "Good sir, I hope you know how to swim."

The Pandit’s eyes went wide with sheer terror. "N-n-no! I've never learnt swimming in my life!"

The farmer shook his head in pity as he prepared to dive into the water. "Never learnt swimming? Then your *whole* life is gone, Panditji! Knowledge derived from books is not enough. One must acquire practical learning too."
