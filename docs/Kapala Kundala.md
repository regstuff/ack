# Kapala Kundala

During the reign of Akbar the Great, a lone boat was returning from a voyage of pilgrimage to Gangasagar. At the mouth of the River Hooghly, a dense fog descended, and the boat lost its way. 

"We are lost! If we strike against a rock, it will be worse if we are washed ashore. Then we'll be at the mercy of wild beasts in the jungles," the passengers panicked. 

When the fog cleared, they disembarked on the shore. "We are tired and hungry. Let us eat first and then rest," one said. "But how shall we cook? We have no firewood." 

Navakumar, a brave young man, volunteered. "I will try. But will someone come with me?" When no one dared, Navakumar went into the forest alone. 

While he was gathering wood, the tide came in. The passengers on the boat realized they would be stranded. "We should wait for a little while longer... Can come back for Navakumar later, when the storm has abated," they decided, abandoning him. 

The stormy waters drove them far off the coast. Navakumar returned to find the boat gone. "No sign of them! They have been swept away by the sea!" Or, perhaps, they've deserted me! 

Looking for shelter, Navakumar saw a fire flickering in the distance. He found a Kapalik (a votary of the Goddess Kali who offers human sacrifice) meditating before the fire. 

"I am a Brahman. I need food and shelter," Navakumar asked. 

The Kapalik took him to his cottage. "You can eat this fruit and sleep here. But don't leave the cottage till I return." Navakumar slept till the next evening. 

When he awoke, the Kapalik had gone again. Navakumar wandered into the forest and lost his way. Suddenly, he met a beautiful maiden. "I am Kapala Kundala. Come with me!" she said, leading him to a cottage and then disappearing. 

The next day, Navakumar encountered her again. "Don't go with him! He is going to sacrifice you to Goddess Kali," she whispered urgently, and fled like a frightened deer. 

The Kapalik caught Navakumar. "You cannot free yourself from my grip," the strong Kapalik dragged him to the place of sacrifice and bound him firmly. 

"Don't try to resist... But where is my scimitar? I must have dropped it while he was struggling with me," the Kapalik muttered, going to look for it. 

When he was gone, Kapala Kundala suddenly appeared with the scimitar. "Sh-shh! Don't make a noise! He might hear us. I'll free you in no time," she whispered, cutting his bonds. 

She led Navakumar to a temple in the forest and told the priest the whole story. "Is there anything I can do to save her from the Kapalik?" Navakumar asked. 

"Yes. You can marry her, and take her with you. She is a Brahman. She was brought up by the Kapalik," the priest explained. "I would be happy to do so." 

So Navakumar and Kapala Kundala became husband and wife. The next day, the priest escorted them to the nearest village. "Here is some money for your expenses on the way. Hire a palanquin for Kapala Kundala." 

After the priest left, a palanquin was hired. Navakumar walked behind, but gradually lost pace. By nightfall, he was worried about dacoits. 

He stumbled upon a broken palanquin. "Kapala Kundala! Is it you?" Looking inside, he saw a lady whose hands and feet were bound. "Oh! It's not Kapala!" 

He untied her. "My servants had just left me and gone ahead to make preparations at the village inn... when suddenly dacoits attacked. They took away all my belongings, while my palanquin-bearers fled." 

"How shall I reach the inn now?" she asked. 

"You can come with me. I am going to the inn to join my wife. I hope she is safe," Navakumar offered. 

They reached the inn. "Ah! My wife has arrived already. Thank God she is safe." 

Navakumar introduced the lady. "This is my wife, Kapala Kundala. I am Navakumar Sharma of Saptagram." 

"My name is Mati Bibi. I have come to thank you," the lady said. She offered valuable ornaments to Kapala Kundala as tokens of gratitude. "Please accept these ornaments... I still have some left in the luggage which my men had already taken to the inn." 

When Mati Bibi returned to her room, her maid questioned her. "Why did you give such valuable jewellery to a stranger?" 

"He is not a stranger. He is my husband," Mati Bibi revealed. "Yes. I was married to him. But when my father became a convert to Islam, his father decided to spurn me." 

Mati Bibi, whose real name was Shyama, was now the rival of the famous Mehr-unnisa for the Prince's heart in the Mughal court, known as Lutf-unnisa. 

A few days later, Navakumar arrived at Saptagram with his bride, receiving a joyous welcome. His family renamed Kapala Kundala as 'Mrinmayee'. 

At first, Kapala Kundala was not happy. "One has to work all the time here. How I miss my care-free life in the forest!" she confided in her sister-in-law. 

In the meantime, Mati Bibi had returned to Agra. But her thoughts were still with Navakumar. When Emperor Akbar died and Prince Salim was to be crowned, she decided to leave Agra. "Let him marry Mehr-unnisa. I am leaving Agra right now! I will go to Saptagram," she declared. 

She summoned Navakumar. "Take me as your wife," she pleaded. 

"That cannot be. A poor Brahman cannot take a rich woman of the court as his maid," he refused. 

"You can't escape me. When I want something, I get it," she threatened angrily. Her gesture revived a childhood memory in Navakumar. "Just because I am your wife you cannot order me around like this!" she had said then. "Padmavati! My God!" he realized. 

Disguised as a man, Mati Bibi went to the forest near Navakumar's house to wait for an opportunity. There, she saw the Kapalik! He had survived a fall from a cliff but lost an arm. 

"I am interested in Kapala Kundala too. What have you against her?" she asked the Kapalik. 

"I brought her up, but she deceived me. She ran away and married Navakumar. I want her to die." 

"I also desire her death. I will tell you how to bring about her death. Listen," Mati Bibi plotted. 

Mati Bibi, disguised as a man, approached Mrinmayee at night. "The herb must be plucked by a woman exactly at midnight. And she must be alone," she told Mrinmayee, luring her into the forest. 

Navakumar, suspecting his wife was meeting a lover, followed her into the forest. He met the Kapalik. "She comes here to meet this young man!" the Kapalik fueled his jealousy. "You displeased the Goddess by running away. Now you can atone by sacrificing your wife to Kali." 

Blinded by jealousy, Navakumar agreed. "I'll do it. Yes, she is unfaithful to me." 

He confronted Mrinmayee. "You have been unfaithful to me. I am taking you to the Kapalik." 

"That's not true. However, I don't wish to defend myself... I'd rather die than be suspected by you." 

As they neared the place of sacrifice, Navakumar suddenly came to his senses. "That is the man who wanted to kill me! What have I done! I have killed an innocent woman. I can atone only by my own death." 

Mrinmayee stopped him. "And this is the woman who saved me! How can I kill her at his suggestion?" Navakumar realized. 

"Mrinmayee! Why don't you show me the light? Why don't you tell me that what I saw was an illusion?" 

"It was! You thought I was meeting a man, but it was a woman in disguise!" Mrinmayee explained. 

"I believe you, Mrinmayee. Forgive me. I am not worthy of you," Navakumar apologized deeply. "The forest makes us take leave of our senses. Let's go away from here quickly." 

It had all started in the forest and was about to end in the forest. We do not know what happened afterwards to the evil Kapalik and Mati Bibi. They were left in the dark while Navakumar and Kapala Kundala came back to the light.
