# Caution Pays

In a dense forest near Varanasi, there lived an antelope which was very fond of the sweet fruits of a particular tree. 

In a village nearby lived a hunter, who had noticed the antelope's habits. Early one morning, the hunter went into the jungle. "Hmm, he is a big animal. I will get quite some money by selling his meat," the hunter schemed. He climbed the fruit tree, set up a 'machan' (a hidden platform), and waited for the antelope. "He should be here any moment."

As usual, the antelope came strolling along the forest path. "Mm...m...m... Just thinking of that fruit makes me hungry," he thought happily. Suddenly, he stopped in his tracks, his keen eyes spotting something unusual on the ground. "Hey! A man's footprints!"

"There must be a hunter nearby, waiting to kill me," he reasoned cautiously. He looked around but couldn't see anyone. 

Up in the tree, the impatient hunter wondered, "Why doesn't he come near? I shall try throwing some fruit to him. Maybe that will lure him to the tree." He plucked a fruit and tossed it down. 

The fruit landed near the antelope. "Oh oh, there is someone up the tree," the antelope realized. "The fruit is being thrown to me. It isn't dropping straight down as it usually does." He carefully examined the canopy above. "Yes. Now I am sure. I can see the corner of a 'machan'."

The clever antelope decided to play a trick. "I'll pretend to talk to the tree and see what happens," he thought. He looked up and spoke loudly. "Listen, tree. You have always dropped your fruit straight down on the earth. Why do you throw it at me today?"

There was complete silence from the tree. 

"Hmmm. No answer. He thinks he is smart, does he?" the antelope chuckled to himself. He spoke again, louder this time. "And so tree, since you have stopped behaving like a normal tree, I too will behave differently. I shall eat my breakfast elsewhere!"

Realizing that his cover was blown and he had lost his chance, the frustrated hunter threw his javelin at the antelope with all his might. *Wooosh!* 

The javelin embedded itself in the dirt, completely missing its target. "Hee...hee, too bad, hunter! Better luck next time," the antelope laughed, bounding away safely into the thick forest.
