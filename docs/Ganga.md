# Ganga

Ganga, beautiful, pure, and strong-willed, was the elder daughter of Mount Himavan. She had the divine power of purifying anything that touched her. 

One day, the Devas, led by Brahma, came to Mount Himavan. "Heaven needs the presence of young Ganga, sanctifier of all creatures," Brahma requested. 

"If Ganga goes, the earth will suffer. But it is my duty to look to the good of all the three worlds," Himavan thought. He agreed, saying, "I am willing to send Ganga, who flows everywhere at will, purifying all in her wake, with you." 

"May we take her with us now?" the Devas asked. 

"Go with the Devas, Ganga, and serve them dutifully," Himavan blessed his daughter. 

At about that time, life on Earth was becoming impossible for all righteous people. A mysterious group of Asuras hid in the ocean bed by day and came out to harass them at night, destroying crops and ruining sacrifices. 

In heaven, the Devas were perturbed. "We must help the good people on Earth. But how can we, when we don't know who their tormentors are?" 

They sought the help of Lord Vishnu. "To kill them you will have to dry up the ocean. Go to Sage Agastya. He alone can help you," Vishnu advised. 

The Devas approached Sage Agastya, who readily agreed. He went to the shore, bent down, and began to drink up the entire ocean! As the waters vanished, the evil Asuras were exposed on the dry ocean bed. 

"Don't spare the murderers!" the Devas cried, falling upon them and vanquishing the Asuras. 

When the battle was won, they turned to Agastya. "O saviour. Now that the evil ones are dead, fill up the ocean again with the water you drank." 

"I cannot," Agastya replied. "I have digested it. You will have to find some other means to fill the ocean." 

The Devas went to Brahma in dismay. "A very long time must pass before the ocean will once again be full," Brahma prophesied. "King Sagara and his descendants will cause the ocean to be filled up again. He has already begun penances to obtain them." 

At that moment on earth, Lord Shiva appeared before King Sagara and his queens, Keshini and Sumati. "I am pleased with your piety. You shall have many sons," Shiva blessed them. "One of your wives shall bear 60,000 proud and heroic sons who will all perish without heirs. Your race however will be continued by the one son that your other wife will bear." 

Accordingly, Queen Keshini gave birth to Asamanja, a wicked prince who was eventually banished. But Asamanja had a son, Amsuman, who was kind and loved by the people. 

Queen Sumati gave birth to 60,000 strong and arrogant princes. 

One day, King Sagara decided to perform the Ashwamedha sacrifice (the horse sacrifice) to declare his supreme power. A sacred horse was let loose, meant to roam freely. Anyone who stopped it would have to fight Sagara's army. 

Indra, afraid that Sagara's growing power might dethrone him, stole the sacrificial horse. He hid it in the nether world, right behind Sage Kapila, who was deep in meditation. 

When Sagara's 60,000 sons could not find the horse on Earth, they dug a massive tunnel down to the nether world. The digging caused tremendous earthquakes. 

In the nether world, they found the horse grazing peacefully near Sage Kapila. "So this sage is the culprit. Thief! Return our horse!" the arrogant princes yelled, rushing to attack the meditating sage. 

Angered by their audacity, the powerful Kapila opened his eyes. Fire blazed from his gaze, and he burned all 60,000 princes to ashes by uttering but one syllable! 

Meanwhile, King Sagara, worried about his missing sons, sent his grandson Amsuman to look for them. Amsuman followed the tunnel and found the horse... and the massive pile of ashes that used to be his uncles. 

He saluted Sage Kapila respectfully. "I have seen them. They were so insolent that I consumed them with the fire of my penances," Kapila explained. 

"But do they deserve to suffer in hell too? Is there no way for them to enter heaven?" Amsuman pleaded. 

"There is. Ganga in all her purity must be brought down from heaven. If she touches these ashes, your uncles will go to heaven," Kapila told him. "You may take the sacrificial horse back with you." 

Amsuman returned with the horse, and the sacrifice was completed. Later, Amsuman became king, but despite his efforts, he failed to bring Ganga down to earth in his lifetime. His son, Dilipa, also tried and failed, dying of a broken heart. 

Dilipa's son, Bhagiratha, undertook severe penances to Brahma, standing on one leg in the forest for years. 

At last, pleased by his single-mindedness, Brahma appeared. "I shall send Ganga down to earth to redeem your ancestors," Brahma granted. "But the earth cannot sustain the force of her fall. Only Shiva can withstand it. Perform penances to please Shiva." 

For a whole year, Bhagiratha adored Shiva. Finally, Shiva appeared. "I will receive the force of Ganga as she descends to Earth," Shiva promised. 

In heaven, Ganga made ready to descend. Halfway down, a proud thought occurred to her. "The mighty one is off his guard. I shall sweep him away with me to the nether world!" 

But Shiva divined her intentions. As the turbulent Ganga crashed down from the heavens, Shiva caught her in his matted locks. He imprisoned her there, swirling about, unable to escape. 

Bhagiratha once again prayed to Shiva. "Lord! You are the source of all kindness. Have pity on me and release Ganga." 

"Your devotion merits it. For your sake I will," Shiva agreed, releasing the river. 

Ganga, now purified by her contact with the Lord's head, flowed gently onto the earth. Bhagiratha led her towards the ocean. 

But Bhagiratha's trials were not yet over. On the way, the irrepressible Ganga rushed forward and flooded the sacrificial fire of Sage Jahnu! 

The sage was furious. "You shall be punished for this!" In one long gulp, the fuming sage drank up the entire river! 

Bhagiratha and the gods pleaded with the sage. "Forgive her, holy one, and release her. Let not the endeavours of Bhagiratha be wasted." 

Out of respect for Bhagiratha's penances, Jahnu relented. He released Ganga from his ear. "Henceforth, Ganga shall also be known as Jahnavi—your daughter, O holy one," the gods declared. 

At last, Bhagiratha and Ganga reached the ocean bed and the nether world beyond. As Ganga's holy waters flowed over the ashes of the 60,000 sons of Sagara, their souls were redeemed and ascended to heaven. 

The vast, dry ocean bed was filled with Ganga's waters. From that day onwards, the ocean came to be known as "Sagara" in honour of the king. 

Brahma appeared before Bhagiratha one last time. "Since you brought Ganga to earth by your penances, she shall also be known as Bhagirathi—your daughter," Brahma blessed him. 

His mission accomplished, Bhagiratha returned to his kingdom, ascended the throne, and ruled wisely for many years.
