# Krishna: The Enchanter

Pareekshit asked Sage Shukadeva, "What happened to Mathura... while Krishna led Kalayavana to his death?" 

"Balarama and his army... decimated the entire army of Kalayavana," Shukadeva explained. They took all the treasure to Dwaraka. 

Then Jarasandha attacked Mathura for the eighteenth time. With their people safely in Dwaraka, Krishna and Balarama pretended to be afraid and fled to the Pravarshana hill. Jarasandha surrounded the hill and set it on fire. But Krishna and Balarama leaped to safety and returned to Dwaraka, leaving Jarasandha convinced he had destroyed his enemies. 

In due time, Balarama married Revati. 

Krishna had set his heart on Rukmini, the lovely daughter of the King of Vidarbha. However, her brother, Rukmi, hated Krishna and arranged for her to marry Shishupala, the Prince of Chedi. 

Heartbroken, Rukmini sent a trusted old Brahman to Krishna with a message. "I have chosen you for my husband... Come to Vidarbha the day before the wedding and take me away forcibly," she pleaded. 

Krishna immediately set out for Kundinapura, the capital of Vidarbha. On the day before the wedding, as Rukmini emerged from the temple of Goddess Parvati, she saw Krishna waiting. "And lifting her up, placed her on his chariot," speeding away with her. 

Rukmi chased after them, vowing to kill Krishna. "But Krishna destroyed all of them with ease." Krishna was about to kill Rukmi, but Rukmini pleaded for her brother's life. Krishna spared him, but tied him up and shaved patches of his hair to humiliate him. 

Balarama intervened. "Disfiguring a relative is as bad as killing him," he chided Krishna, and released Rukmi. Disgraced, Rukmi could not return to his capital and built a new city called Bhojkat instead. 

Krishna and Rukmini were formally married in Dwaraka with great pomp. 

"Pradyumna was very good-looking and was in fact an incarnation of Kamadeva, the God of Love." When Pradyumna was just ten days old, a demon named Shambarasura kidnapped him and threw him into the sea, knowing the child was destined to be his foe. 

A huge fish swallowed the baby. The fish was caught and presented to Shambarasura. The cook, Mayavati, found the infant alive inside the fish! Narada appeared and revealed the baby's identity to Mayavati, who was actually an incarnation of Rati, Kamadeva's wife. 

Mayavati raised Pradyumna in secret. When he grew up, she told him his true identity and taught him the magical arts needed to defeat Shambarasura. 

Pradyumna challenged the demon. "Shambarasura rose rapidly in the air and began to rain weapons down... With the help of Maha-Maya, Pradyumna could counter all the wicked moves of the Asura. Finally he took up a sharp sword... and cut off Shambarasura's head." 

Mayavati took Pradyumna back to Dwaraka, where he was joyfully reunited with his parents, Krishna and Rukmini. 

Later, Satrajit, a devotee of Surya (the Sun God), received the legendary Syamantaka gem. The gem produced gold every day and protected against disease and famine. Krishna suggested Satrajit give it to King Ugrasena, but Satrajit refused. 

One day, Satrajit's brother Prasena went hunting wearing the gem. He was killed by a lion, which in turn was killed by Jambavan, the bear-king. Jambavan took the gem for his child. 

In Dwaraka, rumors spread that Krishna had murdered Prasena for the gem. "I must clear my name," Krishna resolved. 

Krishna followed Prasena's trail, found the dead lion, and traced the tracks to Jambavan's cave. He fought Jambavan continuously for 28 days. Finally, Jambavan realized he was fighting his Lord. "I bear you no grudge, Jambavan," Krishna said. 

Jambavan offered Krishna the gem and his daughter, Jambavati, in marriage. 

Krishna returned to Dwaraka with Jambavati and the gem, clearing his name. He gave the gem back to Satrajit. Embarrassed, Satrajit offered his daughter Satyabhama to Krishna in marriage, along with the gem. Krishna accepted Satyabhama but let Satrajit keep the gem. 

However, Shatadhanva, another Yadava who had hoped to marry Satyabhama, was angry. In the dead of night, he killed Satrajit and stole the gem. 

Satyabhama went to Krishna, who was in Hastinapura. Krishna and Balarama returned to pursue Shatadhanva. Frightened, Shatadhanva left the gem with Akrura and fled. 

Krishna chased Shatadhanva, eventually running him down and killing him. "But when they searched him for the gem—Oh! He doesn't have the gem. We need not have killed him," Krishna realized. 

Akrura, fearing for his life, had fled the city. Krishna sent for him. "Uncle, I know that Shatadhanva left the gem with you," Krishna said. 

Akrura produced the gem. Krishna showed it to the people of Dwaraka to finally clear his name of all doubts, and then graciously returned the gem to Akrura to keep.
