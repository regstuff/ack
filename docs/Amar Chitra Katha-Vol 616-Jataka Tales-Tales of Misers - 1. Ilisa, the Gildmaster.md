# Ilisa, the Gildmaster

Long ago, there lived an incredibly wealthy man named Ilisa. As the local Gildmaster, he had more money than he could ever possibly spend, but he was notoriously tight-fisted. 

One day, while out on a walk, he realized he was late getting home. "I've stayed out longer than I should," he grumbled, hobbling along with his walking stick. "I hope my wife has not bought anything for herself in my absence. She is so extravagant."

He practically jogged, fueled by pure paranoia. "If I didn't keep a constant watch on them they would eat me out of house and home! And as for my worthless children, all they can think of is eating."

"Help a hungry man, sir," an emaciated beggar pleaded, holding out a bowl as Ilisa walked past. 

"Out of my way, you wretch! You won't get a thing from me!" Ilisa yelled, raising his heavy stick and driving the poor man away. "Everyone is after my money."

As he walked through the marketplace, the sweet, irresistible smell of freshly fried *jalebis* hit his nose. He saw a man happily eating a plate of the golden, syrupy sweets. 

"What luscious jalebis!" Ilisa gasped, his mouth instantly watering. 

He stared at a sweetmeat stall, agonizing over the purchase. "But if I buy some here, I'll have to share them with my acquaintances," he reasoned, seeing a group of men sitting nearby. "Tcha! If I send my servant to buy some and bring them home, I'll have to share them with my wife and children."

Unable to control his intense craving, he hatched an elaborate plan. "Unless I eat them somewhere else," he plotted. 

He rushed home, grabbed his servant, and gave him strict instructions. "Go and buy some jalebis from the sweetmeat shop. But don't bring them here! Take them to the thick bushes near the lake. I'll wait there for you, hidden from everyone."

Meanwhile, as Ilisa was making his ridiculous, elaborate plans to eat jalebis in secret, a truly unbelievable event was unfolding at the royal palace. 

A man who was Ilisa's exact, perfect likeness had just presented himself before the King. 

"What brings you here, Gildmaster?" the King asked. 

"I have come to seek your permission... to give away some of my great wealth to the poor," the impostor announced warmly. 

"What!" the King gasped in absolute shock. "You want to give away wealth?" 

"Yes, Maharaj. Hoarded wealth is of no use to anyone."

"You may do so," the King nodded, completely bewildered. As the man walked out, the King whispered to his ministers. "The greatest miser in the land wanting to give away his wealth! Wonders will never cease!"

The impostor then went directly to Ilisa's house. A servant stopped him at the gate. "Master! There's a stranger in town impersonating me," the impostor ordered. "If he comes here, beat him and drive him away."

"Yes, master," the servant agreed loyally. 

The impostor walked inside to Ilisa's wife. "It's a shame that Ilisa's wife should wear such dowdy clothes," he smiled gently. "Go to the market and buy whatever you want... buy something for the children too."

His wife stared at him in utter shock. "Are... are you all right?"

"I've never felt better!" the impostor laughed. "It's just that I am in a generous mood today. Tell everyone you meet that I am giving away half my wealth to the poor and needy."

*He has taken leave of his senses,* his wife thought worriedly. *But I'd better buy something for myself before he changes his mind!*

Soon, word spread, and joyous people began to stream into Ilisa's house. 

"Come in, come in," the impostor greeted them warmly. "Pick up whatever you want and go."

The house turned into a frenzy as the poor and needy eagerly grabbed clothes, food, and brass pots. One clever man worked more methodically than the others. Instead of rushing into the house, he chose Ilisa's best bullock-cart, hitched up the finest pair of bullocks, loaded the cart with valuables from the storeroom, and drove away, happily singing the praises of his benefactor. 

"There is no one greater than the noble Ilisa!" the man sang loudly as he drove past the lake. "May Ilisa live a thousand years!"

Hidden deep in the bushes by the lake, the real Ilisa was busy stuffing his face with jalebis. He paused, his mouth full. "Someone's praising me! What!"

He peeked through the leaves and saw the man driving the cart. "Thank you, my good man!" Ilisa called out. 

"I can see that you are a good judge of men. You are right," the man smiled back. 

Ilisa stepped out of the bushes and squinted at the cart. "I just have to look at a person to know..." His eyes widened in absolute horror. "MY BULLOCKS! MY CART! AND MY BELONGINGS!"

"Thief!" Ilisa screamed, lunging at the man. 

"How dare you call me a thief!" the man yelled, raising his heavy wooden stick and landing a vicious blow on Ilisa's back. *WHAM!* "I got all these things from the noble Ilisa."

"You couldn't have. I am Ilisa!" the miser shrieked from the dirt. 

"You! You are a scoundrel!" the man cursed, driving away with his newly acquired wealth. 

"I'd better rush home and see what's going on there," Ilisa panicked, scrambling to his feet and sprinting back to his house. 

When he reached his gates, his own guards blocked him. "Stop! What are you doing?" 

"Stop them! It's the impostor the master warned us about!" a servant yelled. 

Before Ilisa could explain, his own servants grabbed heavy sticks and brutally beat him. *THUD! WHACK!* They kicked him out into the dust. 

"You can't do this to me..." Ilisa groaned, clutching his bruised head. "Hey, you, take that back to the house! It's mine!" he yelled at a beggar walking out with a bundle of brass. 

"You are all going to pay dearly for this! I'll come back with the King's men," Ilisa threatened. 

He rushed to the royal palace, bruised and battered. "Maharaj, I am being looted!" he cried, falling before the King. "People are carrying away my property!"

The King frowned. "But Gildmaster... you yourself came here and asked for my permission to give away your wealth!"

"Maharaj, have you ever known me to give away anything?" Ilisa pleaded desperately. "Some impostor has taken my place! I will have him brought here."

The King ordered his guards, and soon, the impersonator was brought to the palace. The King and his courtiers found themselves gazing at two men who looked absolutely identical in every single respect. 

"Now who is who?" the King muttered in confusion. 

"My wife could identify me, Maharaj," Ilisa suggested confidently. 

His wife was immediately summoned. She looked at both men, then pointed firmly at the impostor. "That's my husband!"

"Ungrateful woman! After all I've done for you!" Ilisa shrieked in betrayal. Then he had a brilliant idea. "Now who... what... why, my barber of course! Maharaj, my barber could identify me!"

The royal barber was brought in. He carefully examined the heads of both men. "I'll have to examine their heads, Maharaj," the barber said. After a long moment, he shook his head. "This is uncanny! I cannot identify the Gildmaster, Maharaj."

"What do you mean!" Ilisa screamed. "I have a wart on my head. Have you forgotten?"

The barber pointed at both men. "You have a wart... but so does he!"

The sheer shock of the situation was too much. "Even my wife has identified him as her husband! I'll be thrown out of my own house... and I'll lose all my wealth... OOOH!" Ilisa gasped, and fainted dead away on the marble floor. 

The impostor chuckled. "Throw some water on him."

When Ilisa was finally restored to his senses, the impostor smiled down at him. "I shall not frighten you any more," the impostor said softly. His disguise melted away, revealing a divine being. 

"I am your father... come down from heaven," the divine being explained. 

"F-father!" Ilisa gasped. 

"All the wealth you possess is mine," his father declared sternly. "I was generous to the poor and needy during my life... but you have put all my wealth under lock and key and it is of no use to anyone! I will take away all my wealth if you persist in such selfish, greedy behaviour!"

Ilisa fell to his knees, tears streaming down his face. "I... I won't, father! Give me another chance!"

Satisfied that his son had finally learned his lesson, Ilisa's father summoned a magical golden chariot and returned to the celestial world. 

And from that day on, Ilisa returned home a changed man. He became generous, considerate, and spent the rest of his life using his immense wealth to help the poor and needy.
