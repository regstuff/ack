# The Washerman's Jackass

Long ago, there lived an ass and a dog who both belonged to a washerman in Varanasi. 

One evening, the washerman locked his doors and went to bed early. Outside in the yard, the tired ass sighed. "All is quiet. It's been a hard day. Tonight I shall sleep like a log."

But the dog was pacing angrily back and forth. "There! He has once again forgotten to give me my dinner."

A few hours later, in the dead of night, a thief quietly crept into the yard. The washerman was fast asleep inside. 

"Good! He's fast asleep. I'm lucky. I can begin filling my sack," the thief grinned, quietly packing the washerman's goods. 

The ass woke up and noticed the intruder. He turned to the dog, who was watching silently. "Look! A thief is carrying off our master's goods," the ass whispered urgently. "Why don't you bark and wake him up?"

The dog scoffed. "Why should I? Our master neglects me. A little fright will remind him that he needs me and must look after me better."

The ass was furious. "How can you let our master down, you unfaithful, ungrateful cur? Should I be grateful for being neglected? No. I will not let the thief go. I'll wake him up."

"Then I will," the ass declared bravely. "Before the thief goes, I'd better be quick!"

The ass threw his head back and began to bray as loudly as he possibly could. *HE-HAW! HE-HAW!*

The incredible noise startled the thief, who immediately dropped his sack and ran off into the night. 

Inside the house, the washerman woke up with a massive start. "The silly ass! He'll wake up the whole neighbourhood," he grumbled, rushing to the window. But by the time he looked outside, the thief had already gotten away unseen. 

"That stupid ass has ruined my sleep," the washerman fumed. "I'll teach him to bray at odd hours!"

Grabbing a heavy wooden cudgel, the washerman rushed out into the yard. Because he didn't know *why* the ass had brayed so loudly, he beat the poor animal mercilessly. *WHAM! WHAM!*

The dog shook his head in pity, watching the ass suffer. *Poor ass,* the dog thought. *If only you had stuck to your duties instead of trying to perform mine, would you have suffered this fate?*
