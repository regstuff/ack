# Abhimanyu: Star-Crossed Prince

Abhimanyu was the handsome and much-loved son of the great Pandava warrior, Arjuna, and Subhadra, the sister of Lord Krishna. His father could not help loving him more than his other sons. 

"Abhimanyu shall grow up to be the foremost among the Pandavas as well as the Yadavas!" Lord Krishna declared, holding the infant prince. 

"Hush! One ought not to praise one's own child thus," Subhadra smiled modestly. 

But misfortune soon befell the Pandavas. Their jealous cousins, the Kauravas, led by the wicked Prince Duryodhana, plotted against them. 

"The Pandavas are becoming too powerful," Duryodhana fumed. "I have it! Yudhishthira has a weakness for gambling. Let us invite him to a game of dice and make the dice obey us."

The Kauravas carried out their wicked plot with the help of their cunning uncle, Shakuni. They cheated Yudhishthira of his wealth, his kingdom, and forced the Pandavas into a humiliating thirteen-year exile in the forest. 

Abhimanyu was a mere child when these tragic events took place. But he was well looked after by his mother, Subhadra, in the kingdom of Dwaraka. 

"Your valour shall one day avenge this humiliation," she would tell him. 

He grew up to be a valiant youth and Krishna's favourite nephew. "I shall fight the Kauravas and win back our kingdom," the young prince vowed. 

He was loved and admired by all who knew him. He had inherited Yudhishthira's patience, Krishna's conduct, Bhima's strength, the gentleness of Nakula and Sahadeva, and his father Arjuna's stunning looks and prowess with a bow.

Meanwhile, the thirteen years of exile passed. The Pandavas had spent their final year in disguise at the court of King Virata. When Arjuna helped Virata's son triumph over a Kaurava attack, the grateful King offered his daughter, Princess Uttara, in marriage. 

Arjuna respectfully declined for himself, as he had been her teacher, but accepted her for his brave son, Abhimanyu. The wedding was solemnised amidst great feasting, attended by a host of friendly kings. 

After the wedding, the Pandavas held a council. Yudhishthira wanted to win back their kingdom without bloodshed, but King Duryodhana was obstinate. 

"I refuse them even an inch of land," Duryodhana sneered. "Let them fight and take it if they can."

And so, the terrible war of the Mahabharata began on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. 

On the very first day, the Kaurava grand-sire, Bheeshma, created absolute havoc among the Pandava troops. Hearing of this, young Abhimanyu fearlessly charged at the invincible elder. 

"A mere stripling of a boy, but what a fine warrior!" Bheeshma admired, as Abhimanyu's arrows seemingly flew from everywhere at once. The young prince distinguished himself brilliantly on his first day. 

The battle raged on for days. On the tenth day, Bheeshma finally fell, and the great teacher Dronacharya was made the supreme commander of the Kaurava armies. 

Duryodhana demanded a singular boon from Drona: "Capture Yudhishthira alive!" 

Drona tried fiercely, but failed. "We can never take Yudhishthira as long as Arjuna is by his side," Drona admitted. 

Duryodhana hatched a cunning plan. He had his allies draw Arjuna away to a distant part of the battlefield. With Arjuna gone, Drona immediately formed his troops into the impregnable, deadly circular formation known as the *Chakravyuha* and mercilessly attacked the vulnerable Pandavas. 

Desperate, Yudhishthira approached young Abhimanyu. "Only you, Arjuna, Krishna, and Pradyumna know how to break through Drona's circular formation. None of them is here, but you. Son, will you lead us?"

"I will gladly try to break through the formation," Abhimanyu agreed bravely. "But... I do not know how to come out of it, if I am overpowered and in danger."

"Break the formation and make way for us to enter," Yudhishthira promised. "We will follow close upon your heels and protect you."

"I will break through it and slaughter the Kauravas!" the valiant prince declared. 

He commanded his charioteer to drive straight at Drona's troops. "The Pandavas have placed a heavy burden on you today," his charioteer warned. "Think well before you proceed. Drona is an adept in the art of warfare."

"I am Arjuna's son and Krishna's nephew," Abhimanyu replied fearlessly. "Drive on."

Like a proud young lion falling upon a herd of elephants, Abhimanyu rushed at the *Chakravyuha*. He fought with blinding speed, smashing through the outer rings and plunging deep into the heart of the enemy formation, right under the nose of Dronacharya himself. 

"He is indeed the greatest among archers!" Drona marvelled. "He is capable of destroying an entire army if he chooses!"

But alas for the young hero, before Yudhishthira and the Pandava warriors could follow him through the breach, the powerful Kaurava ally, King Jayadratha, swiftly moved in and completely sealed the gap. 

Abhimanyu was trapped alone inside the deadliest formation in the world. 

Unfazed, the young lion fought with the fury of a hurricane. He felled countless Kaurava warriors, covering the earth with broken chariots and mutilated bodies. Terrified by his unstoppable onslaught, Kaurava troops tried to flee. 

Seeing his army being routed by a single boy, Duryodhana grew furious and rushed at him. Drona, alarmed for the King's safety, sent a massive host of warriors to rescue Duryodhana. Enraged that his prize target was snatched away, Abhimanyu vented his fiery wrath on everyone in his path. 

In a shameful violation of the ancient codes of war, the greatest Kaurava generals—Drona, Karna, Ashwatthama, and others—surrounded the lone boy and attacked him all at once. 

But even then, Abhimanyu held them off, his bow a blur of motion. He killed the warrior Asmaka, pierced the mighty Karna's armour, wounded King Shalya so badly he could not move, and shattered the chariot of Shalya's brother with a single arrow. 

"He stands unequalled as a bowman," Drona admitted in awe. "If he wishes he can wipe out this whole host, single-handed."

Duryodhana overheard and screamed at his generals. "Listen to how partial Dronacharya is towards Arjuna's son! We must kill the boy soon!"

Duryodhana's wicked brother, Duhshasana, charged in, but quickly fell senseless under Abhimanyu's arrows. Next came Karna, but Abhimanyu intercepted all his arrows and forced him to retreat. Even Duryodhana's own brave son, Lakshmana, rushed at the boy, but a sharp arrow from Abhimanyu pierced him, killing the Kaurava prince instantly. 

"KILL! KILL THE WICKED ABHIMANYU!" Duryodhana screamed in blind grief. 

The entire might of the Kaurava army descended on the boy. "Tell us how to kill him before he slays us all!" Karna asked Drona desperately. 

"You will never be able to pierce his armour," Drona advised shamefully. "Aim at the reins of his horses and cut them off. He will be disabled. Then attack from behind."

Karna cowardly shot an arrow from behind, slicing Abhimanyu's bowstring. The others quickly killed his horses and his charioteer. 

Stranded on the ground, the unarmed prince grabbed his heavy sword and shield, whirling them to hold his ground against the massive army. Soon, an arrow from Drona broke his sword, and an arrow from Karna shattered his shield. 

Totally disabled but undaunted, the fierce teenager picked up the heavy wooden wheel of his broken chariot. He whirled it above his head, roaring like a wounded tiger at his unjust, cowardly enemies. 

But arrows rained down from all directions, shattering the wheel to splinters. 

Bleeding from a hundred wounds, Abhimanyu picked up a fallen mace. Duhshasana's powerful son closed in with his own mace. The two warriors clashed, striking each other so hard that both fell to the ground, stunned. 

But Duhshasana's son was the first to recover. As Abhimanyu struggled to his hands and knees, the Kaurava prince brought his heavy mace crashing down on the young hero's skull. 

The star-crossed prince fell dead, his blood staining the earth of Kurukshetra. 

Like savages, the Kaurava warriors danced around his dead body, celebrating the murder of a lone, unarmed child. But their victory was incredibly short-lived. Not a single one of the men who cowardly murdered Abhimanyu outlived the war. Arjuna, Bhima, and the mighty Pandavas brutally avenged his death in the terrible battles that followed, utterly annihilating the Kaurava army. 

And the breathtaking bravery of the young lion, Abhimanyu, is remembered and sung about till this very day.
