# The Demon Outwitted

Long ago, a large band of monkeys came and settled on the outskirts of a dense, mysterious forest. Their wise leader, who knew the dangers of the place well, called a meeting. 

"Attention, all of you! I have something important to say," the leader warned. "There are certain poisonous trees with very tempting fruit, and one of the lakes is haunted by a dangerous demon. You will have to be careful about two things in this forest: You must not drink water or eat any fruit without asking me first."

The monkeys nodded, promising to obey. 

However, one hot day, while searching for firewood, the monkeys wandered deep into the forest. Exhausted and parched, a young monkey cried, "Mother, I'm thirsty."

"Ah! There is a lake close by," the mother noticed. 

The thirsty monkeys started running toward the sparkling water. Suddenly, an older monkey stood in their way. "Wait! Don't go near the water," he shouted. "Don't you remember our leader's warning?"

"Oh dear! I'd completely forgotten," the mother gasped, pulling her child back. "Thank you for reminding me. All right! Let us wait till our leader comes."

A little later, the leader arrived to find his troop sitting patiently by the shore. "What's the matter? You seem to have a problem," he asked.

"Master, we are very thirsty," they explained. "We are waiting for you to let us know if this lake is safe."

"That was very wise of you," the leader praised them. "Now let me examine the lake."

The wise leader walked carefully around the edge of the water, studying the muddy banks. "Hmm! All the footsteps lead *into* the lake," he muttered to himself, tracing the prints. "But not a single one leads *out* of it."

He returned to the group, his face pale. "No! This lake is haunted!"

"Oh dear! What shall we do? I'm very thirsty. I must have some water," the monkeys panicked. 

"Let me think in peace," the leader said, crossing his arms. 

Suddenly, the water bubbled and churned. With a terrifying *WHA-SPLASH*, a massive, hideous demon burst from the surface, roaring loudly. "What are you doing near my lake?" 

"Are you the demon of this lake?" the monkey leader asked bravely.

"Yes! And I eat all those who dare enter my lake!" the demon roared, his fangs dripping.

"In that case, we will not enter the lake," the leader replied calmly.

The demon laughed a booming, cruel laugh. "And let your herd die of thirst?"

"Oh no! We won't die. We will drink the water from your lake," the monkey leader smiled confidently.

"What??" the demon scoffed.

"Yes we will. Without entering it."

"And how, may I ask?" the demon mocked.

"Wait and see," the leader said. He turned to his troop. "Come on, boys. Follow me. Collect all the bamboo reeds you can."

The monkeys rushed into the nearby grove, gathering armfuls of long, sturdy bamboo. 

"Hm.. M.. M... This one's fine," the leader inspected one. "Absolutely hollow."

Working quickly, the leader joined a number of the hollow reeds together, creating one incredibly long, continuous pipe. 

"Now I'll slide that end into the water," the leader instructed, dropping one end into the lake. "Now what?" a monkey asked.

"I'll suck the water from this end," the leader said. 

With all his might, the leader took a deep breath and sucked on the giant reed. *SKURRRRR!* The water shot up the pipe and gushed out in a thick, glorious stream over the riverbank. 

"Water! Delicious water! And all for us!" the monkeys cheered, splashing and drinking happily, completely safe on dry land. 

Full of rage, the outwitted demon stomped back into the depths of the lake, defeated by a simple bamboo reed. The monkeys, their thirst well quenched, laughed and returned home.
