# Kumbhakarna

Kumbhakarna was the younger brother of Ravana, the ten-headed Rakshasa King of Lanka. He was strong, but sadly, a big bully who loved to frighten those weaker than him. 

"Ravana, do you know what I've done today? I walked into the Dandaka forest and frightened the sages there! Ha! Ha! Ha! You should have seen them run!" Kumbhakarna boasted. 

But his actions angered Indra, King of the Devas. Indra attacked Kumbhakarna with his army of Devas. "Come, Indra. I was waiting for you," Kumbhakarna sneered. 

Indra hurled his powerful mace, but Kumbhakarna caught it and swallowed it! Then he leapt forward, caught hold of Indra's elephant, Airavata, by its tusks, and flung it far away. Indra's army fled in terror. 

"Not yet. We're strong and powerful. But we must pray to Lord Brahma and get more strength and more power," Ravana decided. The three brothers, Ravana, Kumbhakarna, and Vibhishana, went to a quiet place to perform severe penance. 

When Indra heard this, he was worried. He went to Saraswati, the Goddess of Speech, for help. "O Saraswati, only you can save us now." 

Lord Brahma was pleased with the penances and appeared. He granted Ravana the boon to be Lord of the Three Worlds, and Vibhishana a boon of righteousness. 

But before Kumbhakarna could speak, Saraswati cast her spell on his tongue. "I want to sleep all the time, my Lord!" he blurted out instead of asking for invincibility. 

"May you sleep for twenty-four hours a day!" Brahma granted instantly. 

"My Lord, my brother didn't know what he was asking for. Please take back the boon," Ravana pleaded in horror. 

"I cannot take back a boon I have granted. But... all right. He will remain awake for a whole day, once every six months. But if anyone wakes him up on any other day, his life will be in danger," Brahma amended. 

Kumbhakarna was taken to his palace, where he immediately fell fast asleep. 

Years later, ignoring Vibhishana's advice, Ravana carried away Sita, the wife of Rama. Vibhishana left Lanka and joined Rama, who marched on Lanka with a huge army of monkeys. 

Ravana first sent the terrible Rakshasa, Bhasmalochana, who could reduce anyone to ashes with a mere look. But Rama quickly created millions of mirrors. When Bhasmalochana opened his eyes, he saw hundreds of his own fiery eyes reflected back at him, and he was burnt to ashes by his own power! 

Then Ravana himself went to battle. He was struck unconscious by Rama's arrows. When he recovered, Rama shot off his crowns. Deeply humiliated, Ravana fled back to his palace. 

"What should I do? When I, the King, have to flee, what hope is there for my subjects? Go wake Kumbhakarna up! Ask him to see me," Ravana ordered, completely forgetting Brahma's warning. 

The servants went to Kumbhakarna's palace. As the giant breathed in, the servants were sucked into his nostrils! When he breathed out, they were blown away. 

They tried making a huge racket with drums and conches, pouring ice-cold sandalwood paste on him, beating him with huge logs, and even making elephants walk over him. "It's useless. He hasn't even stopped snoring!" 

Finally, someone brought steaming hot food. "Mm-m-m! Delicious!" Kumbhakarna woke up, his mouth watering. After devouring mountains of food, he went to Ravana. 

"It's not Indra but men and monkeys who are about to attack Lanka," Ravana explained. 

"I've no doubt about it. You are mighty and fearless. I'm sure you'll succeed," Ravana encouraged him, despite Kumbhakarna's initial rebuke about abducting Sita. 

Kumbhakarna marched alone to the battlefield. The earth shook under him, and the monkey-warriors trembled with fear. "We're no match for this giant." 

He created havoc, crushing monkeys underfoot. Neela, a mighty monkey, hurled a huge sal tree at him, but Kumbhakarna broke it into bits. Sugreeva, the monkey-king, challenged him. He snatched Kumbhakarna's iron rod and broke it, but Kumbhakarna picked Sugreeva up like a toy and marched off towards Ravana's palace. 

"I'll make a gift of you to Ravana," Kumbhakarna laughed. But suddenly, Sugreeva tore off Kumbhakarna's ear and leaped back to his army. 

Filled with shame and bleeding, Kumbhakarna returned to the battlefield in a rage. "Call Rama, of whom I've heard so much. Let me see him fight." 

Rama faced him. "Here I am. Use all your strength. After I've killed you and Ravana, I'll make Vibhishana King of Lanka, take Sita, and return with her to Ayodhya," Rama challenged. 

Rama shot a dart, which Kumbhakarna brushed off. Then Rama fixed a special arrow to his bow. "The arrow found its mark." With a final, agonizing cry, Kumbhakarna fell dead. 

When the news reached Ravana, he was devastated. "Alas! Why did I wake up my brother in spite of the warning?" he wept. "I will avenge his death. I'll slay Rama." 

But in the final battle with Rama, it was Ravana who was slain. Rama made Vibhishana the Lord of Lanka, rescued Sita, and returned triumphantly to Ayodhya.
