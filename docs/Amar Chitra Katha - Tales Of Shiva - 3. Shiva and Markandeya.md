# Shiva and Markandeya

Sage Mrikandu and his wife lived a pious, devout life, but they were deeply saddened because they had no children. Hoping to earn a divine blessing, the sage observed severe, painful penances in the forest to propitiate Lord Shiva. 

Pleased by his devotion, Lord Shiva finally appeared before him. "Lord, favour me with a son," the sage begged, falling to his knees. 

Shiva raised his hand in blessing, but offered a difficult choice. "Do you want a son who will live long though lacking in virtues... or a son who will be incredibly wise and virtuous, but will live for only sixteen years?" 

Sage Mrikandu did not hesitate. "I will have the virtuous son, my Lord."

Granting the wish of the sage, Lord Shiva vanished. 

In due course, Mrikandu's wife gave birth to a beautiful, radiant baby boy. "The boy shall be named Markandeya," the proud father announced. 

Markandeya grew up to be a brilliant and deeply devoted child. While barely sixteen, he had already mastered the complex wisdom of the Vedas. 

One day, after a group of visiting sages had left their home, Mrikandu and his wife sat alone, tears streaming down their faces. "Mrikandu, you are fortunate to have such a brilliant son," a sage had remarked. 

"I cannot forget that all this brilliance will soon be put out by cruel death," the father wept. 

Just then, Markandeya came home with a basket of fresh flowers for his daily worship. "Mother! Why are you weeping? What can I do to make you smile?" he asked gently. 

When his mother couldn't speak through her sobs, he turned to his father. "Why? What is wrong with being sixteen?" 

"I will explain. Listen!" Mrikandu sighed heavily, and told him the tragic truth about the events leading to his birth, and the terrifying condition of Lord Shiva's boon. "...I will be sixteen years old tomorrow!" Markandeya realized. 

But instead of panicking, the young boy smiled peacefully. "Don't cry, mother. I will not die," he promised. "I will seek immortality through the grace of Shiva, who is the conqueror of death."

His parents hugged him tightly. "May you succeed by His grace!"

"Shiva, I leave my son to your mercy. Protect him," Mrikandu prayed as Markandeya walked out of the house. 

Early the next morning, on his sixteenth birthday, Markandeya walked down to the peaceful sea-shore. With his own hands, he moulded a beautiful Shiva Linga out of the wet sand, adorned it with fresh, fragrant flowers, and sat down to pray. 

Towards nightfall, filled with pure devotion, he began to sing and dance joyously before the Lord. 

Suddenly, a dark, terrifying shadow fell across the sand. It was Yama, the terrifying God of Death, holding his deadly noose. 

"Get ready to leave this world," Yama boomed. 

Markandeya looked up, unfazed. "O Lord of Death, please wait. I have not yet finished my worship."

"Foolish boy, don't you know that death waits for none?" Yama sneered. 

Markandeya hugged the sand Linga tightly. "Please do not obstruct me in my worship of Lord Shiva," he pleaded. 

"Fool! Do you hope to escape from me by clinging to Shiva? The grip of death is fatal as you shall now know!" Yama roared. 

With a swift, violent motion, Yama threw his heavy rope, catching Markandeya's neck in the deadly noose, and brutally dragged him away from the Linga. 

"Lord, protect me!" Markandeya screamed. 

The very next moment, the sand Linga erupted in a blinding flash of divine light. Lord Shiva himself sprang from the Linga, his eyes blazing with fury, and viciously kicked Yama squarely in the chest. 

Yama crashed to the ground, dropping the noose. Trembling in sheer terror before the Supreme Destroyer, Yama fell to his knees. "Lord! Pardon me," the God of Death begged. 

"O Lord, the source of life, I salute you," Markandeya wept in joy, bowing at Shiva's feet. 

Shiva smiled warmly at the devoted boy. "Markandeya, you will forever be free from death. You will be immortal."

With that ultimate blessing, Shiva vanished. 

And Markandeya returned home, forever a sixteen-year-old boy, running into the arms of his overjoyed parents. "Mother! The Lord did save me!"
