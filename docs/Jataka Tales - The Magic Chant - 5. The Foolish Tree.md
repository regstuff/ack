# The Foolish Tree

Long ago, two trees lived in peace and friendship in a forest which was full of wild beasts. Lions and tigers roamed freely in the forest and preyed on other animals. 

After they had had their fill, they left the carcasses behind... causing a foul stench all round. The forest was reeking with the smell. 

One day, one of the trees complained. "Friend, these animals are such a nuisance! I feel suffocated by the smell. Yes, it is becoming difficult to live here." 

"I can't bear it any longer! I'll drive these animals away," the tree declared angrily. 

"Oh, no! Please don't! The wild animals protect us, and keep men away! But just imagine, how pure and pleasant the air will be!" the other tree warned him gently. 

"I'll do it! I'll drive them away!" 

One night, shortly afterwards, a strong breeze blew through the forest. "Ah! A strong breeze is blowing this way. Here is my chance!" the foolish tree thought. 

The tree shook itself vigorously, making a loud noise. *SHR-SHR-SHR!* 

This awakened the animals sleeping beneath it. "What's happening? Look at that tree! How it is swaying!" the animals gasped. 

"The tree is possessed! Let's run away!" the tigers cried. 

"Yes, all of us had better leave! Hurry!" 

The next morning, the foolish tree was delighted. "At last, I've got rid of those animals! Already, the air smells fresher!" 

Many days passed. Quiet and peace reigned in the forest. Then, one afternoon, a cowherd passed that way. "Sh...o...o...o... come back, you naughty one! The lion will eat you up!" he shouted to a straying calf. 

Following the calf, the boy ventured into the forest. "I hope a tiger doesn't pounce on me!" But as he looked around, he noticed something. "But there seem to be no tigers here! And no animal footprints anywhere around!" 

He went home and told his family about his discovery. The next day, the villagers arrived. "It looks as if no animals have lived here for ages! This land could be very useful to us." 

As the men examined the trees, the wise tree whispered to the foolish one, "You see what they are planning? I had told you to let the wild beasts alone." 

"Don't be silly. I can frighten those men away, as I did the beasts!" the foolish tree boasted. 

The tree shook itself violently, making a loud sound. "What is that awful sound, father?" a young boy asked. 

"It's nothing, son! Just the breeze, rustling through the leaves!" the father smiled. 

The tree tried again, shaking even harder. "Hey! Watch out! Oh! It's nothing to worry about! Just a few twigs have fallen down! We'll cut down these trees. The wood will come in useful." 

Soon the forest was full of people from the village nearby, hacking down the trees. "Just a few more strokes, brother! Yes, then the tree will fall down." 

"They're pointing to me! What shall I do?" the foolish tree cried in despair. 

That night, it pleaded with its friend. "Help, friend! Help me, please! You heard them, didn't you? They are coming for me tomorrow!" 

"It is too late! Now you must bear the consequences of your action!" the wise tree sighed sadly. 

And that was the end of the foolish tree.
