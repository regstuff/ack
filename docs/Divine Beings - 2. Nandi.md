# Nandi

Sage Shilada was in deep meditation. He had been praying to Lord Shiva for many years. Finally, early one morning, Shiva appeared before him. "What do you want, Shilada?" Shiva asked.

"O Shiva, I long for a child," Shilada prayed with folded hands. 

"You will get what you desire," Shiva promised. 

The next morning, as Shilada ploughed the land near his ashram, he found a glowing baby boy in the furrow. "A boy! He glows like the sun. I shall call you Nandi!" Shilada rejoiced, naming him 'the one who brings joy'. 

Shilada brought Nandi up with great love and taught him everything he knew. "I have memorised all the shlokas, father," young Nandi would say. 

"But have you understood the meaning behind each one?" 

"Yes, father," Nandi replied respectfully. 

"Nandi is very bright. He has unparalleled wisdom. He will surely earn great respect some day," Shilada thought proudly. 

One day, two sages, Mitra and Varuna, came to visit Shilada's ashram. "Welcome, O sages," Shilada greeted them. "Nandi, you must look after our guests with great care." 

Nandi served them diligently. When it was time for them to leave, the sages blessed Shilada. "May you live a long and prosperous life, Shilada." 

"It has been an honour serving you," Nandi bowed. 

"May you always be happy, Nandi," they said, their faces suddenly turning serious. As they were walking away, Shilada noticed their expressions. "Please stop! What is the matter, Shilada?" 

"Why didn't you bless my son with a long life?" Shilada asked anxiously. 

"Because... we couldn't," the sages admitted sadly. "We are sorry. Your son does not have long to live." 

Shilada returned home, heartbroken. "What is wrong, father? You look sad," Nandi asked. 

"Nothing, my son," Shilada tried to hide his tears, but eventually, he told Nandi what the sages had foreseen. 

"Is that all? Why are you so worried?" Nandi smiled calmly. "You told me that Lord Shiva watches over us. How can you be afraid? He won't let anything happen to us. I'll pray to him." 

Nandi went to the banks of the river Bhuvana and meditated on Shiva. At the end of the year, Shiva appeared before Nandi. "What is it that you want, my son?" 

"I wish to be able to chant verses in your honour for a year," Nandi requested. 

"So be it." 

Nandi asked for the same boon year after year. His devotion to Shiva was unbreakable. After a few years, Shiva appeared again. "Open your eyes, Nandi. You do not need to do this anymore. You are already immortal. Ask for anything." 

"All I wish is to be at your side for ever," Nandi prayed. 

"So be it. You will be my vehicle and my closest aide," Shiva declared, transforming the boy into a magnificent, divine bull. He took Nandi to his home on Mount Kailash, and from then on, Shiva travelled on Nandi wherever he went. 

Once, when the gods and the asuras churned the ocean to obtain the life-giving amrita, a deadly poison spewed out, threatening to engulf the world. "It is halahala! We are doomed!" the gods panicked. 

The gods pleaded with Shiva. "Please save the world, O Lord." 

"I will drink all the poison. Come, there isn't a moment to lose," Shiva said, mounting Nandi. 

"Look! The poison is spreading through the world. Hurry, Nandi!" Shiva commanded. Shiva began drinking the deadly poison. To protect Shiva, Parvati put her hand on his throat and stopped the poison from flowing inside his body. But as he drank, some of the poison fell from his hands. 

"If that poison falls on the ground it could kill thousands. I must do something," Nandi thought. Without hesitation, Nandi turned his head and lapped up the falling poison. 

"Oh no! Nandi is drinking the poison! It will destroy him!" the gods cried out. 

But to their amazement, Nandi stood unharmed. "It hasn't harmed him at all! What a wonder!" 

"Nandi has devoted himself completely to me. Everything that protects me, protects him too," Shiva explained. "He is as strong as I am." 

One day, Ravana, the ten-headed king of Lanka, came knocking at the gates of Shiva's abode. Nandi was guarding it as usual. "Stop! How dare you come in my way? No one stops the mighty Ravana," Ravana roared. 

"Lord Shiva and Mother Parvati are not to be disturbed. You cannot enter, whoever you may be," Nandi stood his ground firmly. 

"You cannot stop me. Out of my way, you insolent monkey!" Ravana insulted him. 

Nandi was enraged. "You fool! How dare you insult me! Your arrogance will be your end. You will one day be defeated by monkeys," Nandi cursed him. 

Ravana was shocked and eventually returned to Lanka. Nandi's curse came to pass when an army of monkeys led by the Prince of Ayodhya, Rama, defeated Ravana and his mighty army. 

It is said that Nandi taught Hanuman the hymns to praise Shiva. Today, every Shiva temple has a statue of Nandi directly in front of the main sanctum sanctorum. Nandi sits there, guarding his lord through eternity.
