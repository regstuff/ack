# Ganesha and Gana

The Chintamani Vinayaka temple at Theur is one of the Ashtavinayaka temples, the eight Ganesha temples near Pune. Like all the others, this temple has its own story. 

King Abhishek and his beautiful queen Gunavati, were full of despair for they had no children. They went to a sage for advice. "How can we get an heir, O wise one?" 

"Do not worry. Perform the penances I tell you to and you will surely have a son," the sage promised. 

Just as the sage had predicted, the queen gave birth to a son. "Our son is so radiant! We shall call him Gana," the king smiled. 

The young prince grew up to be a great devotee of Shiva. He prayed fervently, and Shiva appeared before him. "I am pleased with your devotion, Gana. I will grant you some boons," Shiva offered. 

Shiva's boons made Gana incredibly powerful, but also arrogant and cruel. He rode through the lands, pillaging and harassing the people. 

One day, Gana and his friends went hunting. "But there was no sign of a kill for hours," they rode through the forest in frustration. 

After wandering for a while, they reached the ashram of Sage Kapila. "Welcome, young prince! You honour me with your presence! Please join me for a meal," the sage greeted them warmly. 

"This is the Chintamani gem given to me by Lord Indra himself. It has the power to grant me whatever I desire," Sage Kapila revealed to the astonished guests. He closed his eyes and chanted a few words, and a magnificent feast appeared before them. 

"What a wonderful spread! So much food!" Gana's friends marvelled. 

As he ate, Gana's selfish mind had but one thought. "I must have this gem. This gem is wasted here. Let me take it to my palace where it will get the honour due to it," he told Kapila after the meal. 

"Thank you, but I prefer to keep it with me," Kapila declined politely. 

Gana flew into a rage. "Then I shall take it away from you!" he shouted, snatching the gem from the sage's hand. 

An anguished Sage Kapila prayed to Ganesha for help. "Please get back the Chintamani, O Ganesha!" 

"Do not worry. I shall restore it to you," Ganesha assured him. 

Ganesha appeared in Gana's dream and threatened him. "Aaaargh!" Gana woke up in a sweat, but far from being frightened, he gathered his army for battle. 

"Don't fight Ganesha, my son! Repent while you can," his father pleaded. 

"Out of my way, father!" Gana yelled, pushing his father aside and thundering towards Sage Kapila's ashram. 

A fierce battle ensued. Ganesha killed Gana with his axe and retrieved the gem. (Ganesha is known as Gananayaka, meaning 'one who defeated Gana'). 

But when he returned the gem to Kapila, the sage looked at it with sadness. "O Ganesha! This gem has brought nothing but misery. Will you keep it?" 

Ganesha agreed to do so. "You have taken a great load off my mind by taking the gem!" Kapila smiled. 

Sage Kapila then installed an idol of Ganesha. "This form of you, O benevolent Lord, shall be called Chintamani Vinayaka," the sage declared. 

Even today devotees flock to the temple of Chintamani Vinayaka in the belief that he can help them get rid of their 'chinta' or worry.
