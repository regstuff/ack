# How an Elephant was Weighed

One day, a king sent for his royal mahout. "I want to know how much my elephant weighs, and if you don't give me the answer by tomorrow morning... I'll have you beheaded." 

"I'm done for! How can anyone weigh an elephant? It's impossible," the terrified mahout thought. In those days, they did not have scales that could weigh elephants. 

As the mahout was wandering around in despair, he met an old sadhu. "Why do you look so glum, my friend?" the sadhu asked. 

"I'm in real trouble, sir. The king has asked me to weigh his elephant," the mahout explained his impossible task. 

"Is that all? Cheer up. Go and bring the animal, along with one of your friends, to the river. I'll wait for you there." 

The mahout did as he was told. He brought the elephant to the riverbank. 

"Now get him into that boat," the sadhu instructed. 

The heavy elephant stepped into the wooden boat, causing it to sink deep into the water. 

"That's it. Now mark the level to which the boat has sunk," the sadhu pointed out. The mahout drew a line on the side of the boat where the water touched it. 

"Now bring the elephant out and fill the boat with sand till it sinks to the mark you have made," the sadhu continued. 

After some time, the mahout and his friend filled the boat with heavy sand. "There! The boat has sunk to the mark, sir." 

"Do you know why?" the sadhu asked. "It's because the sand you have put into it is equal to the weight of the elephant. Now weigh that sand on a pair of scales and... I'll know the weight of the elephant!" the mahout realized joyfully. 

The next day, the mahout went to the court. "Did you find out how much my elephant weighs?" the king asked strictly. 

"Yes, your majesty. It weighs 900 pounds," the mahout answered confidently. 

The king was amazed by his ingenuity and richly rewarded the mahout. And the mahout never forgot the wise sadhu who had saved his life.
