# Practise What You Preach

Once, the King of the Birds took his massive flock to the Himalayas in search of food. 

"Now go and look for seeds and grain," the King ordered his flock. "When you find some, report to me, so we can all safely share it."

As they wandered off in different directions, one particular female bird came to a busy road where heavy wagons loaded with grain used to pass. 

"Look at all that grain on the road! What a feast!" she chirped greedily. "I won't tell the King about this find! I'll keep it all to myself."

But then a thought struck her. "But what if one of the others should fly this way and see the grain?" she worried. "I know! I'll tell them about it and yet keep them away!"

She quickly flew back to the flock. "You've been away a long time! Have you had any luck?" the King asked. 

"None at all!" she lied flawlessly. "In fact, I very nearly lost my life!"

The other birds were all ears. 

"I happened to fly over the main highway!" she gasped dramatically. "Elephants and horses, and heavy wagons drawn by fierce, thundering bullocks go along that route."

"That sounds terrible!" the other birds chirped in fear. 

"There is grain there," she continued, "But once you alight, it's difficult to soar up again before a wagon crushes you. Don't go that way. It's too dangerous."

After her terrifying warning, all the other birds were extremely careful to completely avoid the highway. 

*That was clever of me!* the greedy bird laughed to herself. *I have all this grain to myself now.*

The next day, she happily flew back to the highway and began pecking at the massive piles of spilled grain. Suddenly, she heard a sound in the distance. 

*WHRRRRRR...*

"What's that? Oh! A cart!" she noticed. She glanced down the road. "It's a long way off. There's enough time to peck a few more seeds."

What she didn't know was that it was a fast-moving, heavy express cart. 

*WHRRRR WHRRRR!* 

Before she could even spread her wings to take flight, the massive wooden wheel of the speeding cart ran directly over her, instantly crushing her flat. 

That evening, when all the birds came home to roost, they found her missing. The King sent search parties in all directions to find their lost companion. 

Later, a few birds returned with sad news. "We found her on the highway... a cart must have run over her. She's dead!"

The King flew to the spot at once, looking down at her crushed remains. 

"What a sad fate!" the wise King sighed. "She warned you all not to go near the highway. But she could not control her own greed, and she failed to practise what she preached. Let this be a lesson to all of you!"
