# The Monkey's Heart

There once lived a plump, happy monkey in the trees along the bank of a river. What the monkey didn't know was that a little downstream, a crocodile and his wife were watching him closely.

"Have you seen that plump monkey upstream?" the crocodile's wife drooled over her cooking pot. "I'm longing to eat his heart."

"I would love to get it for you," her husband sighed. "But he lives up in the trees. How can I catch him?"

"You are a crocodile, aren't you? Use your cunning!" his wife scolded. 

The crocodile rubbed his chin. "Humm! Let's see. My idea might just work."

The next morning, the crocodile swam upstream to the monkey's favorite tree. "Hello monkey! Aren't you sick of eating the fruit of the same tree every day?"

The monkey chewed on a banana thoughtfully. "Hmm! I guess so."

"Why don't you go across to the opposite bank?" the crocodile suggested sweetly. "There are some delicious fruits there."

"But how shall I get across?" the monkey asked. "I can't swim."

"Oh, that's simple," the crocodile smiled, exposing rows of sharp teeth. "You can ride on my back."

"You are a friend indeed. Let's go," the trusting monkey cheered, leaping down onto the crocodile's scaly back. "This is an excellent idea. Why didn't we think of it before?"

But as soon as they were completely surrounded by deep water in the middle of the river, the crocodile suddenly dove downward, plunging the monkey into the freezing current. 

"Hey! Are you trying to drown me?" the monkey coughed, scrambling to stay above water.

"Not at all," the crocodile sneered. "For I'm going to kill you."

"But why? What have I done?" the terrified monkey cried.

"Nothing. But my wife wants to eat your heart. So you die."

The monkey clung to the crocodile's back, his mind racing. *I'd better think fast if I love my life.*

Suddenly, the monkey burst into a fit of uproarious laughter. "Ha! Ha! Ha! Ho! Ho! Hee! Heee!"

"Stop that," the crocodile snapped. "What's so funny about dying?"

"The joke's on you, crocodile," the monkey snickered. "You may kill me. But you will never get my heart."

"What do you mean?" the confused crocodile asked.

"We monkeys hide our hearts in safe places before we venture out!" the monkey lied smoothly. 

"Really?" the crocodile gasped. "How was I to know?"

"Never mind," the monkey sighed playfully. "Turn back. Since you were so kind to me, I'll show you where my heart is. Can you see that tall, big tree on the bank where I live?"

"Of course I can," the crocodile said, turning around.

"I hide it in the topmost branch of that tree," the monkey said. "Take me back and I'll give it to you."

As soon as the crocodile bumped against the muddy riverbank, the monkey leaped off his back like lightning, scrambling up the trunk of his tree until he was safe in the highest branches. 

"Now I'd better act fast," the monkey panted, safely out of reach.

He looked down at the waiting crocodile and burst into laughter once again. "You poor fool. If my heart were removed from my body I'd be dead! It's safe inside me, and you'll never lay your hands on it!"

The defeated crocodile slumped back into the water, cursing his own foolishness, while the clever monkey enjoyed his bananas in peace.
