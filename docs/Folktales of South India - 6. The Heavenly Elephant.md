# The Heavenly Elephant

One day a labourer was looking for work in the fields when he saw an incredible sight—a magnificent white elephant descending from the sky. "No elephant on earth can fly! It must have come from heaven," he marvelled. 

He hid behind a bush and watched. After a while, the elephant finished grazing and prepared to leave. "It's about to leave! I'll go to heaven with it!" The daring man ran out and grabbed the elephant's tail just as it lifted off the ground. 

"Aha! Heaven! There should be plenty of work for me here," he thought as they soared into the clouds. 

The man worked in heaven the rest of the day and spent the night there. The next morning, the elephant prepared to return to earth. "Ah, here comes the elephant! Now how do I get back to earth?" He simply grabbed the tail again and rode the elephant down. 

When the man returned home, he told everyone where he had been. "It's such a wonderful place... and it's such a joy to work there! They pay one measure of rice for every hour of work! And their measure is so large! See how much rice I got for half a day's work!" 

After hearing his tale, all the villagers wanted to go to heaven too. So the next morning the man took them to the field where he had seen the elephant. 

"Quiet, now! Don't make a noise or he may not land here," he warned them. 

As the villagers watched with bated breath, the elephant landed. "Quiet! Here he comes," the man whispered. The elephant peacefully grazed, and then as it was about to leave, the man sprinted forward and grabbed its tail. 

"Now take this bag," the second villager grabbed the first man's legs. The third grabbed the second's legs, and so on. Soon, a long human chain of villagers was flying upward towards heaven. 

"We'll stay up there as long as we can! We'll return with sackloads of grain!" they chattered excitedly as they dangled in the sky. 

"How much did you say they pay for one hour's work?" the man at the very bottom of the chain shouted up to the first man. 

"One measure of rice!" he shouted back. 

"How big is the measure?" 

"THIS BIG!" the first man proudly demonstrated, letting go of the elephant's tail to hold his hands wide apart. 

The next moment, all of them plunged screaming down from the sky. *SPLASH!* They all fell into a deep river. 

The heavenly elephant was so startled by the noise and the splash that he never came to that village again.
