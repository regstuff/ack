# Raman's Cat

The Emperor of Vijayanagara, Krishna Deva Raya, wanted to see how clever his ministers were. He sat on his throne and posed a strange challenge. 

"Can any of you bring me a cat... which runs away from milk?" he asked with an amused smile. 

The ministers looked at each other in disbelief. "No cat will ever run away from milk," one declared. 

"There's nothing a cat likes better," another agreed. 

"What? No one?" the Emperor chuckled. 

"I will bring you such a cat, your majesty," a single voice spoke up. 

The man who spoke was Tenali Raman, the cleverest jester in the court. "But not immediately. Then bring it within 30 days," the Emperor challenged. 

Raman went home and placed a saucer of milk before his pet cat. But this was no ordinary milk—it was boiling hot! 

The cat eagerly dipped its tongue into it... and jumped back in alarm. The milk had burned its tongue! The poor cat hissed and scampered away from the saucer. 

For the next thirty days, Raman fed the cat food, but every time he offered milk, he made sure it was scalding hot. The cat quickly learned to associate the sight of white milk with terrible pain. 

On the thirtieth day, Raman returned to the court with his cat. "Here is the cat, your majesty," Raman presented the feline. 

"Let us see if it runs away from milk," the Emperor said, ordering a servant to bring a saucer of fresh, cool milk. 

As soon as the servant placed the saucer of milk in front of the cat, the creature took one terrified look at it, let out a yowl, and bolted out of the court as fast as its legs could carry it. 

The Emperor and his ministers watched in sheer amazement as Raman's cat successfully ran away from the milk!
