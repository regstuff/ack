# Dodda's Dubuka

Dodda was invited to his friend's house for tea. His friend's mother served them a steaming hot, sweet dish. 

"This is delicious! Delicious!" Dodda praised. "I'll ask my mother to make some at home. What is it called?" 

"Kadubu," the mother smiled. 

Dodda thanked her and set off for home. "Kadubu... Kadubu... I mustn't forget. Kadubu... Kadubu..." he repeated to himself as he walked. "I'll have to be going now... Kadubu... Kadubu..." 

As he was walking through a field, still chanting, he suddenly tripped over a rock. "Ka-A-Aah! Oww... oww... oww!" he yelled as he fell. 

Just then, a strange bird flew overhead and let out a loud call: "DUBUKA!" 

Dodda stopped rubbing his toe and looked up. "Now what was that sweet called?" he wondered. His mind went blank, and the only word he could remember was the bird's cry. "What's that? Dubuka! Dubuka... Dubuka..." 

He ran home excitedly. "Dubuka... Mother! Dubuka! I want Dubuka!" 

"Enough of your jokes! What is it?" his mother asked, annoyed. 

"Dubuka is Dubuka! And I want some now!" Dodda insisted stubbornly. 

His mother lost her temper and gave him a smack. "Well then take this Dubuka and more Dubukas! Silly boy! Why did you make me beat you? Now your poor face looks... like a Kadubu." 

"Kadubu! That's it!" Dodda's eyes widened. "That's what I want! Kadubu!" 

"Then why didn't you say so? I'll make some for you right away," she said with a sigh. 

She made plenty of them, and for three days, silly Dodda ate nothing but Kadubus—morning, noon, and night.
