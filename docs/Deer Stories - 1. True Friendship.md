# True Friendship

In a thicket near a lake, lived three friends: a tortoise, a woodpecker, and an antelope. 

One day, a hunter came that way. "Ah! Footprints!" he exclaimed. "I'll set the trap right here. I'll come back for him tomorrow."

At dawn, the antelope went to the lake. "It's good to be alive," he thought. "I must have a drink." Suddenly, he stepped into the snare and was caught. "Help!!" he cried out in terror.

The tortoise and the woodpecker heard his cries and rushed to his side. The woodpecker quickly came up with a plan. "I'll tell you what," he said to the tortoise, "you have strong teeth. You free him while I keep the hunter away. When he comes out of his hut, I'll give him a good pecking."

"Will you please hurry?" the antelope pleaded as the tortoise began gnawing at the tough leather trap. 

"The tortoise was as slow as a tortoise could be," the woodpecker noted as he flew off towards the hunter's hut. 

Meanwhile, the hunter had heard the antelope's shouts for help and rushed out. The woodpecker spied him and zoomed down, striking him right on the head. "Screeeeeeech!! Ouch!!!" the hunter yelled, dropping his sword. "Ah... ah, he's caught. I'd better get indoors." 

Once inside, the hunter thought, "I've got it, I'll try to go out by the back door." 

But the clever woodpecker was already waiting. "I bet he will try to get out from the back door." As the hunter opened the back door, the bird struck again. "Ouch!!!" 

"Hmm, it looks safe this side," the hunter muttered, trying the front door again. But as he had expected, the woodpecker was there. *Wack!* With a groan, the hunter crashed back into his hut. 

Leaving him there, the woodpecker flew back to his friends. "Hey friend Tortoise, you had better hurry. He'll be here any minute," he warned. 

"I am trying my best," the tortoise panted. Finally, with a loud *thud*, the trap broke. 

"Come on. Let's get out of here," the antelope urged. But the poor tortoise, as usual, was too slow. 

The hunter arrived just in time to see the tortoise. "Ah, so you are the culprit. You freed my antelope," he sneered. "I'll teach you not to interfere. Since you robbed me of my dinner... I'll put you in this sack, take you home, and eat you." He shoved the tortoise into his sack. 

Seeing this, the antelope knew it was his turn to save his friend. He deliberately showed himself to the hunter. "There goes my antelope," the hunter thought. 

"If he sees me, he will chase me, and my friend can escape," the antelope planned. 

The greedy hunter dropped his sack and ran after the antelope. "I'll lead him into my favourite old cave," the antelope decided. 

He dashed into the dark cave, and the hunter followed blindly. "Huh—where did he go?" the hunter wondered, completely lost in the darkness. "Once he is in, he will not know how to get out," the antelope thought. "What a fool! He'll be trapped in the cave. He's done for."

The antelope rushed back to the spot where the tortoise had been abandoned. "Don't worry, my friend, I'll soon get you out of the bag!" Using his antlers, he ripped the sack open. 

As the tortoise crawled out, the woodpecker flew down from the tree. "What happened to the hunter?" asked the tortoise.

"Ha! Ha! Ha! He is running around in circles in our old cave!" the antelope laughed, and the three friends rejoiced in their safety.
