# What's In a Name?

In ancient Takshashila, there once lived a brilliant Vedic scholar who had five hundred young disciples studying under him. 

One of these boys was unfortunately given the name 'Lowly' when he first became a student. Despite his terrible name, he was an incredibly sweet boy. 

"Lowly, can you help me with this complex shloka? I can't seem to get it right," a fellow student asked. 

"Eh? Oh! It's easy. I'll help you," Lowly smiled warmly. 

"Lowly, will you help me carry this heavy firewood?" another asked. 

"Eh! Ah! To be sure, I will," Lowly agreed, happily lifting the bundle. 

So helpful and kind was Lowly that all his fellow students absolutely liked him. But deep down, he was not happy. 

*Why have I been given such a terrible name?* he thought miserably, holding his head in his hands. *Every single time I hear it, I feel sad.*

So, one day, he went to his wise teacher. "Sir, please give me a new name which sounds more respectable," he requested politely. 

"All right, my son," the teacher smiled gently. "Go, travel through the land wherever your fancy takes you. Observe the world. Then come back, and I will change your name for you."

So Lowly packed a small bundle and wandered from village to village. He walked for days, till he came to the bustling outskirts of a large city. There, he saw a dead body being carried to the cremation ground by grieving pallbearers. 

"What was that man called?" Lowly asked a bystander respectfully. 

"His name was 'Life', sir," the man replied. 

"What? Can a man named 'Life' be dead?" Lowly gasped in shock. 

The man stared at him. "Why not? Whether he was called 'Life' or 'Death', he would have had to die all the same! A name only serves to mark who's who! You seem to be a fool!"

Pondering over this strange matter, Lowly entered the city. Suddenly, he heard the sharp crack of a whip. A wealthy mistress was violently beating a poor slave girl in the street. 

"Mercy! Mercy, my mistress! I'll try and do better tomorrow," the slave girl sobbed on the ground. 

Moved by the terrible sight, kind Lowly immediately intervened. "Wait, my good woman! Why do you whip the poor girl?"

"She is my slave," the angry mistress snapped. "I sent her out to earn money, and she has come back empty-handed!"

Lowly quickly took out one of his few coins and gave it to the woman. "Here. Keep this and spare the girl. She'll do better tomorrow."

As Lowly walked away, he spoke to a passerby who had witnessed the cruel scene. "Poor girl! She must have an absolutely accursed name to suffer such a fate!"

"She is called 'Rich'!" the passerby laughed. 

"What! And with a name like that she could not even earn a day's paltry wages!" Lowly gasped in amazement. 

"You seem to be a fool!" the passerby shook his head. "A name only serves to mark who's who, and not what they actually are."

More reconciled to his own name, Lowly left the city and took the long road back towards Takshashila. 

"I am going to Takshashila, but I have completely lost my way," a traveller on the road told him. "Can you help me?"

"I am going there myself. You can come with me," Lowly smiled. "What is your name, friend?"

"I am called 'Guide'," the man replied. 

"'Guide'? And you've lost your way?" Lowly asked, his eyes widening. 

"Are you making fun of me?" the man frowned. "Whether one's name is 'Guide' or 'Misguide', one can lose one's way all the same! Everyone knows that names only serve to mark who's who."

Lowly smiled a bright, beaming smile. "Yes, I have at last come to understand that profound truth."

When he finally arrived at Takshashila, Lowly went directly to his teacher. 

"Well, do you still want to change your name?" the teacher asked knowingly. 

"Master, I find that death can come to a man named 'Life', that 'Rich' and 'Poor' may both be poor, and that a 'Guide' and a 'Misguide' can easily lose their way," Lowly bowed respectfully. 

"I know now that a name serves only to tell who is who, and does absolutely not govern the owner's destiny. I am quite content with my own name, and do not want to change it for another."
