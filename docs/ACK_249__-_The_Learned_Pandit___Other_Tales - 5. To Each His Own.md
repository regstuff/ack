# To Each His Own

One evening, after a long, exhausting day at the market, a fishwife finally sold her very last fish. 

On her way home, she stopped at her friend's house and knocked on the door. Her friend, who was a flower-girl by trade, answered the door with a bright smile. 

"Why, it's you! Come in, my friend," the flower-girl greeted her warmly. "You must have dinner with me and spend the night here. We can have a long chat."

"All right, my friend," the fishwife agreed happily. 

After a lovely dinner and catching up on all the town gossip, it was time to go to bed. 

"We'll sleep in the room where I keep my flowers," the flower-girl said, leading her into a room filled to the brim with thousands of fresh, blooming roses and jasmine. "You'll love it."

"Oh! It's a beautiful room," the fishwife smiled. 

The two women lay down to sleep. But hours later, the fishwife was tossing and turning, unable to close her eyes. 

"Why are you so restless? What's the matter?" the flower-girl asked, waking up. 

"I just can't sleep," the fishwife groaned miserably. "I think it's the smell of these flowers. It's too sweet. I can't bear it."

The flower-girl looked at her in surprise. 

"I know what!" the fishwife suddenly suggested. "May I have my fish-basket?"

"Certainly! I'll bring it in for you," her friend agreed. 

The flower-girl went out, fetched the empty, foul-smelling fish-basket, and brought it back into the room. 

The fishwife excitedly grabbed the basket and sprinkled it with some water to freshen it up. 

"Ugh! What a stench!" the flower-girl gagged, covering her nose. 

But the fishwife took a deep, happy breath, hugging the stinking basket close to her pillow. "Ah! This is much better," she sighed contentedly. "Now I shall sleep like a log."

And, right enough, within a few minutes, she was fast asleep and snoring loudly. 

The flower-girl watched her friend in complete disbelief. "Surprising! She prefers the stench of fish to the fragrance of flowers! Well, I suppose one feels most at home with what one is used to."
