# Ganesha and the Moon

Once on his birthday, Ganesha was invited to Chandralok, the abode of the Moon, for a feast. 

"Everything is splendid, but I like these modaks best," Ganesha declared, happily popping the sweet dumplings filled with coconut and jaggery into his mouth. He ate plate after plate of them. 

Ganesha ate all the modaks that were kept before him. Very soon, he leaned back, rubbing his large belly. "I have eaten too much. I feel so uncomfortable. I need to get some fresh air. I'll go for a ride on my mouse." 

It was a beautiful night. The moon shone so brightly, it was almost like day! Ganesha rode his mouse steadily along the path. 

Suddenly, a large snake slithered across the road. 

"SQUEAK!" the startled mouse cried, rearing up on its hind legs in terror. 

The sudden movement threw Ganesha off balance. "Oh no! All the modaks I ate have fallen out," he cried as he hit the ground with a heavy thud, his stomach bursting open and the modaks spilling out. 

"Pardon me, Ganesha. I did not mean to frighten your mouse," the snake hissed apologetically. 

Up in the sky, the Moon had watched the entire incident. The sight of the elephant-headed god tumbling off his tiny steed was too much for him. The Moon burst out laughing. "HA HA HA! You look so funny! What a fall that was!" 

Ganesha was very angry. But first, he put all the modaks back into his stomach. "...And used the snake to tie his stomach up," securing it like a belt. 

"HO HO HO!" the Moon continued to roar with laughter. The Moon just would not stop laughing. 

Furious at being mocked, Ganesha broke off a piece of his own tusk. "...And hurled it, furiously... slashing the Moon's luminous face." 

"O foolish Moon! Your unblemished appearance has made you vain! I command you to vanish from the universe!" Ganesha cursed him. 

Instantly, the Moon disappeared, and there was chaos in heaven and on earth. 

"Oh! I cannot see anything. What has happened to the Moon? It is so dark!" the people cried. 

Realizing the terrible consequences of his vanity, the Moon was sorry and came to Ganesha with the other gods. 

"I have learned my lesson. I will never mock you again. Take back your curse, O Lord! The world is plunged in darkness," the Moon pleaded. 

The ever-forgiving Ganesha thought for a while. Then he said, "You were wrong to laugh at my discomfort. I cannot take back my curse but I can soften it. From now on, you will not glow brightly every night." 

"You will wax and wane from one bright fortnight to one dark fortnight alternately," Ganesha declared. "You will be seen as a full moon, in all your splendour, only once a month. Also, you will be invisible for one night, every month." 

The Moon bowed before Ganesha... and went back to his place in the sky, destined to wax and wane forevermore.
