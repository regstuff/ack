# True Friends

Long, long ago, during the reign of King Magadha, a wedding was being celebrated in Rajagriha. It was a sumptuous feast with pomp and ceremony. The bride was demure and beautiful, and the groom was Shakha Kumar, the son of the richest merchant in town. 

On the same day that Shakha Kumar was born, two other boys were born in the city. One was born in a weaver's house and was named Pottik. The other, born under a Nigrodha tree, was named Nigrodha Kumar. 

As children, the three boys played together. When they were about eleven years old, they set out for Takshashila for their studies. "We must go to Takshashila for studies," they agreed. 

"You two can afford the two thousand-coin fee... but my poor father is just a weaver. I cannot go with you," Pottik said sadly. 

"Yes, you can," Nigrodha Kumar assured him. "I spoke to my father and he has agreed to pay for your studies too." 

They studied together at Takshashila and grew into fine young men. While roaming from one place to another after their studies, they reached the outskirts of Varanasi and camped near a grove of trees for the night. 

Early at dawn, Pottik woke up after hearing the cackle of a hen. "Why are you cackling so loudly over an egg?" he heard another hen ask. 

"Because this one is not an ordinary egg. The one who eats the yolk of it is destined to be a king... The one who eats the white of it will become commander of the army... And the one who eats the shell will become a treasurer. Well, isn't such an egg worth crowing about?" the first hen replied. 

"I must try to find out whether there is any truth in what the hen said," Pottik thought. When the hens moved away, he quietly picked up the egg. 

Carefully, he separated the parts of the egg and mixed them in the breakfast porridge. "Here's your porridge, Nigrodha. With the yolk," Pottik served his friend. "And this is yours, Shakha. The white is in this one." 

"But we haven't eaten any part of the egg," they protested. 

"Yes, you have. I separated the parts and served them to each of you. I'll eat the one with the shell," Pottik explained, telling them what the hens had said. 

"I gave the yolk to Nigrodha, the white to Shakha and I ate the shell myself," Pottik thought. Shakha was unhappy but kept silent. He pretended he was not hurt. "Who is he to decide? He should have given the yolk to me. I'll teach him a lesson one day," Shakha vowed silently. 

They went into the town of Varanasi. That same day, the childless king of Varanasi had died. According to tradition, a flower-decorated chariot was sent out with no charioteer. "The new king will be the one in front of whom the chariot stops," the people explained. 

The three young men rested in a park. Nigrodha Kumar slept on a stone slab. An excited mob followed the decorated chariot into the park. "Look! The horse wants to ascend those steps," the people marvelled as the chariot stopped right in front of the sleeping Nigrodha. 

"Ah! What a noble face this young man has," the priest noted. "He has the signs of an Emperor." 

Nigrodha Kumar was awakened and promptly installed as the King of Varanasi. He appointed Shakha Kumar as the Commander-in-Chief of his forces. "Pottik, you must stay with me as my constant companion," the new king told his weaver friend. 

One day, Shakha asked to go back to Rajagriha to fetch his parents so they could live in luxury in the palace. "Will you go, Pottik?" the king asked. "Of course I will," Pottik agreed. 

With a small retinue, Pottik and Shakha set off. But when they reached Rajagriha, Shakha showed his true colours. "Come with me to Varanasi," Pottik told Shakha's parents. 

"No, son! Our place is here. We cannot come with you," they declined. 

Pottik had spent all his money by then. "Here's Shakha's house. I'll rest here and refresh myself before going to the palace," he thought, knocking on his friend's door. 

"Sir, your friend Pottik is here," a servant told Shakha. 

"Had he given me the yolk, I would have become the king," Shakha thought bitterly. "Ah! Now's my chance to take revenge!" 

Shakha refused to see Pottik. "Who's Pottik? I don't know anyone by that name. Beat him and throw him out. He must be a madcap." 

Pottik was beaten and thrown out into the street. With a heavy heart, he returned to Varanasi. When he reached the palace, he told King Nigrodha everything. 

The king summoned his Commander-in-Chief. "Really. I stopped by at his house on my way here, but he said he didn't know me and had me beaten and thrown out by his guards," Pottik repeated. 

Shakha stood silently, unable to deny it. 

"A favour done to a deserving noble person is like a seed sown in fertile soil. It will reap rich rewards," the king said sternly. "Indeed, any favour done to an ungrateful man is like a seed burnt by fire. Nothing ever comes out of it." 

"O you wicked liar! Take him away! Kill him," the infuriated king ordered. 

But Pottik intervened. "No! Please don't, O King! It is not easy to bring anyone back to life... Therefore, I don't want him killed. Please forgive him." 

"If you insist. But I want him banished from my kingdom," the king declared. 

Nigrodha then offered Pottik the position of Commander-in-Chief. "But I must refuse. I have always known my limitations," Pottik replied humbly. 

"Why don't you accept the post of Bhandagarik (Treasurer) in my court? You will have the authority to determine the taxes payable by the various guilds. You will be in charge of the treasury." 

"As you wish. I accept the post," Pottik smiled, remembering the hen's prophecy. 

With his true friend Pottik by his side, Nigrodha Kumar ruled wisely and well for many years.
