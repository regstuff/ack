# The Stupid Crocodile and the Monkey

In a dense jungle, near a rushing river, lived a clever little monkey. Right in the middle of the river was a small, lush island where plenty of delicious fruit grew. Whenever the monkey felt hungry, he would go straight to the island. 

To cross the rushing water, the monkey had a perfect system. He would jump from the riverbank onto a large, smooth rock jutting out of the water, and from there, leap safely onto the island. 

"Breakfast, here I come!" he would cheer every morning.

Not far away, beneath the murky waters, lived a crocodile and his wife. The crocodile's wife had been watching the monkey jump back and forth every day. 

"You know dear, I've got it!" she told her husband one afternoon. "I know how we can catch that little monkey for our dinner."

The crocodile groaned. "Oh please! Not again. We've tried it too many times before. Let us be happy with the fish I catch."

"This time my plan won't fail. I promise," she insisted, swimming toward the monkey's favorite rock. "You see that rock there? The monkey uses it to reach the island. When he's on the island, busy eating his breakfast, you lie on that rock. When he has to return, he will think you're the rock. As soon as he jumps on you, kill him!"

The crocodile smiled. "Hmm! The plan sounds good."

The next day, as usual, the monkey jumped onto the rock and over to the island. While he was busy stuffing his cheeks with fruit, the massive crocodile quietly climbed onto the rock and waited.

When the monkey had eaten his fill, he walked to the edge of the island to jump back. He stopped. "Hey! That's strange! That rock looks bigger today," he observed closely. "The river has not gone dry for it to look bigger. I'm sure it is that stupid crocodile, up to his tricks again. I won't jump till I'm sure."

The clever monkey cupped his hands around his mouth. "Hello rock! How are you today, my friend?"

There was only silence.

The monkey sighed loudly. "Why aren't you speaking to me today, rock? Have I annoyed you? It looks as if this rock used to talk to him!"

Under the water, the stupid crocodile panicked. *I'd better reply,* he thought. Rising slightly, he croaked, "What is it, monkey?"

"You look rather big today, rock," the monkey giggled.

"I'm not a rock," the crocodile growled, realizing his cover was blown. "I'm a crocodile and I'm going to kill you for my dinner."

*I was right. It is him. Now I'm in real trouble,* the monkey thought, his mind racing. *Must think fast. Wait... I've got it. I've seen him eating many times before. Whenever he opens his mouth wide, his eyes always close.*

"I'll tell you what, crocodile," the monkey yelled out. "You open your mouth wide, and I'll jump right into it from here!"

The stupid crocodile was thrilled. "Good! This is going to save us both a lot of trouble."

"Okay, get ready now," the monkey said. "Open wide!"

The crocodile opened his massive, terrifying jaws as wide as he could. Just as the monkey had observed, the crocodile's eyes squeezed tightly shut. 

"Take that!" the monkey yelled. He leaped through the air, but instead of landing in the jaws, he stomped his feet down hard on the top of the crocodile's snout with a heavy *THUD*. Using the crocodile's head as a springboard, the monkey vaulted safely onto the riverbank.

The crocodile opened his eyes, dizzy from the blow. "What was that? What are you doing over there?" he sputtered. "You promised you would jump into my mouth!"

The monkey laughed from the safety of the trees. "Did you really believe I'd do a stupid thing like that?"

"Grr! Wait till I get my teeth into you," the crocodile thrashed angrily.

"You never will, crocodile," the monkey grinned. "You may be big and strong... but you are stupid!"
