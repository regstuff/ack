# Aruni

One cold winter day, Aruni of Panchala, a young and devoted disciple of Sage Dhoumya, was on his way back to the hermitage carrying a heavy bundle of firewood he had collected for the sage's household. As he passed by a field that belonged to his master, he noticed something alarming. 

"There's a breach in the embankment!" Aruni realized in dismay. "The master's field will dry up! If I wait here to try and stop it, I'll be delayed. And there's not a bit of firewood at the hermitage!" 

Torn between his duties, Aruni rushed back to the hermitage as fast as he could. 

Meanwhile, Sage Dhoumya and his disciples had assembled for the day's lessons. The sage looked around. "Where is Aruni?" he asked.

"He's gone to fetch firewood," one of the disciples replied. 

Just then, Aruni came running in, panting heavily under the heavy load he carried. 

"Ah! There you are!" the sage said. "Why are you late, Aruni?"

"There's a breach in the embankment," Aruni gasped. "The water is escaping..."

"Oh! Oh! Go and attend to it," the sage instructed urgently. "Quick! Leave the firewood here! Upamanyu or Veda will take it in. He's gone already!" 

As Veda and Upamanyu carried the firewood in, they marveled at his dedication. "How responsible and devoted Aruni is! A disciple like him is hard to find."

Running all the way back to the field, a breathless Aruni reached the trouble spot. He laid logs across the gap and packed it tightly with wet mud. "There! That should hold the water in," he thought, feeling relieved. 

But as Aruni turned to go, the rushing water broke through his makeshift dam. "Oh, no! It's given way! What shall I do? If I go back for help, the field will be drained dry!" 

As he stood gazing helplessly at the widening breach, a desperate idea occurred to him. For a moment, he wavered. "If I stay here, I will miss my studies for the day. But my Guru's field! I must save the crops!" Without hesitation, Aruni laid his own body across the breach, blocking the cold water with his flesh.

Meanwhile, engaged in his daily routine, the sage forgot about Aruni and the errand he had sent him on. It was only towards evening that he realized his absence. 

"The lamps are not yet lit!" Sage Dhoumya said. "It's not like Aruni to forget his duties. Where's Aruni?"

"You sent him to the field on an errand, Master," a disciple reminded him. "He has not yet returned."

"Not yet returned? I hope no mishap has befallen him. Come, let us go to the field and see."

When the anxious sage and his disciples reached the field, it was dark and quiet. "He's not here! Where could he have gone?" the sage wondered aloud. 

Suddenly, a feeble voice called out from the mud and freezing water. "I am here... Master! Ar-u-u-ni!"

Tears filled his eyes as the sage bent down to lift up his freezing disciple. "He's been here all day! He stopped the gap with his own body! Bless the child! My child, are you all right?"

"Aruni! You're cold and stiff!" the other disciples cried.

"The water, Master! It's flowing out! The crops..." Aruni shivered, still worried about his duty.

"Never mind, my son," the sage said gently. 

"Don't worry, Aruni. We'll take care of it," his friends assured him.

Later, when the breach had been properly attended to and all had returned to the warmth of the hermitage, Sage Dhoumya blessed the boy. "My son, your devotion and obedience to your Guru are unmatched. They shall forever bring you renown."
